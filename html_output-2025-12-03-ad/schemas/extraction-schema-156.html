Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-156

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of representational formats of conceptual knowledge at a functional (not neural) level, including descriptions of the format, supporting or challenging evidence, cognitive tasks or phenomena used as evidence, comparisons between formats, and theoretical claims or implications.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
representational_format_name str The name of the representational format of conceptual knowledge discussed (e.g., 'prototype theory', 'exemplar model', 'semantic network', 'symbolic representation', 'distributed representation', 'theory-based representation', etc.).
representational_format_description str A concise description of the representational format, including how it is proposed to represent conceptual knowledge at a functional level.
format_type str The type or category of the representational format (e.g., symbolic, distributed, hybrid, feature-based, theory-based, etc.).
cognitive_task_or_phenomenon str The cognitive task(s) or psychological phenomena used as evidence for or against the representational format (e.g., categorization, semantic priming, property verification, typicality effects, etc.).
key_findings str A brief summary of the key findings or results that support or challenge the representational format, including any relevant quantitative or qualitative results.
comparison_with_other_formats str Any direct comparisons, contrasts, or evaluations between this representational format and other formats, including which format is favored by the evidence (if any).
limitations_or_counter_evidence str Any reported limitations, challenges, or counter-evidence to the representational format, including tasks or phenomena it fails to explain.
theoretical_claims_or_implications str Any theoretical claims or implications made about the functional role of the representational format in conceptual knowledge, such as how it supports reasoning, generalization, or other cognitive functions.
