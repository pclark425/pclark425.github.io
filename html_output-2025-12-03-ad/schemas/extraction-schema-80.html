Extraction Schema Details for extraction-schema-80

Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-80

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of functional-level theories or models of how conceptual knowledge is represented in the brain, including descriptions of the representational format, supporting evidence, counter-evidence, and comparisons between theories.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
theory_name str The name of the representational format or theory (e.g., 'prototype theory', 'exemplar theory', 'symbolic representation', 'distributed representation', 'theory-based representation').
theory_description str A concise description of the theory or representational format, focusing on how conceptual knowledge is functionally represented (e.g., 'concepts are represented as averages of experienced instances', 'concepts are represented as sets of stored exemplars', etc.).
level_of_analysis str The level of analysis at which the theory operates (e.g., 'functional', 'computational', 'algorithmic'). Should be 'functional' for relevant entries.
supporting_evidence str A brief summary of the key empirical findings, cognitive phenomena, or tasks that support this theory (e.g., 'typicality effects in categorization', 'generalization patterns', 'reaction time data', etc.).
counter_evidence_or_challenges str Any reported counter-evidence, limitations, or challenges to the theory (e.g., 'fails to account for context effects', 'cannot explain rapid concept learning', etc.).
comparison_to_other_theories str Any direct comparisons made between this theory and other representational theories (e.g., 'prototype theory outperforms exemplar theory on X task', 'symbolic and distributed representations are contrasted in Y experiment').
notable_limitations_or_open_questions str Any notable limitations or open questions highlighted in the paper regarding the theory or representational format.
