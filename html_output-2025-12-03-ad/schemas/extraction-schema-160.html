Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-160

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of faster-than-light (FTL) spacecraft travel concepts, their physical principles, required technologies, cost estimates, engineering challenges, and assessments of their viability with current or near-term resources.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
ftl_concept_name str The name of the faster-than-light (FTL) travel concept or approach discussed (e.g., Alcubierre warp drive, traversable wormhole, quantum tunneling, etc.).
ftl_concept_description str A brief description of the FTL concept, including how it is theorized to work.
physical_principle str The underlying physical principle(s) or theory supporting the FTL concept (e.g., general relativity, quantum mechanics, exotic matter, negative energy, etc.).
required_technology str A list or description of the key technologies, materials, or components required to realize the FTL concept.
technology_readiness_level str The current technology readiness level (TRL) or maturity of the required technology (e.g., theoretical only, lab demonstration, prototype, etc.).
estimated_cost str Any estimates of the cost or budget required to develop or build the FTL spacecraft, including units and context (e.g., USD, per mission, per year, etc.).
engineering_challenges str A summary of the main engineering or physical challenges identified for this FTL concept (e.g., energy requirements, stability, material limitations, etc.).
proposed_solutions str Any proposed solutions or mitigations for the identified challenges.
experimental_or_simulation_results str Any reported experimental results, simulations, or practical demonstrations relevant to the FTL concept (null if none).
viability_assessment str The authors' assessment of the near-term viability of the FTL concept, especially with respect to current or near-term technology and budgets.
counterarguments_or_critiques str Any counterarguments, critiques, or reasons why the FTL concept may not be viable today, as discussed in the paper.
