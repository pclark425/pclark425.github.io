Extraction Schema Details for extraction-schema-52

Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-52

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of training machine learning models or RL agents using circuit simulators (including analog, digital, RF, mixed-signal, or power circuits), with particular focus on simulator fidelity levels, sim-to-real transfer experiments, and comparisons of different simulation approaches.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
circuit_type str What type of circuit is being studied? (e.g. 'analog amplifier', 'digital logic', 'RF filter', 'power converter', 'mixed-signal ADC', 'power distribution system', etc.)
simulator_used str What circuit simulator is used for training? (e.g. 'SPICE', 'LTspice', 'Cadence Spectre', 'MATLAB/Simulink', 'custom simulator', 'Ngspice', 'HSPICE', etc.)
simulator_fidelity_details str What level of fidelity does the simulator have? Be specific about what is modeled: parasitic capacitances, resistances, inductances, nonlinearities, thermal effects, noise, process variations, aging, electromagnetic effects, etc. Also note what is simplified or omitted.
component_models str What component models are used? (e.g. 'ideal components', 'BSIM transistor models', 'behavioral models', 'SPICE models with parasitics', 'datasheet-based models', etc.)
ml_model_type str What type of ML model or RL agent is being trained? (e.g. 'neural network', 'PPO agent', 'DQN', 'policy gradient', 'transformer', 'GNN', etc.)
task_description str What is the specific task? (e.g. 'circuit sizing optimization', 'fault diagnosis', 'voltage control', 'parameter tuning', 'topology selection', 'power management', etc.)
sim_to_real_transfer bool Does the paper report transferring from simulation to real physical circuits/hardware? (true, false, or null)
real_hardware_details str If sim-to-real transfer is performed, describe the real hardware used (e.g. 'fabricated CMOS chip', 'breadboard prototype', 'PCB implementation', 'FPGA', 'power grid testbed', etc.)
simulation_performance str What performance does the trained model achieve in simulation? Include metrics with units (e.g. 'reward of 0.95', '98% accuracy', '5% error', etc.)
real_world_performance str What performance does the model achieve on real hardware after transfer? Include metrics with units and comparison to simulation performance if available.
fidelity_comparison bool Does the paper compare different simulator fidelity levels (e.g. ideal vs detailed models, with/without parasitics, different time steps, etc.)? (true, false, or null)
fidelity_comparison_results str If different fidelity levels are compared, what are the key findings? Which level of fidelity was necessary for successful transfer? What features could be simplified without hurting performance?
domain_randomization bool Does the paper use domain randomization of circuit parameters (component tolerances, process variations, temperature, etc.)? (true, false, or null)
randomization_details str If domain randomization is used, what parameters are randomized and by how much? (e.g. 'resistor values ±10%', 'temperature 0-70°C', 'process corners', etc.)
physics_informed_approach bool Does the approach incorporate circuit physics laws (KVL, KCL, Ohm's law, etc.) into the learning process? (true, false, or null)
physics_constraints_used str If physics-informed, what specific circuit laws or constraints are incorporated? (e.g. 'KVL/KCL in loss function', 'power conservation constraint', 'stability constraints', etc.)
transfer_failure_cases str Are there any reported failures where the model trained in simulation failed to transfer to real hardware? If so, what was the cause? (e.g. 'unmodeled parasitics', 'thermal drift', 'noise sensitivity', etc.)
minimal_fidelity_claims str Does the paper make any explicit claims about what minimum simulator fidelity is required for successful transfer? Quote or summarize any such claims.
ablation_studies str Are there ablation studies showing the impact of including/excluding specific simulator features (parasitics, nonlinearities, noise, etc.) on transfer performance? Summarize key results.
