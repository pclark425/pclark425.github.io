Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-137

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of faster-than-light propulsion concepts, their theoretical foundations, required exotic resources, energy and mass estimates, engineering challenges, experimental evidence, cost and budget considerations, feasibility assessments, and any noted counterarguments.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
concept_name str The name of the faster-than-light concept (e.g., Alcubierre warp drive, Krasnikov tube, traversable wormhole, quantum tunneling propulsion).
concept_description str A brief description of how the concept is supposed to enable superluminal travel.
theoretical_framework str The underlying physics theory or model supporting the concept (e.g., General Relativity, quantum field theory, semiclassical gravity).
required_exotic_matter str Type of exotic matter or negative energy needed (e.g., Casimir vacuum, scalar field with negative energy density).
exotic_matter_quantity str Quantitative estimate of the exotic matter required (e.g., kg, energy density J/m^3, total negative energy).
energy_requirement str Total energy needed to create and sustain the FTL effect (include units such as joules, megatons of TNT, or equivalent mass).
spacecraft_mass_estimate str Estimated mass of the spacecraft (including payload) considered in the analysis.
propulsion_mechanism str Specific mechanism by which the concept achieves superluminal motion (e.g., spacetime metric contraction, wormhole throat expansion).
engineering_challenges str Key engineering obstacles identified (e.g., material strength, stability of exotic matter, control of spacetime geometry). List multiple challenges separated by semicolons.
experimental_evidence str Any laboratory‑scale experiments, analogs, or simulations that provide supporting evidence for the concept.
cost_estimate str Estimated monetary budget required for development and deployment (include currency, magnitude, and time horizon).
feasibility_assessment str Authors' overall assessment of near‑term viability (e.g., "currently infeasible", "requires breakthrough in negative energy generation", "potentially realizable within 30 years").
assumptions str Critical assumptions made in the analysis (e.g., existence of stable negative energy, no violation of causality).
counterarguments str Noted theoretical or experimental objections, no‑go theorems, or physical constraints that challenge the concept.
