Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-124

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of neuronal populations in the visual cortex that are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and/or tau protein tangles in dementia, including the identity of the neuronal population, the type of pathology, evidence for vulnerability or resistance, proposed mechanisms or factors underlying this selectivity, disease context, and species or model studied.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
neuronal_population str The specific neuronal population in the visual cortex being discussed (e.g. 'layer 2/3 pyramidal neurons', 'parvalbumin interneurons', 'V1 neurons', 'calbindin-positive cells').
vulnerability_status str Whether the neuronal population is described as vulnerable, resistant, or intermediate to amyloid-β plaques and/or tau tangles (e.g. 'vulnerable', 'resistant', 'intermediate', 'no information').
pathology_type str The type of pathology affecting the neuronal population (e.g. 'amyloid-β', 'tau', 'both', 'other', 'no information').
evidence_for_status str A concise summary of the experimental or observational evidence supporting the vulnerability or resistance status (e.g. 'increased tau tangle density in layer 5 neurons', 'no amyloid-β detected in calretinin interneurons', 'cell loss observed', 'no pathology observed').
proposed_mechanisms str Any proposed mechanisms or factors underlying the selective vulnerability or resistance (e.g. 'differential expression of tau isoforms', 'synaptic connectivity patterns', 'metabolic differences', 'gene expression profiles', 'calcium buffering capacity').
disease_context str The disease context in which the findings were observed (e.g. 'Alzheimer's disease', 'frontotemporal dementia', 'mouse model of tauopathy').
species_or_model str The species or experimental model used in the study (e.g. 'human', 'mouse', 'rat', 'transgenic mouse model', 'postmortem human tissue').
counterexamples_or_contradictions str Any reported counterexamples, contradictory findings, or exceptions to the general pattern of vulnerability or resistance (e.g. 'some studies report resistance in layer 4 neurons, while others report vulnerability').
