Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2384

Name: Network-Connectivity-Driven Selective Vulnerability Theory: Global Network Topology

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This general theory posits that the selective vulnerability or resistance of neuronal populations in the visual cortex to amyloid-β (Aβ) plaques and tau tangles is determined by their position and role within the cortical network topology. Neurons with high centrality, extensive long-range connectivity, and integrative hub-like properties are more susceptible to the initiation and propagation of pathological protein aggregates, while neurons with low centrality and predominantly local connectivity are more resistant.

THEN
• neuronal_population → is_selectively_vulnerable_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neuronal populations with high betweenness and degree centrality are early sites of tau and Aβ pathology in Alzheimer's disease.
  2. Network analysis of cortical connectomes shows that hub regions are more likely to accumulate pathology.
  3. Disruption of network hubs correlates with cognitive decline in dementia.

Statement 1: Local Connectivity and Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_low_network_centrality → True, and
• neuronal_population → has_predominantly_local_connections → True
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_selectively_resistant_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neuronal populations with low centrality and local connectivity are less likely to accumulate Aβ and tau pathology.
  2. Inhibitory interneurons and local circuit neurons are relatively spared in early Alzheimer's pathology.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Somewhat related to existing work, but novel in its explicit, network-based conditional formulation.

What Already Exists: Relative resistance of local circuit neurons and interneurons is observed in neuropathological studies.

What is Novel: This law links resistance explicitly to network topology metrics.

References:

  • Roussarie (2020) Selective Neuronal Vulnerability in Alzheimer's Disease: A Network-Based Analysis [Interneuron resistance]
  • de Haan (2012) Disrupted modular brain dynamics reflect cognitive dysfunction in Alzheimer's disease [Network topology]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Neuronal populations with the highest network centrality in the visual cortex will show the earliest and most severe Aβ and tau pathology in both human and animal models.
  2. Interventions that reduce the centrality or long-range connectivity of hub neurons will decrease their vulnerability to pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing the network centrality of a normally resistant neuronal population will render it more vulnerable to Aβ and tau pathology.
  2. Altering the global network topology (e.g., via developmental manipulation) will shift the pattern of selective vulnerability in the visual cortex.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding that neurons with high centrality and long-range connections are resistant to pathology would challenge the theory.
  2. Observing that local circuit neurons with low centrality are among the earliest to accumulate pathology would contradict the theory.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Intrinsic molecular or genetic factors that confer vulnerability or resistance independent of network topology are not addressed.
  2. The role of glial cells and non-neuronal elements in modulating vulnerability is not considered.
