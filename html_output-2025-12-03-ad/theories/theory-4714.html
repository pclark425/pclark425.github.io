ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → exposed_to → air pollution (PM2.5, ultrafine particles, NOx, etc.)
THEN
• systemic circulation → increases → inflammatory cytokines and oxidative stress markers

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Air pollution exposure is associated with elevated blood cytokines and oxidative stress in humans and animals.
  2. Systemic inflammation is a known risk factor for neurodegeneration.

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

What is Novel: Its centrality as a trigger for epigenetic and AD pathology is newly emphasized.

Statement 1: Inflammation-Induced Epigenetic Changes Amplify Amyloid/Tau Pathology (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• systemic inflammation → induces → epigenetic modifications in brain and immune cells
THEN
• brain → increases → amyloid-beta and tau pathology, and
• neuroinflammation → is_amplified_by → epigenetic changes

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Inflammatory cytokines can induce DNA methylation and histone changes in neural tissue.
  2. Epigenetic changes in microglia and astrocytes are linked to increased amyloid and tau pathology.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law proposes a novel feed-forward loop integrating inflammation, epigenetics, and AD pathology.

What Already Exists: Inflammation and epigenetic changes are each linked to AD.

What is Novel: The amplification loop between inflammation, epigenetics, and pathology is new.

References:

  • Heneka et al. (2015) Neuroinflammation in Alzheimer's disease [inflammation, AD]
  • Patrick et al. (2017) Air pollution, DNA methylation, and neurodegeneration [pollution, epigenetics, neurodegeneration]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Anti-inflammatory interventions in pollution-exposed individuals will reduce epigenetic changes and slow AD progression.
  2. Peripheral blood epigenetic markers will correlate with both systemic inflammation and AD pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Blocking the inflammatory-epigenetic amplification loop will halt or reverse AD pathology in animal models.
  2. Individuals with genetic resistance to inflammation-induced epigenetic change will be protected from pollution-related AD.

Negative Experiments

  1. If anti-inflammatory treatments do not reduce epigenetic changes or AD pathology, the amplification loop is questioned.
  2. If systemic inflammation does not precede epigenetic changes in pollution-exposed individuals, the causal order is challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. AD cases with minimal systemic inflammation or epigenetic change despite high pollution exposure.
