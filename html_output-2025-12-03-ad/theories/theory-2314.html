Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2314

Name: Proteostatic Failure Cascade Theory of Alzheimer's Disease

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the causes of Alzheimer's disease and effective detection methods.

Description: This theory proposes that Alzheimer's disease is fundamentally a disorder of proteostasis, where age-related decline in protein quality control systems (e.g., autophagy, ubiquitin-proteasome system) leads to the accumulation of misfolded proteins (amyloid-beta, tau). This proteostatic failure initiates a cascade of cellular stress responses, synaptic dysfunction, and neurodegeneration. Early detection is possible by identifying markers of proteostatic stress before overt protein aggregation.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostatic Failure Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal proteostasis systems → are_impaired → age or genetic risk
THEN
• misfolded proteins → accumulate → in neurons, and
• cellular stress responses → are_activated → in neurons

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Aging and genetic risk factors (e.g., APOE4) impair autophagy and proteasome function.
  2. Impaired proteostasis precedes amyloid and tau aggregation in models.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law reframes AD as a primary proteostasis disorder.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis impairment is recognized in neurodegeneration.

What is Novel: The central, causal role of proteostatic failure as the initiating event in AD is novel.

References:

  • Ciechanover & Kwon (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin-proteasome system in neurodegeneration [proteostasis in AD]
  • Nixon (2013) The role of autophagy in neurodegenerative disease [autophagy impairment in AD]

Statement 1: Proteostatic Biomarker Detection Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → elevated markers of proteostatic stress (e.g., ubiquitinated proteins, autophagy markers) in CSF or blood
THEN
• individual → is_at_high_risk_for → developing AD before clinical symptoms

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. CSF and blood markers of proteostatic stress are elevated in preclinical AD.
  2. Proteostatic stress markers predict conversion from mild cognitive impairment to AD.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law proposes a new class of early detection biomarkers.

What Already Exists: Some proteostatic markers are studied in AD.

What is Novel: The use of proteostatic stress markers for preclinical detection is novel.

References:

  • Ciechanover & Kwon (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin-proteasome system in neurodegeneration [proteostasis in AD]
  • Nixon (2013) The role of autophagy in neurodegenerative disease [autophagy impairment in AD]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with high proteostatic stress markers but no amyloid/tau pathology will develop AD at higher rates.
  2. Therapies that enhance proteostasis (e.g., autophagy inducers) will delay or prevent AD onset.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Proteostatic stress markers will identify at-risk individuals decades before clinical symptoms.
  2. Some individuals with proteostatic failure may not develop AD if compensatory mechanisms are strong.

Negative Experiments

  1. If proteostatic stress markers do not precede amyloid/tau pathology, the theory is challenged.
  2. If enhancing proteostasis does not affect AD risk, the theory is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases of AD with normal proteostasis markers.
