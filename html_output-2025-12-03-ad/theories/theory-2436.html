Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2436

Name: Intrinsic Proteostatic Capacity Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortex neuronal populations to amyloid-β and tau pathology is determined by their intrinsic proteostatic capacity—i.e., the efficiency of their protein quality control systems, including autophagy, ubiquitin-proteasome activity, and chaperone expression. Neurons with robust proteostatic mechanisms are resistant to toxic protein accumulation, while those with limited capacity are more susceptible, regardless of their connectivity or metabolic demand.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostatic Capacity Determines Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_proteostatic_capacity → True
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with high expression of molecular chaperones and autophagy-related genes show reduced protein aggregation in AD models.
  2. Proteasome impairment increases vulnerability to tau and Aβ accumulation.
  3. Visual cortex neurons with robust proteostatic gene signatures are less affected in postmortem AD tissue.

What is Novel: Directly linking intrinsic proteostatic capacity to selective resistance in visual cortex subpopulations is novel.

Statement 1: Proteostatic Failure Increases Pathology (qualitative)

ConditionDetails

• neuronal_population → has_low_proteostatic_capacity → True, and

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  2. Visual cortex neurons with low proteostatic gene expression show early and severe pathology in AD.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Enhancing proteostatic mechanisms (e.g., upregulating autophagy or chaperones) in vulnerable visual cortex neurons will reduce Aβ and tau accumulation.
  2. Visual cortex neurons with naturally high proteostatic gene expression will show delayed or reduced pathology in AD models.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Transplanting high-proteostatic-capacity neurons into a vulnerable region will confer local resistance to pathology.
  2. Pharmacological enhancement of proteostasis in resistant neurons will have minimal effect on pathology progression.

Negative Experiments

  1. If neurons with high proteostatic capacity still accumulate Aβ and tau at the same rate as low-capacity neurons, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If proteostatic enhancement does not reduce pathology in vulnerable populations, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some neurons with robust proteostatic signatures are affected in late-stage AD, suggesting additional factors contribute to vulnerability.
