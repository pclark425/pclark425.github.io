Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4762

Name: Proteostasis-Immune Axis Gradient Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that a gradient of proteostasis capacity and immune gene expression exists across neuronal populations in the visual cortex, and that the position of a neuron along this axis determines its vulnerability or resistance to Aβ and tau pathology. Neurons at the low-proteostasis, high-pro-inflammatory end are most vulnerable, while those at the high-proteostasis, low-pro-inflammatory end are most resistant.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostasis-Immune Gradient Predicts Vulnerability (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → has_proteostasis_score → P, and
• neuron → has_immune_score → I, and
• P → is_low → relative to population mean, and
• I → is_high → relative to population mean
THEN
• neuron → is_highly_vulnerable_to → Aβ and tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Single-cell analyses reveal continuous variation in proteostasis and immune gene expression across neuronal subtypes, correlating with observed patterns of pathology.
  2. Regions with a higher proportion of neurons at the low-proteostasis, high-immune end show earlier and more severe pathology.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law formalizes a continuous, quantitative relationship between intrinsic properties and vulnerability, which is a new conceptual advance.

What Already Exists: Gradients of gene expression are observed, but their integration as a predictive axis for vulnerability is new.

What is Novel: The quantitative, axis-based model for predicting vulnerability is novel.

References:

  • Mathys et al. (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [Gene expression gradients]
  • Roussarie et al. (2020) Selective neuronal vulnerability in Alzheimer's disease: a network-based analysis [Population-level vulnerability]

Statement 1: Shifting Proteostasis-Immune Axis Alters Vulnerability (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → is_manipulated_to_increase → proteostasis gene expression, and
• neuron → is_manipulated_to_decrease → pro-inflammatory gene expression
THEN
• neuron → becomes_more_resistant_to → Aβ and tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Experimental upregulation of proteostasis genes or downregulation of pro-inflammatory genes in neurons reduces pathology in animal models.
  2. Pharmacological interventions targeting these pathways confer neuroprotection in preclinical studies.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law extends known intervention effects into a new, quantitative, cell-intrinsic framework.

What Already Exists: Interventions targeting proteostasis or inflammation are known to be neuroprotective, but the axis-based, cell-intrinsic model is new.

What is Novel: The prediction that shifting a neuron's position along the proteostasis-immune axis alters its vulnerability is novel.

References:

  • Cohen et al. (2013) The role of proteostasis in neurodegenerative disease [Proteostasis interventions]
  • Henstridge et al. (2019) The role of immune cells in neurodegeneration [Neuroinflammation interventions]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Mapping the proteostasis-immune axis in human visual cortex will predict regions and populations most susceptible to pathology.
  2. Interventions that shift neurons toward higher proteostasis and lower pro-inflammatory gene expression will reduce pathology in a dose-dependent manner.
  3. Neuronal populations with intermediate axis scores will show intermediate levels of vulnerability.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially creating a bimodal distribution along the axis (e.g., by genetic engineering) will result in sharply segregated vulnerable and resistant populations.
  2. Longitudinal single-cell profiling will show that neurons shift along the axis prior to developing pathology.
  3. Axis position will predict not only vulnerability to Aβ and tau, but also to other proteinopathies.

Negative Experiments

  1. If axis position does not correlate with vulnerability in independent datasets, the theory is challenged.
  2. If shifting axis position experimentally does not alter vulnerability, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The influence of extrinsic factors (e.g., glial cells, vascular changes) on axis position and vulnerability is not addressed.
