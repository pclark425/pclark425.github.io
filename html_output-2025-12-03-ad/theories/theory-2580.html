ConditionDetails

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Baseline plasma IL‑12p70 and IFN‑γ were associated with faster longitudinal tau PET progression in amyloid‑positive participants (e7930.0). [e7930.0]

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Provides a directional, quantitative link from peripheral inflammation to central tau pathology.

What Already Exists: Neuroinflammation is known to accompany AD pathology; CSF inflammatory markers correlate with disease severity.

What is Novel: Specific peripheral cytokines (IL‑12p70, IFN‑γ) predict the rate of tau accumulation measured by PET.

References:

  • Zeng et al. (2024) Multi‑analyte proteomic analysis identifies blood‑based neuroinflammation, cerebrovascular and synaptic biomarkers

Statement 1: Elevated plasma CHI3L1 (YKL‑40) and CHIT1 indicate tau‑dependent neurodegeneration (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• plasma CHI3L1 concentration → exceeds → threshold Y1, and
• plasma CHIT1 concentration → exceeds → threshold C1
THEN
• individual → shows → accelerated neurodegeneration (higher NfL, greater atrophy)

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. CHI3L1 and CHIT1 showed tau‑dependent longitudinal changes (p=0.003) and were associated with neurodegeneration markers (e7930.0). [e7930.0]

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Extends known CSF findings to plasma with longitudinal predictive relevance.

What Already Exists: YKL‑40 and CHIT1 are established microglial/astrocytic activation markers in CSF.

What is Novel: Demonstration that plasma levels of these proteins track tau‑dependent neurodegeneration longitudinally.

References:

  • Zeng et al. (2024) Multi‑analyte proteomic analysis identifies blood‑based neuroinflammation, cerebrovascular and synaptic biomarkers

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. In a cohort of cognitively normal adults, individuals with plasma IL‑12p70 and IFN‑γ above the identified thresholds will exhibit a >30% faster increase in tau PET SUVR over 3 years compared to low‑inflammation peers.
  2. Therapeutic anti‑inflammatory treatment (e.g., NSAIDs) reducing plasma IL‑12p70 by 20% will correspondingly slow tau PET progression by ~15%.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If peripheral cytokine blockade (e.g., anti‑IL‑12 antibodies) is initiated in preclinical AD, it may prevent conversion to amyloid positivity, a hypothesis with uncertain outcome.
  2. In individuals with chronic systemic infections, elevated cytokines may mimic AD‑related tau acceleration, potentially leading to false‑positive tau PET predictions.

Negative Experiments

  1. If plasma IL‑12p70 and IFN‑γ levels do not correlate with tau PET progression after adjusting for age, APOE4, and baseline amyloid, the proposed causal link is invalid.
  2. If CHI3L1 and CHIT1 levels are not associated with neurodegeneration markers in an independent cohort, their utility as plasma tau‑dependent markers is refuted.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. APOE4 carriers have lower soluble plasma Aβ42 and Aβ42/40 ratio, which may confound inflammation‑related predictions (e7957.2). [e7957.2]
