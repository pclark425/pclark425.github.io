Theory Statements

Statement 0: Homeostatic Loop Disruption Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• miRNA-lipid-amyloid/tau loop → is_disrupted_by → chronic dyslipidemia or metabolic stress
THEN
• feedback regulation → is_lost → in miRNA-lipid-amyloid/tau loop, and
• amyloid/tau accumulation → is_exacerbated_by → lipid and miRNA dysregulation, and
• neurodegeneration → is_promoted_by → loop breakdown

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. miRNAs regulate both lipid metabolism and amyloid precursor protein processing.
  2. Dyslipidemia is associated with altered miRNA profiles and increased amyloid/tau pathology.
  3. Loss of homeostatic feedback in metabolic and inflammatory pathways is a hallmark of AD.
  4. Feed-forward cycles of dysregulation are observed in AD brain tissue.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: While the components are known, the systems-level feedback loop and its breakdown as a unifying mechanism is novel.

What Already Exists: miRNA, lipid, and amyloid/tau dysregulation are each linked to AD.

What is Novel: The explicit homeostatic loop and its breakdown as a central driver of AD is newly formalized.

Statement 1: Feed-Forward Pathology Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• amyloid/tau accumulation → occurs → in brain, and
• lipid metabolism → is_dysregulated → in brain
THEN
• miRNA expression → is_further_altered → in brain, and
• loop dysregulation → is_amplified → over time

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Amyloid and tau pathology can alter lipid metabolism and miRNA expression, creating a vicious cycle.
  2. Longitudinal studies show progressive worsening of miRNA and lipid profiles with AD progression.
  3. Interventions that break the cycle (e.g., lipid-lowering drugs, miRNA modulators) can slow pathology in models.

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The general concept of feed-forward pathology is known, but the specific molecular loop is novel.

What is Novel: The specific miRNA-lipid-amyloid/tau loop as a self-amplifying driver is newly articulated.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Early intervention to restore miRNA or lipid homeostasis will prevent or delay amyloid/tau accumulation and cognitive decline.
  2. Longitudinal monitoring of miRNA and lipid profiles will predict AD onset before clinical symptoms.
  3. Pharmacological or dietary interventions that restore loop feedback will slow or halt AD progression.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially restoring loop feedback in advanced AD will reverse established pathology.
  2. Unique miRNA-lipid-amyloid/tau loop signatures will distinguish AD from other dementias.
  3. Non-invasive detection of loop breakdown (e.g., via blood miRNA/lipid biomarkers) will enable preclinical AD diagnosis.

Negative Experiments

  1. If restoring miRNA or lipid homeostasis does not affect amyloid/tau pathology, the loop model is challenged.
  2. If feed-forward amplification is not observed in longitudinal studies, the theory is weakened.
  3. If loop breakdown is not specific to AD but occurs in other neurodegenerative diseases without amyloid/tau pathology, the model's specificity is questioned.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases of AD without clear dyslipidemia or miRNA changes are not explained.
  2. Familial AD due to APP/PSEN mutations may bypass the loop.
