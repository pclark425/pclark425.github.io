Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4782

Name: Intrinsic Molecular Signature Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory posits that the selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortical neuronal populations to amyloid-β and tau pathology is determined by their intrinsic molecular and genetic profiles. Neurons expressing higher levels of protective genes (e.g., those involved in proteostasis, antioxidant defense, or synaptic stability) are resistant, while those with higher expression of susceptibility genes (e.g., those promoting amyloid precursor protein processing, tau phosphorylation, or pro-apoptotic pathways) are vulnerable.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostatic Defense Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_expression_of → proteostasis_and_antioxidant_genes
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Single-cell transcriptomics reveals that resistant neurons express higher levels of chaperones, proteasome subunits, and antioxidant enzymes.
  2. Experimental upregulation of proteostasis genes in vulnerable neurons confers resistance to amyloid and tau toxicity.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

What is Novel: The law applies this at the level of population-specific gene expression within the visual cortex.

References:

  • De Strooper (2016) The cellular phase of Alzheimer's disease [proteostasis and cell-intrinsic defense]

Statement 1: Susceptibility Gene Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_expression_of → amyloidogenic_and_tau-phosphorylating_genes
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_vulnerable_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Vulnerable neurons show higher expression of APP, BACE1, and kinases involved in tau phosphorylation.
  2. Genetic manipulation to reduce expression of these genes in vulnerable populations reduces pathology.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The molecular mechanisms are known, but their role in population-selective vulnerability is a novel application.

What Already Exists: APP processing and tau phosphorylation are established contributors to pathology.

What is Novel: The law links selective vulnerability to population-specific expression of these genes in the visual cortex.

References:

  • Roussarie (2020) Selective vulnerability in neurodegenerative diseases: from stressor thresholds to degeneration [molecular signatures and selective vulnerability]
  • Mathys (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [cell-type specific gene expression and vulnerability]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Overexpressing proteostasis genes in a vulnerable visual cortical population will confer resistance to amyloid-β and tau pathology.
  2. Knocking down APP or tau kinases in a vulnerable population will reduce local pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Introducing a synthetic gene circuit that mimics the resistant population's molecular profile into a vulnerable population will confer resistance.
  2. CRISPR-based editing of susceptibility gene enhancers in vivo will shift vulnerability patterns in the visual cortex.

Negative Experiments

  1. If populations with high proteostasis gene expression are still highly vulnerable, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If reducing susceptibility gene expression does not reduce vulnerability, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some populations with similar molecular profiles show different vulnerability, suggesting extrinsic factors also play a role.
