Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4823

Name: Intrinsic Molecular Signature Theory of Neuronal Vulnerability: Synaptic Activity and Calcium Homeostasis Axis

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability of visual cortex neurons to Aβ and tau pathology is governed by their intrinsic molecular signature related to synaptic activity and calcium homeostasis. Neurons with high baseline synaptic activity and low expression of calcium-buffering proteins are more susceptible to proteopathic stress due to increased calcium influx and excitotoxicity.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Synaptic Activity Vulnerability Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_baseline → synaptic_activity, and
• neuronal_population → has_low_expression_of → calcium_buffering_proteins
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_highly_vulnerable_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with high synaptic activity are more prone to Aβ-induced calcium dysregulation and excitotoxicity.
  2. Low expression of calcium-buffering proteins (e.g., parvalbumin) correlates with increased vulnerability to neurodegeneration.
  3. Experimental reduction of synaptic activity or enhancement of calcium buffering reduces Aβ/tau toxicity.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

What Already Exists: Synaptic activity and calcium dysregulation are implicated in AD pathogenesis.

What is Novel: The explicit link between intrinsic synaptic/calcium molecular signature and selective vulnerability in the visual cortex is novel.

References:

  • Busche & Konnerth (2016) Impairments of neural circuit function in Alzheimer's disease [Synaptic dysfunction in AD]
  • Mattson (2007) Calcium and neurodegeneration [Calcium dysregulation in neurodegeneration]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Visual cortex neurons with high synaptic activity and low calcium-buffering protein expression will show early Aβ/tau pathology.
  2. Pharmacological enhancement of calcium buffering will protect vulnerable neurons from Aβ/tau toxicity.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Altering synaptic activity patterns in resistant neurons may render them vulnerable to Aβ/tau pathology.
  2. Some neurons may compensate for high synaptic activity with alternative calcium regulation mechanisms, conferring resistance.

Negative Experiments

  1. If neurons with high synaptic activity and low calcium buffering do not show increased vulnerability, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If boosting calcium buffering does not confer resistance, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The role of non-synaptic factors (e.g., mitochondrial function, immune signaling) is not addressed.
