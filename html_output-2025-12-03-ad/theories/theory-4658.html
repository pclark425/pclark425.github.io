Theory Statements

Statement 0: Cumulative Risk Surpasses Pathogenic Threshold (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has_cumulative_risk_score → above_threshold
THEN
• individual → initiates_AD_pathogenesis → true

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Polygenic risk, metabolic syndrome, and chronic inflammation each increase AD risk, but combinations are more predictive.
  2. Longitudinal studies show individuals with multiple risk factors develop AD at higher rates than those with single risk factors.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The explicit threshold and integration of risk factors is novel.

What Already Exists: Multifactorial risk is acknowledged, but not formalized as a threshold model.

What is Novel: This law formalizes a quantitative threshold for AD initiation based on cumulative risk.

References:

  • Livingston (2020) Dementia prevention, intervention, and care: 2020 report of the Lancet Commission [Multifactorial risk, not threshold]
  • Desikan (2017) Polygenic hazard score for Alzheimer's disease: implications for precision medicine [Polygenic risk, not integrated threshold]

Statement 1: Integrated Multi-Modal Detection Outperforms Single Biomarkers (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has_multi_modal_risk_profile → high
THEN
• individual → is_in_preclinical_AD → true

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Combining genetic, metabolic, and inflammatory markers improves early AD prediction over amyloid/tau alone.
  2. Machine learning models integrating multi-modal data outperform single biomarker approaches in AD risk stratification.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The primacy and formalization of multi-modal detection is novel.

What Already Exists: Multi-modal detection is being explored, but not as the primary detection paradigm.

What is Novel: This law asserts integrated risk profiling as the most effective early detection method.

References:

  • Jack (2018) NIA-AA Research Framework: Toward a biological definition of Alzheimer's disease [Multi-modal, but still amyloid/tau-centric]
  • Iturria-Medina (2017) Multifactorial causal model of brain (dis)organization and therapeutic intervention: Application to Alzheimer's disease [Multifactorial, but not formalized detection]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with high cumulative risk scores but normal amyloid/tau will develop AD at higher rates.
  2. Interventions that reduce multiple risk factors simultaneously will delay or prevent AD onset more effectively than targeting single factors.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Personalized prevention based on multi-modal risk profiling will eliminate AD incidence in high-risk populations.
  2. Wearable biosensors integrating metabolic and inflammatory markers will enable real-time AD risk monitoring.

Negative Experiments

  1. If individuals with high cumulative risk but normal amyloid/tau do not develop AD at higher rates, the theory is challenged.
  2. If multi-modal detection does not outperform single biomarker approaches, the theory is undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases of AD with no identifiable risk factors may not fit the model.
