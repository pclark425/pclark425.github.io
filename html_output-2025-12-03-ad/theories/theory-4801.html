Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4801

Name: Molecular Signature-Driven Susceptibility Model

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability or resistance of neuronal populations in the visual cortex to Aβ and tau pathology is determined by their unique molecular signatures, including expression of specific receptors, proteostasis machinery, and stress response genes. Populations expressing high levels of Aβ/tau-binding receptors, low levels of proteostasis components, or deficient stress response pathways are more vulnerable, while those with protective molecular profiles are resistant.

THEN
• neuronal_population → is_selectively_vulnerable_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neuronal populations with high PrPC or LRP1 expression show increased Aβ/tau uptake and toxicity.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

What is Novel: The population-level prediction based on receptor expression profiles in the visual cortex is novel.

References:

  • Lauren et al. (2009) Cellular prion protein mediates impairment of synaptic plasticity by amyloid-β oligomers [PrPC as Aβ receptor]

Statement 1: Proteostasis-Driven Resistance Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_expression_of → proteostasis_machinery (e.g., chaperones, autophagy genes)
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Populations with robust proteostasis (e.g., high chaperone/autophagy gene expression) show reduced Aβ/tau accumulation.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Proteostasis is known to be protective, but its population-specific application in the visual cortex is new.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis impairment is linked to neurodegeneration.

What is Novel: The explicit prediction that high proteostasis gene expression confers resistance in specific visual cortex populations is novel.

References:

  • Hetz & Saxena (2017) ER stress and the unfolded protein response in neurodegeneration [Proteostasis in neurodegeneration]
  • Ciechanover & Kwon (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin–proteasome system in neurodegeneration [Proteostasis machinery]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Overexpressing Aβ/tau-binding receptors in resistant populations will increase their vulnerability.
  2. Enhancing proteostasis machinery in vulnerable populations will confer resistance to Aβ/tau pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. CRISPR-mediated editing of stress response genes in resistant populations will render them vulnerable.
  2. Single-cell transcriptomics will reveal a correlation between molecular signatures and vulnerability across visual cortex populations.

Negative Experiments

  1. If populations with high receptor expression remain resistant, the theory is challenged.
  2. If enhancing proteostasis does not confer resistance, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some populations with protective molecular signatures still develop pathology, suggesting additional factors are involved.
