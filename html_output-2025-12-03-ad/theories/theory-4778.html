Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4778

Name: Intrinsic Molecular Signature Theory of Neuronal Vulnerability: Proteostasis Threshold Model

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that each neuronal population in the visual cortex has a unique proteostasis threshold, determined by its intrinsic molecular signature. When the burden of misfolded proteins (amyloid-β, tau) exceeds this threshold, the neuron becomes vulnerable to degeneration. The threshold is set by the expression of chaperones, autophagy-lysosomal genes, and related stress response pathways.

Statement 0: Proteostasis Threshold Law (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron_population → has_proteostasis_capacity → P, and
• neuron_population → is_exposed_to → misfolded_protein_burden M, and
• M → greater_than → P
THEN
• neuron_population → is_vulnerable_to → amyloid-β and tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Proteostasis capacity varies between neuronal populations and correlates with vulnerability.
  2. Experimental overload of misfolded proteins leads to degeneration when proteostasis is insufficient.
  3. Upregulation of chaperones or autophagy genes increases the proteostasis threshold and confers resistance.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law introduces a quantitative, testable model for population-specific vulnerability.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis failure is implicated in neurodegeneration, but a quantitative threshold model for population vulnerability is not standard.

What is Novel: The explicit threshold law relating molecular signature to a quantitative vulnerability threshold is novel.

References:

  • Hetz & Saxena (2017) ER stress and the unfolded protein response in neurodegeneration [Proteostasis in neurodegeneration]
  • Mathys et al. (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [Population-specific molecular data]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Populations with higher proteostasis capacity will tolerate greater misfolded protein burden before showing pathology.
  2. Pharmacological enhancement of proteostasis will raise the threshold and delay pathology onset.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. There may be populations with extremely high thresholds that never develop pathology, even in severe AD.
  2. Chronic, sub-threshold misfolded protein burden may induce compensatory upregulation of proteostasis genes.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding populations with low proteostasis capacity that remain resistant despite high misfolded protein burden would challenge the theory.
  2. Demonstrating that increasing proteostasis capacity does not alter the threshold for pathology would call the theory into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The influence of non-cell-autonomous factors (e.g., glial support, vascular supply) is not included.
