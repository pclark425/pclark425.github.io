Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-551

Name: Peripheral Blood-Based Biomarker Constellation for Early AD Detection

Type: specific

Theory Query: Build a theory of the cause of Alzheimer's disease and how it can be effectively detected, based on the following results.

Description: This theory asserts that a constellation of minimally invasive blood-based biomarkers—including plasma Aβ42/40 ratio, phosphorylated tau isoforms (p-tau181, p-tau217, p-tau231), neurofilament light (NfL), GFAP, and exosomal neuronal proteins—can collectively detect the earliest stages of Alzheimer's disease with high sensitivity and specificity, rivaling or surpassing CSF and PET-based methods. The theory further proposes that the temporal sequence of changes in these markers reflects the underlying pathophysiological cascade, and that their combined use enables both early detection and staging of disease progression.

ConditionDetails

• individual → has → abnormal plasma Aβ42/40 ratio, elevated plasma p-tau isoforms, and/or increased NfL/GFAP

Supporting Evidence for this Law

Statement 1: Temporal Sequence Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → is progressing → from preclinical to symptomatic AD
THEN
• blood biomarker changes → occur in sequence → Aβ42/40 ratio decreases first, followed by p-tau increases, then NfL/GFAP elevation

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Longitudinal studies and cross-sectional biomarker panels show that Aβ42/40 changes precede p-tau, which in turn precedes NfL/GFAP elevation. [e4033.8] [e3908.7] [e3942.15] [e3932.4]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. A multi-analyte blood panel will detect preclinical AD in cognitively normal individuals who later convert to MCI/AD, with accuracy comparable to CSF/PET.
  2. Temporal changes in blood biomarkers will correlate with disease stage and rate of progression, enabling personalized staging and prognosis.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Blood biomarker panels will identify subtypes of AD with distinct biomarker trajectories (e.g., Aβ-first, tau-first, NfL-first), each with different clinical outcomes.
  2. Blood-based detection will enable population-level screening and early intervention, reducing the incidence of clinical AD.

Negative Experiments

  1. If blood biomarker panels do not outperform single markers or do not match CSF/PET accuracy, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If the predicted temporal sequence of biomarker changes is not observed in longitudinal studies, the staging model would be undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Peripheral comorbidities (e.g., kidney/liver disease, inflammation) that alter blood biomarker levels independent of AD are not fully addressed. [e3933.10] [e3942.15]
