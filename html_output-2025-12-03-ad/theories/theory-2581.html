Name: Neurovascular Dysfunction as Parallel Pathogenic Axis

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the cause of Alzheimer's disease, and how it can be effectively detected, based on the following results.

Description: A model in which age‑related blood‑brain barrier (BBB) breakdown, pericyte loss, and chronic neuroinflammation act as an independent but interacting pathway that accelerates amyloid and tau pathology, leading to neurodegeneration. Biomarkers of BBB permeability (Ktrans), plasma GFAP, and inflammatory cytokines provide early detection of this vascular axis.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Age‑dependent hippocampal BBB permeability increase (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• subject age → exceeds → 65 years, and
• pericyte health → declines → below threshold P1
THEN
• hippocampal Ktrans → increases → by >30% relative to young adults, and
• CSF/plasma albumin ratio → elevates → by ~30%

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. DCE‑MRI shows hippocampal Ktrans +41% in older NCI vs young, +24% in MCI (e8104.0). [e8104.0]
  2. CSF Qalb correlates with hippocampal Ktrans (e8104.0). [e8104.0]

What is Novel: Specific quantitative hippocampal Ktrans increases linked to pericyte loss and albumin ratio, and their temporal relationship to MCI conversion.

Statement 1: Neuroinflammation amplifies amyloid‑tau pathology (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• plasma GFAP level → exceeds → threshold G1, and
• pro‑inflammatory cytokines (e.g., IL‑12p70, IFN‑γ) → elevated → above baseline
THEN
• rate of amyloid PET centiloid increase → accelerates → by >10% per year, and
• tau PET progression → speeds up → by >15% per year

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Higher GFAP associated with amyloid PET positivity and predicts conversion (e7937.2). [e7937.2]
  2. Baseline IL‑12p70 and IFN‑γ predict faster tau PET progression (e7930.2). [e7930.2]

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Adds predictive temporal dynamics to known inflammatory involvement.

What Already Exists: Neuroinflammation is implicated in AD; GFAP is an astrocytic marker.

What is Novel: Quantitative link between plasma GFAP/cytokines and accelerated amyloid/tau PET progression.

References:

  • Zeng et al. (2024) Multi‑analyte proteomics identifies neuroinflammation biomarkers [provides cytokine data]
  • Sweeney et al. (2019) Vascular contributions to AD

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. In a cohort of cognitively normal adults >70 y, individuals with hippocampal Ktrans >1.5 × young baseline will convert to MCI within 3 years at double the rate of those with lower Ktrans.
  2. Elevated plasma GFAP (>150 pg/mL) combined with high IL‑12p70 will predict a >20% faster increase in amyloid centiloid values over two years.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Targeted pericyte‑protective therapy (e.g., PDGFR‑β agonist) will normalize Ktrans and subsequently reduce the rate of amyloid PET accumulation, testing causality of BBB breakdown.
  2. Long‑term anti‑inflammatory treatment (e.g., IL‑1β blockade) will lower plasma GFAP and cytokine levels and delay tau PET conversion, revealing whether inflammation drives tau spread.

Negative Experiments

  1. If hippocampal Ktrans does not differ between converters and non‑converters after controlling for age, the BBB breakdown component would be invalidated.
  2. If plasma GFAP levels are not associated with amyloid PET progression in a large longitudinal sample, the inflammatory amplification hypothesis would be refuted.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some AD patients show normal Ktrans despite progression, indicating alternative pathways (e8104.2). [e8104.2]
  2. Microvascular disease markers (e.g., WM Ktrans) are not consistently altered across cohorts (e8104.0). [e8104.0]
