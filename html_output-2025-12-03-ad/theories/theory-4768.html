Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4768

Name: Metabolic-Proteostatic Threshold Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortical neurons to Aβ and tau pathology is governed by the balance between their metabolic demand and proteostatic capacity. Neurons with high energy consumption and low proteostatic reserve are more likely to accumulate misfolded proteins, while those with lower metabolic rates or enhanced proteostasis are resistant.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: High Metabolic Demand Increases Proteopathic Risk (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_metabolic_demand → true, and
• neuronal_population → has_low_proteostatic_capacity → true
THEN
• neuronal_population → accumulates → Aβ_plaques_and_tau_tangles

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Upper-layer pyramidal neurons show high mitochondrial activity and low expression of proteostasis genes, correlating with early pathology.
  2. Proteostatic stress markers are elevated in metabolically active, vulnerable neurons.

What is Novel: The explicit threshold model linking the ratio of metabolic demand to proteostatic capacity to selective vulnerability in visual cortex is novel.

  • Mattson (2004) Pathways towards and away from Alzheimer's disease [Metabolic stress in AD]

Statement 1: Proteostatic Enhancement Confers Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_proteostatic_capacity → true
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with upregulated chaperone and autophagy genes are less likely to accumulate Aβ/tau aggregates.
  2. Experimental enhancement of proteostasis reduces pathology in vulnerable neurons.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law extends a general mechanism to a specific, testable context.

What Already Exists: Proteostatic enhancement is known to reduce neurodegeneration in models.

What is Novel: The application of this principle to explain selective resistance in visual cortex is novel.

References:

  • Koren et al. (2019) Proteostasis in the brain: mechanisms and roles in neurodegeneration [Proteostasis and resistance]
  • Cohen et al. (2013) The ubiquitin-proteasome system and proteostasis in aging and disease [Proteostasis and neuroprotection]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Reducing metabolic demand in vulnerable neurons will decrease Aβ/tau aggregation.
  2. Boosting proteostatic capacity in high-demand neurons will confer resistance to pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Altering the metabolic/proteostatic ratio in resistant neurons will render them vulnerable.
  2. Genetic or pharmacological interventions that shift the threshold will change the spatial pattern of pathology in visual cortex.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding neurons with high metabolic demand and low proteostatic capacity that do not develop pathology would challenge the theory.
  2. Demonstrating that proteostatic enhancement does not confer resistance would call the theory into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some neurons may be resistant despite high metabolic demand, possibly due to unknown compensatory mechanisms.
