Name: Network Disintegration Cascade and Multimodal Early Detection

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the cause of Alzheimer's disease and how it can be effectively detected.

Description: Alzheimer's disease is initiated by a cascade of disruptions in large-scale brain network connectivity, triggered by subtle synaptic dysfunction and metabolic stress, which precede and drive classical protein aggregation. Theory Statements

Statement 0: Network Disintegration Precedes Proteinopathy (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → synaptic/metabolic stress
THEN
• large-scale brain networks → show → reduced connectivity and synchrony, and
• protein aggregation → is → not yet overt

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Functional connectivity changes (EEG, fMRI) are observed in preclinical and early Alzheimer's before significant amyloid/tau deposition.
  2. Synaptic dysfunction and metabolic deficits are early features in AD models and human studies.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The primacy of network-level changes as the initiating event is a novel synthesis.

What Already Exists: Network dysfunction and synaptic loss are recognized in AD, but usually as downstream effects.

What is Novel: This law posits network disintegration as the earliest, primary driver, preceding proteinopathy.

References:

  • Seeley (2009) Neurodegenerative diseases target large-scale human brain networks [network vulnerability in AD]
  • Brier (2014) Functional connectivity and Alzheimer's disease: a review [network changes in AD]

Statement 1: Multimodal Network Biomarkers Enable Preclinical Detection (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → early network disintegration
THEN
• multimodal measures (EEG, fMRI, MEG, synaptic/metabolic markers) → show → abnormal patterns before clinical symptoms

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. EEG and fMRI detect altered connectivity in at-risk individuals before cognitive decline.
  2. Synaptic and metabolic PET tracers (e.g., SV2A, FDG) show early changes in preclinical AD.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: The explicit integration of multimodal network and metabolic/synaptic markers for earliest detection is novel.

What Already Exists: Multimodal imaging and synaptic/metabolic markers are used in AD research.

What is Novel: This law proposes their integration as the most sensitive preclinical detection strategy, before proteinopathy.

References:

  • Jack (2018) NIA-AA Research Framework: Toward a biological definition of Alzheimer's disease [biomarker framework]
  • Brier (2014) Functional connectivity and Alzheimer's disease: a review [network changes in AD]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with early network disintegration (by EEG/fMRI) will progress to Alzheimer's at higher rates, even without amyloid/tau positivity.
  2. Interventions that restore network synchrony/metabolism will delay or prevent Alzheimer's onset.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Network disintegration patterns in adolescence or early adulthood will predict Alzheimer's risk decades later.
  2. Non-invasive brain stimulation to restore network synchrony will reverse preclinical AD changes.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding individuals with network disintegration but no increased Alzheimer's risk would challenge the theory.
  2. Demonstrating that Alzheimer's can develop in the absence of early network disintegration would refute the theory.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases of Alzheimer's with minimal detectable network changes prior to proteinopathy.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: new
