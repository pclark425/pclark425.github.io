Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2387

Name: Network-Connectivity-Driven Selective Vulnerability Theory: Activity-Dependent Propagation

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability of visual cortical neurons to Aβ and tau pathology is driven by their network connectivity in combination with their activity-dependent synaptic dynamics. Neurons with high synaptic activity and strong participation in recurrent excitatory loops are more likely to facilitate the trans-synaptic propagation of pathological proteins, while neurons with low activity and weak recurrent connectivity are more resistant.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Activity-Dependent Propagation of Pathology (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_synaptic_activity → True, and
• neuronal_population → is_part_of_recurrent_excitatory_loops → True
THEN
• neuronal_population → facilitates_propagation_of → Aβ_and_tau_pathology, and
• neuronal_population → is_vulnerable_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Experimental models show that increased synaptic activity accelerates tau and Aβ spread.
  2. Recurrent excitatory circuits are early sites of pathology in Alzheimer's disease.
  3. Visual cortex neurons with high activity are more affected in transgenic models.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Somewhat related to existing work, but more formal and integrative in its predictions.

What Already Exists: Activity-dependent propagation of tau and Aβ is described in some experimental studies.

What is Novel: This law formalizes the interaction between network connectivity and activity in determining vulnerability.

References:

  • Wu et al. (2016) Neuronal activity enhances tau propagation and pathology in vivo [Activity-dependent tau spread]
  • Yamamoto et al. (2015) Chronic optogenetic activation augments Aβ pathology [Activity and Aβ]

Statement 1: Low Activity and Weak Recurrence Confer Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_low_synaptic_activity → True, and
• neuronal_population → is_not_part_of_recurrent_excitatory_loops → True
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Layer 4 spiny stellate cells, which have low activity and weak recurrence, are relatively spared in Alzheimer's disease.
  2. Animal models show that silencing activity reduces tau and Aβ spread.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Somewhat related to existing work, but more formal and specific in its predictions for visual cortex.

What Already Exists: Low activity and weak recurrence are associated with resistance in some experimental models.

What is Novel: This law formalizes the resistance mechanism as a function of both activity and network structure.

References:

  • Wu et al. (2016) Neuronal activity enhances tau propagation and pathology in vivo [Activity-dependent tau spread]
  • Braak (2011) Staging of Alzheimer-related cortical destruction [Layer 4 sparing]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Neuronal populations with high synaptic activity and recurrent connectivity in the visual cortex will show greater Aβ and tau pathology.
  2. Reducing synaptic activity in vulnerable populations will decrease pathology spread.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing activity in resistant populations will render them vulnerable to pathology.
  2. Disrupting recurrent connectivity in vulnerable populations will confer resistance.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding that high-activity, recurrently connected neurons are resistant to pathology would challenge the theory.
  2. Observing that low-activity, non-recurrent populations are vulnerable would contradict the theory.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The role of molecular chaperones or intrinsic protein handling is not addressed.
