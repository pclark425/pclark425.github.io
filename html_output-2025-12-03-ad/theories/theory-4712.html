Statement 0: Air Pollution Triggers Systemic Inflammation and Epigenetic Reprogramming (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → is_exposed_to → chronic air pollution
THEN
• individual's immune system → shows → increased pro-inflammatory cytokines and altered DNA methylation/histone marks at immune genes

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Air pollution exposure increases circulating cytokines and induces epigenetic changes in immune cells.
  2. Systemic inflammation is a risk factor for AD and is linked to accelerated cognitive decline.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law extends environmental immunology to neurodegenerative disease risk.

What Already Exists: Air pollution is known to cause systemic inflammation and immune cell epigenetic changes.

What is Novel: The direct link to AD risk via immune-epigenetic amplification is new.

References:

  • Block (2012) Air pollution and Alzheimer's disease: mechanisms and implications [systemic inflammation, not explicit epigenetic mediation]
  • Baccarelli (2009) Air pollution, inflammation, and epigenetics: mechanism and implications for cardiovascular disease [immune epigenetics, not AD]

Statement 1: Peripheral Inflammation and Epigenetic Changes Amplify CNS Amyloid/Tau Pathology (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → systemic inflammation and immune cell epigenetic reprogramming
THEN
• individual's brain → shows → increased neuroinflammation and accelerated amyloid/tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Peripheral inflammation and immune cell epigenetic changes are associated with increased neuroinflammation and AD pathology in animal models.
  2. Systemic inflammation is linked to increased amyloid and tau burden in human studies.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

What Already Exists: Peripheral inflammation is linked to neuroinflammation and AD pathology.

What is Novel: The amplification via epigenetic reprogramming of immune cells is a novel mechanistic step.

References:

  • Heneka (2015) Neuroinflammation in Alzheimer's disease [peripheral-CNS inflammation link, not epigenetic]
  • Baccarelli (2009) Air pollution, inflammation, and epigenetics: mechanism and implications for cardiovascular disease [immune epigenetics, not AD]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with high air pollution exposure and elevated peripheral inflammation will show accelerated AD biomarker progression.
  2. Anti-inflammatory or epigenetic-modifying therapies will slow AD progression in pollution-exposed populations.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Targeted reversal of immune cell epigenetic changes will reduce neuroinflammation and amyloid/tau pathology in AD models.
  2. Distinct immune epigenetic signatures will predict AD risk independent of genetic background.

Negative Experiments

  1. If air pollution does not induce immune cell epigenetic changes, the amplification model is challenged.
  2. If peripheral inflammation does not precede or amplify CNS pathology, the theory is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. AD cases with minimal systemic inflammation or immune epigenetic changes.
