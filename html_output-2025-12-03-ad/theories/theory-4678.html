Theory (General Information)

Name: Integrated Digital-Pathobiological Detection Theory of Alzheimer's Disease (IDPD-AD): Network Vulnerability and Digital Phenotype Model

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the cause of Alzheimer's disease and how it can be effectively detected.

Description: This theory proposes that Alzheimer's disease is initiated by selective vulnerability of specific brain networks (e.g., default mode, limbic, and language networks) to molecular pathologies (amyloid, tau, neuroinflammation), and that these network disruptions manifest as quantifiable digital phenotypes in behavior, cognition, and sensorimotor function. The integration of network-level pathobiology with digital phenotyping enables precise mapping of disease onset, subtype, and progression.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Network-Pathology-Digital Mapping Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → molecular pathology (amyloid, tau, neuroinflammation) in specific brain networks
THEN
• individual → exhibits → network-specific digital phenotypes (e.g., language errors for language network, spatial navigation errors for default mode network)

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Amyloid and tau deposition show network-specific patterns in AD, with default mode and limbic networks affected early.
  2. Digital phenotyping (e.g., speech, navigation, fine motor tasks) can reveal network-specific deficits.
  3. Functional MRI and connectomics studies show that network disruption correlates with specific cognitive and behavioral deficits.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: While network-based and digital approaches exist, their formal integration as a mapping law is new.

What Already Exists: Network-based vulnerability to AD pathology is established, and digital phenotyping is emerging.

What is Novel: The explicit mapping of network-level pathology to specific digital phenotypes, and the use of this mapping for detection and subtyping, is novel.

References:

  • Seeley et al. (2009) Neurodegenerative diseases target large-scale human brain networks [Network vulnerability]
  • Brier et al. (2014) Functional connectivity and network dysfunction in Alzheimer's disease [Network disruption]
  • Kourtis et al. (2019) Digital biomarkers for Alzheimer's disease: the mobile/wearable devices opportunity [Digital phenotyping]

Statement 1: Digital Phenotype Progression Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → progressive network-level pathology
THEN
• individual → shows → progressive changes in digital phenotypes corresponding to affected networks

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Longitudinal digital assessments show progressive changes in speech, navigation, and motor function in AD.
  2. Network-level pathology progression (e.g., tau spreading) correlates with worsening digital phenotypes.
  3. Digital phenotyping can track disease progression and response to therapy.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: The explicit linkage of digital phenotype progression to network pathology progression is new.

What Already Exists: Progression of network pathology and digital changes are each observed.

What is Novel: The law that digital phenotype progression mirrors network-level pathology progression is novel.

References:

  • Brier et al. (2014) Functional connectivity and network dysfunction in Alzheimer's disease [Network progression]
  • Fristed et al. (2021) Evaluation of speech-based digital biomarkers for Alzheimer's disease [Digital progression]
  • Kourtis et al. (2019) Digital biomarkers for Alzheimer's disease: the mobile/wearable devices opportunity [Digital phenotyping]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with early network-specific digital phenotypes will show corresponding network pathology on imaging.
  2. Digital phenotype progression will predict the spatial and temporal spread of pathology in the brain.
  3. Network-based digital phenotyping will enable subtyping of AD (e.g., language-dominant, visuospatial-dominant) with higher accuracy.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Previously unrecognized digital phenotypes (e.g., micro-motor signatures) will be linked to specific network pathologies.
  2. Some individuals may show digital phenotype progression without detectable network pathology, suggesting alternative mechanisms.
  3. Network-based digital phenotyping will identify preclinical AD in individuals with atypical presentations.

Negative Experiments

  1. If digital phenotypes do not map to network pathology, the mapping law is falsified.
  2. If digital phenotype progression does not mirror network pathology progression, the theory is undermined.
  3. If network-based digital phenotyping does not improve subtyping or prediction, the theory is challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Individuals with mixed or atypical pathology (e.g., vascular, Lewy body) may not fit network-digital mapping.
  2. High cognitive reserve may mask digital phenotypes despite network pathology.
