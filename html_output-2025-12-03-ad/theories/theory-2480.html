Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2480

Name: Activity-Dependent Network Vulnerability Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory posits that the selective vulnerability of visual cortex neurons to amyloid-β and tau pathology is governed by their participation in highly active, recurrent network motifs. Neurons that are central to recurrent excitatory loops or are frequently co-active with other vulnerable populations experience greater metabolic and synaptic stress, facilitating pathological protein aggregation and spread. Conversely, neurons with sparse activity or those embedded in feedforward, non-recurrent circuits are more resistant.

Statement 0: Recurrent Network Participation Increases Vulnerability (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → is_part_of → recurrent_excitatory_motif, and
• neuron → has_high_activity_level → true
THEN
• neuron → is_vulnerable_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons in recurrent circuits show higher metabolic demand and are early sites of tau pathology.
  2. Activity-dependent release and uptake of tau and amyloid-β have been demonstrated in vitro and in vivo.
  3. Highly active neurons accumulate more pathological proteins in animal models.

Statement 1: Feedforward or Sparse Activity Neurons Are More Resistant (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → is_part_of → feedforward_or_sparse_activity_circuit
THEN
• neuron → is_resistant_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Feedforward circuits and sparsely active neurons are less affected in early Alzheimer's pathology.
  2. Layer 4 spiny stellate cells, which are part of feedforward circuits, are relatively spared.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

What Already Exists: Feedforward circuits are less affected in early Alzheimer's disease.

What is Novel: The mechanistic link to sparse activity and feedforward motif participation is new.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Optogenetic stimulation of recurrent circuits in the visual cortex will accelerate amyloid-β and tau pathology in those neurons.
  2. Neurons with higher spontaneous activity rates will show greater pathological protein accumulation.
  3. Feedforward circuit neurons will remain relatively spared even in advanced disease stages.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially inducing recurrent activity in resistant neurons will increase their vulnerability to pathology.
  2. Silencing recurrent circuits will delay or reduce pathology spread in the visual cortex.

Negative Experiments

  1. If feedforward or sparsely active neurons show equal or greater vulnerability than recurrent, highly active neurons, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If activity manipulation does not alter vulnerability, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  2. Glial and vascular contributions to vulnerability are not addressed by this theory.
