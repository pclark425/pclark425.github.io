Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4809

Name: Hierarchical Network-Metabolic Cascade Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the hierarchical organization of the visual cortex, combined with metabolic gradients, creates a cascade of vulnerability to amyloid-β and tau pathology. Higher-order visual areas, which integrate information from multiple lower-order regions and have higher metabolic rates, are more vulnerable. Pathology propagates preferentially along hierarchical and metabolic gradients, explaining the spatial-temporal pattern of neurodegeneration.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Higher-order visual areas (e.g., V4, inferotemporal cortex) show early and severe pathology in Alzheimer's disease.
  2. These regions have higher metabolic rates and integrate information from multiple sources.

Statement 1: Cascade Propagation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• pathology → is_present_in → higher_order_high_metabolic_region
THEN
• pathology → propagates_to → adjacent_lower_order_or_lower_metabolic_regions

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Pathology in dementia often spreads from higher-order to lower-order regions, following connectivity and metabolic gradients.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: The explicit integration of hierarchy, metabolism, and propagation is novel.

What Already Exists: Trans-synaptic propagation of pathology is observed in neurodegenerative diseases.

What is Novel: The law links propagation to hierarchical and metabolic gradients in the visual cortex.

References:

  • Braak & Braak (1991) Neuropathological stageing of Alzheimer-related changes [propagation patterns]
  • Felleman & Van Essen (1991) Distributed hierarchical processing in the primate cerebral cortex [hierarchical organization]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Early pathology will be observed in higher-order, metabolically active visual areas before lower-order regions.
  2. Disruption of hierarchical connectivity will alter the spatial pattern of pathology propagation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing metabolic rate in lower-order regions will increase their vulnerability only if hierarchical connectivity is also enhanced.
  2. Blocking trans-synaptic signaling will slow or prevent propagation of pathology along the hierarchy.

Negative Experiments

  1. If lower-order, low-metabolic regions show early pathology, the theory is challenged.
  2. If pathology does not propagate along hierarchical or metabolic gradients, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Intrinsic molecular differences (e.g., protein expression profiles) between regions that may influence vulnerability independently of hierarchy or metabolism.
