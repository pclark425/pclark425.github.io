Name: Proteostatic Collapse Cascade Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the cause of Alzheimer's disease and how it can be effectively detected.

Description: Alzheimer's disease is initiated by a collapse in neuronal proteostasis, leading to a self-amplifying cascade of amyloid-beta and tau misfolding, which propagates through prion-like mechanisms and triggers secondary neuroinflammatory and synaptic dysfunction. Early detection is possible by identifying molecular signatures of proteostatic stress and misfolded protein spread.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostatic Collapse Initiation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → has_proteostatic_stress → above_threshold
THEN
• neuron → initiates_amyloid_and_tau_misfolding → True

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Proteostatic stress (e.g., ER stress, impaired autophagy) precedes amyloid and tau aggregation in models.
  2. Genetic and environmental risk factors converge on proteostasis pathways.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law formalizes the causal role of proteostatic collapse as the trigger for AD pathology.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis failure is recognized as a risk factor.

What is Novel: Explicit law of threshold-driven initiation of misfolding cascade.

References:

  • Hetz & Saxena (2017) ER stress and the unfolded protein response in neurodegeneration [proteostasis in AD]
  • Hipp, Park & Hartl (2014) Proteostasis impairment in protein-misfolding and aggregation diseases [proteostasis collapse]

Statement 1: Prion-like Propagation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• misfolded_protein → is_present_in_neuron → True
THEN
• misfolded_protein → spreads_to_connected_neurons → True

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Amyloid-beta and tau can propagate between neurons in a prion-like manner.
  2. Patterns of pathology spread follow neural connectivity.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law formalizes the prion-like propagation as a necessary step in AD progression.

What Already Exists: Prion-like spread of tau and amyloid is increasingly recognized.

What is Novel: Explicit law linking misfolded protein presence to network propagation.

References:

  • Walker & Jucker (2015) Neurodegenerative diseases: expanding the prion concept [prion-like propagation]
  • Braak & Braak (1991) Neuropathological stageing of Alzheimer-related changes [spread along neural networks]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Early molecular markers of proteostatic stress will predict later amyloid and tau aggregation.
  2. Blocking prion-like propagation will slow or halt the spread of pathology and cognitive decline.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Novel proteostasis-enhancing therapies will prevent or reverse early AD pathology.
  2. Non-neuronal cells (e.g., glia) may also participate in prion-like propagation.

Negative Experiments

  1. If proteostatic stress does not precede amyloid/tau aggregation, the theory is challenged.
  2. If blocking prion-like propagation does not alter disease progression, the law is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some cases of AD show little evidence of prion-like spread.
