Theory Statements

Statement 0: Network Topology Resistance Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• V1 → has → high local clustering coefficient, and
• V1 → has → low long-range connectivity to early-affected regions
THEN
• propagation of Aβ/tau pathology into V1 → is_reduced → relative to association cortex

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. V1 is a highly modular, locally clustered network with fewer long-range connections than association cortex.
  2. Network models show that modularity and low long-range connectivity slow the spread of pathological agents.
  3. Empirical data show delayed and reduced pathology in V1 compared to more interconnected regions.

Statement 1: Homeostatic Plasticity Protection Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• V1 neurons → exhibit → robust homeostatic synaptic plasticity, and
• homeostatic mechanisms → maintain → synaptic protein turnover and clearance
THEN
• accumulation of Aβ/tau aggregates in V1 → is_limited → relative to regions with weaker homeostasis

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. V1 shows strong homeostatic plasticity and synaptic scaling in response to activity changes.
  2. Homeostatic mechanisms can enhance protein clearance and limit aggregate accumulation.
  3. Regions with impaired homeostasis are more vulnerable to aggregation and degeneration.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law extends known homeostatic principles to a new context of selective neurodegenerative resistance.

What Already Exists: Homeostatic plasticity and its role in synaptic maintenance are established.

What is Novel: Directly linking these mechanisms to resistance against Aβ/tau aggregation in V1 is novel.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Disruption of V1 network modularity (e.g., by increasing long-range connectivity) will increase vulnerability to pathology.
  2. Impairment of homeostatic plasticity in V1 (e.g., via genetic manipulation) will lead to increased aggregate accumulation.
  3. Other brain regions with similar network topology and homeostatic capacity will show similar resistance.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If V1 is transplanted into a highly interconnected network, will it lose its resistance?
  2. Are there subpopulations within V1 with distinct network properties that show differential vulnerability?
  3. Can artificial enhancement of homeostatic plasticity in association cortex confer resistance similar to V1?

Negative Experiments

  1. If V1 remains resistant despite disruption of network topology or homeostatic mechanisms, the theory is challenged.
  2. If regions with similar topology and homeostasis do not show resistance, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The role of molecular seeding factors is not directly addressed.
  2. Cases of V1 pathology in the absence of network or homeostatic disruption are not explained.
