Theory (General Information)

Name: Cellular Defense Capacity Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective resistance or vulnerability of visual cortical neurons to amyloid-β and tau pathology is determined by the intrinsic capacity of each neuronal population to mount cellular defense responses, including proteostasis, autophagy, and antioxidant mechanisms. Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with high expression of chaperones and autophagy genes are less likely to accumulate misfolded proteins.
  2. Visual cortical layers with robust proteostasis machinery show delayed or reduced pathology.
  3. Impairment of autophagy increases vulnerability to amyloid and tau accumulation.
