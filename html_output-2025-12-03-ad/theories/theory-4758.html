Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4758

Name: Cell-Intrinsic Proteostasis-Immune Signature Threshold Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory posits that each neuronal population in the visual cortex possesses a unique cell-intrinsic proteostasis-immune signature, defined by the expression levels and responsiveness of key proteostasis and immune genes. Selective vulnerability or resistance to Aβ and tau pathology is determined by whether this signature crosses a critical threshold for adaptive response.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Signature Above Threshold Confers Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal population → has_proteostasis-immune_signature → above_adaptive_threshold
THEN
• neuronal population → is_resistant_to → Aβ and tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Single-cell RNA-seq reveals that resistant neurons express higher levels of both proteostasis and immune regulatory genes.
  2. Experimental overexpression of key proteostasis and immune genes in vulnerable neurons confers resistance to Aβ and tau aggregation.
  3. Threshold effects are observed in stress response pathways, where only above-threshold activation leads to protection.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law synthesizes known threshold effects into a new, combined proteostasis-immune signature model.

What Already Exists: Threshold effects in stress response pathways are known, but not specifically for combined proteostasis-immune signatures.

What is Novel: The idea that a combined cell-intrinsic proteostasis-immune signature threshold determines selective vulnerability is novel.

References:

  • Hetz & Saxena (2017) ER stress and the unfolded protein response in neurodegeneration [UPR threshold effects]
  • Mathys et al. (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [Single-cell data]

Statement 1: Signature Below Threshold Confers Vulnerability (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal population → has_proteostasis-immune_signature → below_adaptive_threshold
THEN
• neuronal population → is_vulnerable_to → Aβ and tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Vulnerable neurons in AD models show low expression of both proteostasis and immune regulatory genes.
  2. Knockdown of key proteostasis or immune genes in resistant neurons increases vulnerability.
  3. Failure to reach a critical threshold in stress response pathways leads to pathology.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law extends known gene expression correlates into a formal threshold model.

What Already Exists: Low expression of protective genes is associated with vulnerability, but not as a formal thresholded signature.

What is Novel: The formalization of a combined proteostasis-immune signature threshold as a determinant is novel.

References:

  • Mathys et al. (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [Single-cell data]
  • Hetz & Saxena (2017) ER stress and the unfolded protein response in neurodegeneration [UPR threshold effects]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Neuronal populations with proteostasis-immune signatures above the adaptive threshold will be resistant in multiple AD models.
  2. Gene editing to raise the signature above threshold in vulnerable neurons will confer resistance.
  3. Proteostasis-immune signature profiling will predict vulnerability across brain regions.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Small molecule drugs that boost the signature above threshold will prevent pathology in vivo.
  2. Longitudinal tracking of signature dynamics will predict conversion from resistance to vulnerability.
  3. Signature manipulation in vivo will alter disease progression in animal models.

Negative Experiments

  1. If raising the signature above threshold does not confer resistance, the theory is challenged.
  2. If neurons with below-threshold signatures are not vulnerable, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The influence of post-translational modifications and protein activity (not just gene expression) is not directly addressed.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: No prior theory formalizes a combined proteostasis-immune signature threshold for selective vulnerability.

References:

  • Hetz & Saxena (2017) ER stress and the unfolded protein response in neurodegeneration [UPR threshold effects]
  • Mathys et al. (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [Single-cell data]
