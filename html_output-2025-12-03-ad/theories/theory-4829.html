Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4829

Name: Activity-Dependent Proteostatic Stress Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory posits that the selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortical neurons to amyloid-β and tau pathology is governed by their baseline and stimulus-driven activity levels, which modulate proteostatic load, synaptic turnover, and metabolic stress. Neuronal populations with high sustained activity or plasticity are more prone to proteostatic failure and subsequent pathology, while those with lower activity or robust homeostatic mechanisms are more resistant.

• neuronal_population → has_high_baseline_or_evoked_activity → True
THEN

• neuronal_population → has_increased_vulnerability_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Highly active neurons accumulate more misfolded proteins and are more susceptible to tau pathology.
  2. Sensory stimulation increases amyloid deposition in corresponding cortical regions.
  3. Neuronal activity modulates APP processing and tau phosphorylation.

What is Novel: The explicit link between sustained activity, proteostatic stress, and selective vulnerability in the visual cortex is novel.

References:

  • Busche & Konnerth (2016) Impairments of neural circuit function in Alzheimer's disease [activity and pathology]

Statement 1: Homeostatic Plasticity Confers Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_robust_homeostatic_mechanisms → True
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with strong homeostatic plasticity maintain proteostasis and resist degeneration.
  2. Homeostatic scaling reduces synaptic stress and protein aggregation.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law applies general homeostatic principles to a new context of selective vulnerability.

What Already Exists: Homeostatic plasticity is known to protect against excitotoxicity and stress.

What is Novel: The prediction that homeostatic mechanisms specifically confer resistance to amyloid/tau pathology in the visual cortex is novel.

References:

  • Turrigiano (2012) Homeostatic synaptic plasticity: local and global mechanisms for stabilizing neuronal function [homeostatic plasticity]
  • Styr & Slutsky (2018) Imbalance between firing homeostasis and synaptic plasticity drives early-phase Alzheimer's pathology [homeostasis and AD]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Visual cortical regions with higher baseline activity will show earlier or more severe amyloid/tau pathology.
  2. Enhancing homeostatic plasticity in vulnerable populations will reduce pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing activity in resistant populations will induce vulnerability.
  2. Suppressing activity in vulnerable populations will confer resistance only if homeostatic mechanisms are intact.

Negative Experiments

  1. If activity levels do not correlate with vulnerability, the theory is challenged.
  2. If homeostatic plasticity enhancement does not confer resistance, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Microenvironmental factors (e.g., glial states, vascular features) may override activity-dependent effects.
