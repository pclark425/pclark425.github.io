Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4675

Name: Integrated Network Failure Theory of Alzheimer's Disease

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the cause of Alzheimer's disease and how it can be effectively detected.

Description: Alzheimer's disease arises from a systems-level breakdown in neural network homeostasis, driven by the convergence of proteostatic failure (amyloid and tau misfolding), chronic neuroinflammation, and vascular dysfunction. This network failure precedes and predicts clinical symptoms, and can be detected by multi-modal biomarkers reflecting these converging pathologies.

Statement 0: Convergent Pathology Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has_brain_amyloid_accumulation → True, and
• individual → has_brain_tau_pathology → True, and
• individual → has_chronic_neuroinflammation → True, and
• individual → has_cerebrovascular_dysfunction → True
THEN
• individual → has_high_risk_for_AD_network_failure → True

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Amyloid and tau pathologies are necessary but not sufficient for AD; neuroinflammation and vascular dysfunction are increasingly recognized as critical contributors.
  2. Multi-modal imaging and fluid biomarkers show that these pathologies often co-occur and interact before clinical symptoms.
  3. Network-level functional connectivity disruptions precede overt neurodegeneration.

ConditionDetails

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  2. Network-level imaging abnormalities (e.g., default mode network disruption) predict cognitive decline.

References:

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with convergent abnormalities in amyloid, tau, neuroinflammation, and vascular biomarkers will show accelerated network dysfunction and higher risk of cognitive decline.
  2. Multi-modal biomarker panels will outperform single-modality tests in predicting conversion from preclinical to symptomatic AD.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Interventions targeting all four pathologies simultaneously will be more effective at preventing AD than those targeting only amyloid or tau.
  2. Novel network-level biomarkers (e.g., connectomics) will detect AD risk before any single pathology is detectable.

Negative Experiments

  1. If individuals with all four pathologies do not develop network failure or AD, the theory is challenged.
  2. If multi-modal biomarker abnormalities do not predict preclinical network dysfunction, the law is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some individuals with significant amyloid and tau pathology remain cognitively normal for life.
  2. Rare genetic forms of AD may not follow the same multi-factorial convergence.
