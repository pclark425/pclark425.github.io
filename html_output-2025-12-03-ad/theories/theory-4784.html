Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4784

Name: Molecular Signature and Proteostasis Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortical neurons to amyloid-β and tau pathology is determined by their intrinsic molecular signatures, including expression of proteostasis-related genes, chaperones, and clearance pathways. Neurons with robust proteostasis machinery and efficient protein degradation pathways are resistant, while those with weak proteostasis or high expression of amyloidogenic/tau-prone isoforms are vulnerable.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostasis Vulnerability Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_proteostasis_capacity → low, and
• neuronal_population → expresses_amyloidogenic_or_tau_prone_isoforms → high
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_vulnerable_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Single-cell transcriptomics reveals that vulnerable neurons express lower levels of chaperones and proteasome components.
  2. Neurons with high expression of APP or tau isoforms prone to aggregation are more likely to develop pathology.
  3. Proteostasis impairment precedes and predicts regional vulnerability in Alzheimer's models.

Statement 1: Proteostasis Protection Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_proteostasis_capacity → high, and
• neuronal_population → has_protein_clearance_pathways → efficient
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → amyloid-β_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with high expression of chaperones, autophagy, and proteasome genes are less likely to accumulate amyloid-β and tau.
  2. Enhancing proteostasis in animal models reduces pathology and confers resistance.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law extends general proteostasis protection to a specific neuronal context.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis enhancement is protective in neurodegeneration models.

What is Novel: The explicit, conditional application to visual cortex and selective resistance is novel.

References:

  • Hetz & Saxena (2017) ER stress and the unfolded protein response in neurodegeneration [proteostasis and resistance]
  • Ciechanover & Kwon (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin-proteasome system in neurodegeneration [protein clearance and resistance]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Visual cortical neurons with high chaperone and proteasome gene expression will show reduced amyloid-β and tau pathology.
  2. Pharmacological enhancement of proteostasis in vulnerable neurons will confer resistance to pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Overexpression of specific chaperones in vulnerable visual cortical neurons will prevent pathology even in the presence of high amyloidogenic/tau-prone isoforms.
  2. Knockdown of proteostasis genes in resistant neurons will render them vulnerable to amyloid-β and tau pathology.

Negative Experiments

  1. If neurons with low proteostasis capacity do not develop pathology, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If enhancing proteostasis does not confer resistance, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some neurons with robust proteostasis still develop pathology, suggesting additional extrinsic factors are involved.
