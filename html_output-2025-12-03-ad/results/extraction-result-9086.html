Paper Title: Gravitational Lensing and Deflection Angle by generalised Ellis-Bronnikov wormhole Embedded in Warped Braneworld Background

Paper Abstract: Null-trajectories, deflection angle, and gravitational lensing are studied in the background spacetime of the generalised Ellis-Bronnikov wormholes and it's five dimensional version embedded in a warped background. We show that these wormholes can be distinguished through the study of gravitational lensing. In particular, the difference in deflection angles, radius of the Einstein rings, and the image positions are quantified by the wormhole parameters -- the steepness constant ($m\geq 2$), the throat radius, and `$\delta$' that characterizes the warping in extra dimension. We demonstrate our results analytically with $m=2$ cases and graphically otherwise.

I. INTRODUCTION

Wormholes create "short-cuts" that enable "apparently faster than light" movement between two distant spacetime sites [1][2][3].Although initially the concept was discussed with great hope [4,5], it was quickly discovered that the Einstein-Rosen "wormhole" is not a stable structure as was previously thought.The speed at which the wormhole opens and closes prevents even photons from "traveling" through it.A wormhole may be supported by exotic matter [6], albeit it's not yet known if such configurations are physically possible.The presence of negative energy density makes them challenging to produce in macroscopic quantities [3].The initial purpose may have faded, but the Einstein-Rosen bridge is nevertheless mentioned on occasion as a practical answer to the perplexing problem of interplanetary travel [2,7].Many people believe that traversable wormholes are more science fiction than science.In classical general relativity, they are prohibited by the average null energy condition.Thus, within the paradigm of general relativity, wormholes are not real if energy conditions are strictly followed [1,2,8,9].Note that quantum fluctuations allow for the formation of tiny wormholes [10][11][12].However, they are insufficient to create macroscopic wormholes.

Exotic matter that violates energy conditions can be avoided classically [13][14][15][16].One approach is to use modified gravity theories as a framework.There are numerous modified gravity models with non-exotic matter [17][18][19][20][21][22][23].However, in some circumstances, the convergence criterion of null geodesics is violated.Using the models of dynamical wormholes [24][25][26][27][28] violations of the energy conditions could be avoided.The f (R) and higher order gravity theories [29][30][31][32] are common modified gravity theories that analyze wormhole geometries using a feasible matter source.Recent studies have successfully modeled and analyzed wormholes under Born-Infeld gravity [33][34][35] and torsional gravity [36][37][38] that satisfy energy conditions.

Various circumstances, including gravitational lensing [39][40][41], have distinct physical signatures.Advances in gravitational wave astronomy [42][43][44] and black hole imaging [45] have opened up the possibility of the search for wormhole signatures.Recently, the idea of astrophysical wormholes in dark matter galaxy haloes has been raised [46,47].

Wormholes, also known as black hole mimickers, have a distinct signature that can be imprinted during mergers or quasi-normal modes [48][49][50][51][52].These signatures, if detected, would support modified theories of gravity above general relativity.This article explores lensing properties of a recently introduced model of a wormhole embedded in a warped five-dimensional thick braneworld.Previous research has focused on wormholes within the Kaluza-Klein, DGP, and Randal-Sundrum thin braneworld scenarios [53][54][55][56][57][58].

Extra dimensions have been discussed in the literature for nearly a century, but have yet to be confirmed through experiments [59,60].This notion has remained popular due to the benefits it provide.For example, when creating models of unification, such as superstrings [61], or addressing the age-old hierarchy problem [62,63].Using octonions to reinterpret the standard model has led to extra dimensions naturally [64][65][66][67][68][69].Theories of extra dimensions are based on fundamental physical symmetries and not just extensions of existing theories.Warped braneworld models [70][71][72] are a prominent type of higher-dimensional model.This model has a nonfactorizable geometry and a curved five-dimensional spacetime.The geometry of the four-dimensional section is affected by the extra dimension via a warping factor, which is unique to this type of model.

Recently embedding of a four-dimensional wormhole in a static five-dimensional warped geometry has been considered [73].Numerical construction of such 5D geometry with a cosmological thick brane in the presence of the both growing and decaying warp factor is given earlier in [74].The wormhole family used for embedding is based on [75], which extends the Ellis-Bronnikov (EB) spacetime [76,77] to give a generalized family (GEB) of spacetimes that partially satisfy the energy conditions.

The GEB wormhole geometry embedded in a five-dimensional warped background, demonstrate that a diminishing warp factor leads to minimisation of violation of the weak energy conditions even for the EB geometry, while improving all other energy conditions in comparison to the four-dimensional case.Earlier we have analysed timelike trajectories and evolution of geodesic congruences in this background [78,79].

In continuation, in the present work, we study the null trajectories and gravitational lensing around the GEB wormholes and the resulting distinguishing features that differentiate the 4-D GEB from its warped 5-D counter parts.In Sec II, we briefly review the warped GEB (WGEB) model.Comparative studies of Null-Trajectories in 4D-GEB and 5D-WGEB models are presented in Sec III.Deflection of light rays and gravitational lensing by different species are studied and thereby distinguished respectively in the Sec.IV and Sec.V. Finally, we summarize our results in Sec.VI.

VI. DISCUSSION

In this work we discussed deflection of light rays and gravitational lensing of the four dimensional generalised Ellis-Bronnikov wormholes and the five dimensional warped spacetime that embeds a 4D-GEB wormhole.Our goal is to find various distinguishing features of the two models that can help one to identify signature of a 4D or 5D wormhole.4D-GEB models were introduced as an improvement on the classic EB spacetimes regarding violation of energy conditions.It was shown recently that the warped nature provides even further improvement over 4D-EB spacetimes.

Thus our primary goal was to compare 5D-WEB with 4D-EB.However, for the sake of completeness we have analysed 4D-GEB and 5D-WGEB scenarios in general.Below we summarise our results in a systematic way.

• Null geodesics with angular momentum larger than certain values (determined by wormhole parameters and impact parameter) will return before reaching the neck and will acquire a net deflection angle.

• This trajectories will whirl around before reaching the point of return and whirl back thereafter.Number of windings increases with increasing angular momentum.

• Some of the trajectories may come back to the point of it's origin thus can be made to interfere with itself revealing information about wormhole parameters.

Implications of such interference phenomenon will be explored elsewhere.

• We found analytic expression for deflection angle for m = 2 case that corresponds to 4D-EB and 5D-WEB scenario.Deflection angles for generalised models (m > 2) are found numerically.

• Graphical demonstrations reveal the distinguishing features of wormhole parameters and extra dimension.Naturally, for a given steepness constant m, it is the ratio l 0 /b 0 , that quantifies relative strength of two length scales one coming from the kinetic properties of the photon and other from the wormhole, decides the nature of the deflection angle.From Fig. 1 we note that the deflection angle reduces for 5D-WGEB models in comparison to the 4D-GEB models for lower values of l 0 /b 0 .

• Since the gravitational lensing formula is directly connected to the deflection angle, the 4D-GEB and 5D-WGEB wormholes can be further distinguished through the study of gravitational lensing.In particular, the radius of the Einstein ring and the angular positions of images are affected by the steepness constant m as well as the constant δ arising from the extra dimension.We restrict ourselves only in the study of weak gravitational lensing and the primary image positions.For m = 2 we obtain analytical formula for the Einstein radius for 4D-EB and 5D-WEB nodels and we note that for 5D case, the Einstein radius reduces.The study of strong gravitational lensing could be more effective to distinguish the effect of extra dimension.We leave it for future work.
