Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-45

Name: Multifactorial Model of Organotropism Incorporating Anatomical, Exosomal/PMN and Tumor-Intrinsic Programs

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the best explanation for preferential metastatic organotropism of different cancers, based on the following results.

Description: Preferential metastatic organotropism emerges from the dynamic integration of three major determinants: (1) anatomical and hemodynamic factors (route of venous/lymphatic drainage, mechanical trapping); (2) exosome/PMN-driven microenvironmental conditioning (as above); and (3) tumor-intrinsic molecular programs (epigenetic, transcriptional, and functional plasticity that enable adaptation to specific niches, including neuronal/ECM mimicry, stemness, nuclear mechanics, and EMT status). The combined, context-specific output of these interacting axes specifies the eventual pattern and efficiency of metastasis to particular organs.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Anatomical/Vascular Route Determinant Law

Statement: Venous and lymphatic drainage patterns, and capillary bed architecture, set the first-level probability for organotropic metastatic seeding through mechanical trapping of circulating tumor cells (CTCs), particularly for organs like liver and lung with large first-pass blood supply or connections (e.g., via portal vein or Batson's plexus).

Domain/Scope: Applies broadly to all cancers where mechanical trapping/circulation determines early phase DTC/CTC arrest; especially relevant to colorectal-to-liver, rectum-to-lung, and prostate-to-spine patterns.

Special Cases

  1. Circulatory pattern is necessary but not sufficient: microenvironmental compatibility must also exist.
  2. Anatomical shunts, vascular diseases (e.g., portal hypertension), or surgical alterations can modify expected patterns.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Autopsy, clinical, and epidemiological data demonstrate dominant seeding patterns explained by anatomy (e.g., colorectal cancer to liver via portal drainage, prostate to spine via Batson's plexus, frequency matching venous plumbing). [e175.2] [e173.6]
  2. SEER and pathology studies provide frequency data matching venous routes (higher liver metastasis from proximal colon/rectum, higher lung from distal rectum/anus). [e175.2]

Statement 1: Tumor-Intrinsic Organ Adaptive Program Law

Statement: Metastatic colonization and outgrowth within a specific organ requires that disseminated tumor cells possess, acquire, or reactivate context-appropriate transcriptional and/or epigenomic programs—including but not limited to: (a) stem-cell–like (WNT, HIPPO) and metabolic adaptation for liver; (b) neuronal/ECM mimicry and cell–cell junction adaptability in brain; (c) nuclear/cytoskeletal mechanics (e.g., LMNA downregulation) for tissues with high mechanical or confinement stress (e.g., liver); (d) EMT plasticity or osteomimicry for bone; often selected through in vivo lineage pressures or microenvironmental induction.

Domain/Scope: Applies primarily to the outgrowth phase of metastatic colonization (i.e., successful DTCs following arrest and initial survival) in all solid tumor organotropism contexts (liver, brain, bone, lung, etc.).

Special Cases

  1. Some tumor types (e.g., SCLC, melanoma) display especially prominent transcriptional and epigenomic reprogramming; other tumors rely more heavily on pre-configured adaptation.
  2. Context-dependence: PMN and local selection pressures can induce or repress adaptive programs.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. SCLC lines derived from liver metastases upregulate WNT-beta catenin and HIPPO stemness/metabolic programs, as well as show chromatin accessibility signatures for liver adaptation (RNA-seq, ATAC-seq); LMNA loss increases nuclear deformability and liver colonization specifically. [e170.2] [e170.1]
  2. SCLC and breast cancer lines from brain metastases upregulate neuronal identity and cell–cell/ECM junction programs, co-opting synaptic and astrocyte interactions required for brain colonization. [e170.3] [e176.10] [e234.1] [e234.7]
  3. Breast cancer bone-tropic cells activate osteomimicry, osteolytic or osteoblastic feedback programs (e.g., PTHrP, RANKL, etc.) for bone adaptation. [e234.2] [e173.0] [e234.3]
  4. Epigenetic expansion in VHL-mutant RCC enables HIF output and metastatic activity across multiple sites. [e234.8]

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Surgical rerouting of venous drainage (e.g., during colorectal surgery) will shift initial patterns of metastasis (e.g., raise lung over liver seeding).
  2. CRISPR or RNAi knockdown of critical organ-adaptive genes (e.g., stemness regulators, WNT/HIPPO, neuronal markers, LMNA) in matching tumor cells will abolish the ability to colonize and grow within the respective organs in animal models.
  3. Lineage tracing will show that the majority of overt metastases arise from subclones with pre-existing or rapidly plastic organ-adaptive gene expression.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Reprogramming DTCs with non-endogenous adaptive programs (e.g., overexpression of 'wrong' adaptation genes) will induce ectopic (non-canonical) organ colonization when combined with appropriate PMN conditions.
  2. Single-cell omics trajectory mapping will reveal that microenvironmental signals (e.g., PMN chemokines, ECM cues) induce rapid switching of tumor cell identity at the point of extravasation.
  3. Blocking multiple axes (anatomical trapping, PMN, and adaptation) simultaneously will have a supra-additive effect in preventing organ-specific metastasis compared to any single-axis intervention.

Negative Experiments

  1. Inhibition of all organ adaptation programs (e.g., LMNA, WNT, stemness) in tumor cells has no effect on metastatic organotropism—contradicting necessity.
  2. Human tumor samples from patients with unusual or ectopic metastatic spread will not show congruent adaptation programs relative to their host organ.
  3. Altering anatomical drainage routes does not affect organ-seeding distribution—contradicting anatomical determinant law.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Situations where rare, stochastic events or co-migrating fusion hybrids (e.g., tumor–macrophage hybrids) impart new organ tropism not predicted by anatomy, PMN, or classic adaptive programs. [e175.4] [e175.5] [e175.8]
