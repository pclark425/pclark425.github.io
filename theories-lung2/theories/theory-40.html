Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-40

Name: Dual-Code Organotropism: EV 'zip-codes' write organ-specific programs that match (and amplify) seed traits

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of preferential metastatic organotropism (non-random metastatic spread to specific organs) across cancers (e.g., prostate/breast to bone, lung to brain/adrenals), based on the following results.

Description: Organotropism reflects a two-layer code. Layer 1 is an EV surface-address code (integrins/adhesion determinants) that biases which organ and which resident cell type receives the signal. Layer 2 is an EV cargo instruction code (miRNAs/proteins/lncRNAs) that reprograms that recipient into an organ-specific premetastatic niche (PMN) state—immune recruitment/suppression, ECM deposition, vascular remodeling, metabolic diversion—thereby amplifying selection for compatible disseminating tumor cells. Organ preference arises when (address code × instruction code) yields maximal niche payoff for a given tumor's phenotypic repertoire.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: EV address code law (surface determinants drive recipient/organ uptake bias)

Statement: If tumor-derived EVs display specific surface integrin/adhesion profiles, then EV biodistribution and uptake will be biased toward organs whose resident cells/ECM provide matching ligands, and this biased uptake precedes and predicts site-specific PMN formation and subsequent organotropic metastasis.

Domain/Scope: Tumor EVs in systemic circulation in preclinical and clinical contexts where EV surface profiling and organ uptake can be measured; applies to lung/liver/brain examples where exosomal integrins are associated with organ tropism and to studies showing preferential organ accumulation of tumor EVs.

Special Cases

  1. Uptake bias may be modulated by vascular permeability and organ macrophage filtering (e.g., Kupffer cells).
  2. Clinical associations may reflect tumor burden rather than causality unless supported by mechanistic perturbation.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Exosomal integrin 'zip codes' summarized: α6β4/α6β1 associate with lung tropism; αvβ5 with liver tropism; integrin β3/αvβ3 (or related) implicated in brain tropism in reviews and clinical association. [e158.5] [e163.0] [e160.4] [e165.0] [e162.0] [e159.0]
  2. Melanoma exosomes injected IV preferentially accumulate in organs common for melanoma metastasis (lung, spleen, bone marrow, liver), consistent with non-random EV organ homing. [e165.1]
  3. Lung-cancer-derived exosomes localize around cerebral vessels and are preferentially taken up by brain endothelium after systemic delivery (demonstrated in vivo). [e157.1] [e157.0]
  4. Clinical association: circulating exosomal integrin β3 levels in lung cancer patients with brain metastases correlate with intracranial failure/survival after WBRT (association consistent with an EV address signature linked to brain disease course). [e163.9]

Statement 1: EV instruction code law (cargo programs niche modules)

Statement: If EVs are taken up by the targeted organ’s recipient cells, then EV cargo (miRNAs/proteins/lncRNAs) induces recipient-cell transcriptional and phenotypic programs that constitute a PMN module (immune recruitment/suppression, ECM remodeling, barrier remodeling, metabolic diversion), and perturbing the key cargo or recipient response abrogates the pro-metastatic organ-specific effect.

Domain/Scope: Systems with demonstrated EV uptake by specific organ-resident recipients and measurable downstream niche changes; applies to lung (AEC II), liver (Kupffer/stellate/LSEC), brain (BMEC/microglia), peritoneum (mesothelium), and macrophage-mediated modules in lung/liver.

Special Cases

  1. Different EV cargos can converge on common host pathways (e.g., PTEN→AKT→NF-κB; TGF-β→fibronectin), producing similar niche states from distinct tumors.
  2. Host-derived EVs (bone-marrow EVs) can also provide the instruction code, not only tumor EVs.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Engineering EVs with a liver-address integrin profile but lung-instruction cargo (e.g., miR-200b-3p-like program) will preferentially deliver the lung-like immune program to liver recipients and partially shift metastatic patterns toward liver immune-gated colonization.
  2. In lung cancer brain metastasis models, blocking BMEC Dkk-1 secretion (endothelial-specific knockdown) will restore microglial M1 markers and reduce transendothelial migration in vitro and reduce brain colonization in vivo.
  3. In gastric cancer, inhibiting ERK signaling in pulmonary macrophages will reduce PD-L1 induction and reduce lung colonization specifically in the setting of miR-92a-3p-high EV exposure.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Personalized EV-code panels (integrins + cargo miRNAs/proteins) will outperform tumor genomics alone in predicting first metastatic organ across heterogeneous patient cohorts; may fail if EV heterogeneity and clearance dominate signal.
  2. Therapeutic EV 're-addressing': administering engineered 'sink' EVs that competitively occupy organ address ligands without delivering instruction cargo will reduce PMN formation and metastasis; could backfire by triggering inflammation.

Negative Experiments

  1. Show that EV integrin profile does not predict organ uptake when cargo is controlled (or vice versa), contradicting separable address and instruction codes.
  2. Demonstrate that recipient-cell-type specificity (AEC II vs endothelium vs macrophage) does not change downstream niche programming given the same EV input.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Fusion-hybrid-mediated organotropism can occur without explicit EV dependence in the cited hybrid injection experiments; the two-code EV model does not directly explain hybrid advantages unless combined with organ admission control or hybrid intrinsic traits. [e161.1] [e161.2]
