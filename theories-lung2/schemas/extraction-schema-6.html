Extraction Schema Details for extraction-schema-6

Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-6

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of factors that influence why only some cigarette smokers develop lung cancer, including genetic, environmental, behavioral, and biological mechanisms, as well as study results comparing smokers who develop lung cancer to those who do not.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
study_type str The type of study (e.g., case-control, cohort, genome-wide association study, mechanistic study, review, meta-analysis).
population_description str Description of the study population, including sample size, demographics (age, sex, ethnicity), and smoking status.
risk_factors_identified str List and brief description of risk factors found to increase lung cancer risk among smokers (e.g., genetic variants, duration/intensity of smoking, co-exposures, family history).
protective_factors_identified str List and brief description of protective factors or mechanisms that may reduce lung cancer risk among smokers (e.g., genetic resistance, efficient DNA repair, lifestyle factors).
genetic_findings str Summary of any genetic findings related to susceptibility or resistance to lung cancer among smokers (e.g., specific gene variants, GWAS results, heritability estimates).
behavioral_modifiers str Description of behavioral factors that modify risk (e.g., age of smoking initiation, quitting, type of tobacco, inhalation depth).
environmental_modifiers str Description of environmental or occupational exposures that modify risk (e.g., radon, asbestos, air pollution).
biological_mechanisms str Summary of biological mechanisms proposed or identified (e.g., differences in metabolism of carcinogens, DNA repair capacity, immune response).
quantitative_results str Key quantitative results (e.g., odds ratios, relative risks, p-values) comparing smokers who develop lung cancer to those who do not.
counterexamples_or_exceptions str Any notable counterexamples or exceptions (e.g., heavy smokers who do not develop lung cancer, light smokers who do).
study_limitations str Any limitations of the study as reported by the authors (e.g., sample size, confounding, generalizability).
