Extraction Schema Details for extraction-schema-9

Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-9

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of mechanisms, models, explanations, and supporting or refuting evidence for why specific cancers preferentially metastasize to particular organs (metastatic organotropism), including details for cancer type, primary and metastatic sites, molecular or microenvironmental factors, and any quantitative or experimental evidence.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
cancer_type str The type of cancer or primary tumor discussed (e.g., prostate cancer, breast cancer, lung cancer).
primary_site str The anatomical site of the primary tumor (e.g., prostate, breast, lung).
preferred_metastatic_organ str The organ(s) to which the cancer preferentially metastasizes (e.g., bone, brain, adrenal gland, liver, lung).
proposed_mechanism str A concise description of the proposed mechanism(s) or explanation(s) for the observed organotropism (e.g., molecular pathways, chemokine/receptor interactions, microenvironmental factors, anatomical routes).
supporting_evidence str A brief summary of the key experimental, clinical, or molecular evidence supporting the proposed mechanism (e.g., animal models, patient data, in vitro studies, molecular profiling).
counter_evidence_or_alternative_explanations str Any reported counter-evidence, limitations, or alternative explanations for the observed organotropism.
key_molecules_or_factors str Names of key molecules, genes, pathways, or microenvironmental factors implicated in the mechanism (e.g., CXCR4, SDF-1, integrins, exosomes, bone microenvironment).
experimental_model str The type of experimental model or system used to study the mechanism (e.g., mouse model, cell culture, patient-derived xenograft, clinical cohort).
quantitative_data str Any quantitative data reported (e.g., frequency of metastasis to specific organs, rates, percentages, statistical significance).
limitations_or_open_questions str Any limitations of the study or open questions highlighted by the authors regarding organotropism.
