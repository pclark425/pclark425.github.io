Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-7

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of cancer types and their preferential metastatic sites, including the proposed mechanisms, molecular factors, and experimental evidence for why specific cancers metastasize to specific organs.

Extraction Schema (Details)

Field Name Type Description
primary_cancer_type str The type of primary cancer being studied (e.g. 'breast cancer', 'prostate cancer', 'lung cancer', 'melanoma', etc.)
metastatic_site str The specific organ or tissue site where metastasis occurs (e.g. 'bone', 'brain', 'liver', 'lung', 'adrenal gland', etc.)
metastatic_frequency str How common is metastasis to this site for this cancer type? Include percentages or frequency data if available. (null if no information)
proposed_mechanism str What mechanism is proposed to explain this organotropism? (e.g. 'seed and soil hypothesis', 'chemokine receptor interactions', 'vascular architecture', 'pre-metastatic niche formation', 'adhesion molecule expression', etc.)
cancer_cell_factors str What specific molecular factors, receptors, or properties of the cancer cells contribute to organotropism? (e.g. 'CXCR4 expression', 'integrin expression', 'specific gene signatures', etc.)
target_organ_factors str What specific molecular factors, properties, or characteristics of the target organ make it receptive to metastasis? (e.g. 'CXCL12 secretion', 'specific ECM components', 'vascular permeability', 'growth factors', etc.)
experimental_evidence str What experimental evidence supports this mechanism? Describe the key experiments and findings. (e.g. 'knockout of CXCR4 reduced bone metastasis by 80%', 'in vivo imaging showed...', etc.)
contradictory_evidence str Is there any evidence that contradicts or challenges this mechanism? (null if no information)
therapeutic_implications str Are there any therapeutic strategies mentioned that target this organotropism mechanism? (null if no information)
other_contributing_factors str Are there other contributing factors mentioned, such as hemodynamic factors, immune evasion, metabolic requirements, or anatomical considerations? (null if no information)
