contradictory_evidence The review notes heterogeneous outcomes after thymectomy: while thymectomy can reduce anti-AChR titers and improve disease, thymectomy has also been followed by appearance or exacerbation of other autoimmune diseases (e.g., SLE) in some cases, indicating that interventions altering thymic tissue can have complex effects.
patient_demographics Okumura et al. cohort: majority female; no sex differences were documented between thymoma-associated MG and non-thymoma MG in that study; thymoma–MG association reported across a wide age range including infancy (case report).
interventions_modulating_autoimmunity Thymectomy: reported to reduce anti-AChR antibody titers and can improve MG; pre-thymectomy anti-AChR levels may predict post-thymectomy MG risk (reported by Okumura et al.).
key_findings Strong, repeatedly observed association between thymoma and MG supported by histology (immature thymocytes, germinal centers), immunology (anti-AChR antibodies), and clinical response to thymectomy; certain thymoma histotypes (B1/B2) enriched for immature double-positive thymocytes implicated in defective negative selection.
limitations Evidence is largely observational and correlative (case reports, cohort observations, histology); mechanistic causality is not definitively proven in experimental models within this review; heterogeneity of outcomes after thymectomy indicates incomplete understanding.
