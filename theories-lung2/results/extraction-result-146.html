proposed_mechanism Combined mechanism: (1) general mediators that promote extravasation through non-fenestrated capillaries (shared with lung), and (2) brain-specific mediators that enable adhesion to brain endothelium and passage through the BBB; overall framed as 'specialized functions for breaching the BBB' (organ-specific extravasation plus subsequent colonization).
cancer_cell_factors COX2 (PTGS2), EGFR ligands (HBEGF, EREG), ST6GALNAC5 (α2,6-sialyltransferase), MMP1, ANGPTL4, LTBP1, FSCN1, RARRES3 (mentioned). COX2 and EGFR ligands promote extravasation; ST6GALNAC5 promotes adhesion to brain endothelial cells via altered cell-surface sialylation and enhances BBB transmigration.
target_organ_factors Non-fenestrated brain capillary endothelium with tight junctions and astrocyte foot processes (BBB); brain-restricted glycan-recognition context (targets for α2,6-sialylated ligands); factors regulating vascular permeability (e.g., prostaglandin signaling via COX2) and TGF-β–induced ANGPTL4-mediated endothelial junction disruption are discussed as permissive cues.
experimental_evidence Key experiments from the paper: (a) In vivo selection produced brain-homing derivatives (BrM1→BrM2) from CN34 and MDA-MB-231 lines that formed multifocal brain lesions and lodged in brain capillaries within 24 h. (b) Genome-wide expression identified a 17-gene brain metastasis signature (BrMS) associated with clinical brain relapse. (c) Functional perturbation: RNAi/shRNA knockdown of COX2 reduced brain metastatic activity of BrM2 cells in mice; treatment of mice with cetuximab (anti-EGFR) reduced brain metastasis. (d) In vitro human BBB model (primary brain endothelial cells + astrocytes producing a tight, albumin-impermeable barrier): BrM2 cells showed 3-4× higher transmigration than parental cells; COX2 knockdown or EGFR blockade (cetuximab) inhibited transmigration; combined knockdown of COX2, HBEGF and EREG further reduced transmigration. (e) ST6GALNAC5: expression was dramatically upregulated in BrM2 derivatives (MDA231: ~30×; CN34: >100×; other pleural isolates similarly elevated). SNA lectin staining (binds α2,6-linked sialic acid) was strong in BrM2-derived tumours and in 50% (6/12) of human breast-cancer brain metastasis samples versus 18% (2/11) of lung metastases. ST6GALNAC5 knockdown decreased adhesion to brain endothelial monolayers, reduced BBB transmigration to baseline, and decreased brain metastatic activity in mice; conversely, ectopic ST6GALNAC5 expression in an aggressive lung-colonizing LM2 line increased BBB transmigration and brain infiltration (more micrometastases). (f) ST6GALNAC5 knockdown did not affect in vitro growth, primary tumour growth, or lung seeding by CN34-BrM2 cells, indicating a brain-specific role.
contradictory_evidence null
therapeutic_implications EGFR blockade (cetuximab) reduced brain metastasis in mouse models; COX2 pathway implicated (suggesting COX2/prostaglandin pathway inhibitors could modulate extravasation); targeting cell-surface sialylation or ST6GALNAC5-mediated interactions is proposed as a potential therapeutic approach to disrupt BBB infiltration.
other_contributing_factors Anatomical/vascular differences (non-fenestrated brain capillaries vs fenestrated bone marrow/liver capillaries) and parenchymal composition (presence of astrocytes, reactive glia) are highlighted; reactive gliosis observed around lesions; temporal dynamics (breast cancer often has long latency before brain relapse) suggest selection/evolution of organ-specific traits over time.
