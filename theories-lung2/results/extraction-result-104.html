Paper Title: Synaptic clustering during development and learning: the why, when, and how

Paper Venue: Frontiers in Molecular Neuroscience

Paper TL;DR: New insights are discussed and an overview of the candidate plasticity mechanisms that could be involved in synaptic clustering during development and learning in different neuronal circuits are given.

Paper Abstract: To contribute to a functional network a neuron must make specific connections and integrate the synaptic inputs that it receives in a meaningful way. Previous modeling and experimental studies have predicted that this specificity could entail a subcellular organization whereby synapses that carry similar information are clustered together on local stretches of dendrite. Recent imaging studies have now, for the first time, demonstrated synaptic clustering during development and learning in different neuronal circuits. Interestingly, this organization is dependent on synaptic activity and most likely involves local plasticity mechanisms. Here we discuss these new insights and give an overview of the candidate plasticity mechanisms that could be involved.
