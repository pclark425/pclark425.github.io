Paper Title: Triplets of Spikes in a Model of Spike Timing-Dependent Plasticity

Paper Venue: Journal of Neuroscience

Paper TL;DR: A triplet rule is examined, a rule which considers sets of three spikes and is possible to fit experimental data from visual cortical slices as well as from hippocampal cultures and can be mapped to a Bienenstock–Cooper–Munro learning rule.

Paper Abstract: Classical experiments on spike timing-dependent plasticity (STDP) use a protocol based on pairs of presynaptic and postsynaptic spikes repeated at a given frequency to induce synaptic potentiation or depression. Therefore, standard STDP models have expressed the weight change as a function of pairs of presynaptic and postsynaptic spike. Unfortunately, those paired-based STDP models cannot account for the dependence on the repetition frequency of the pairs of spike. Moreover, those STDP models cannot reproduce recent triplet and quadruplet experiments. Here, we examine a triplet rule (i.e., a rule which considers sets of three spikes, i.e., two pre and one post or one pre and two post) and compare it to classical pair-based STDP learning rules. With such a triplet rule, it is possible to fit experimental data from visual cortical slices as well as from hippocampal cultures. Moreover, when assuming stochastic spike trains, the triplet learning rule can be mapped to a Bienenstock–Cooper–Munro learning rule.

synaptic_rule_description Weight updates: on presynaptic spike w → w - o1(t)[A2- + A3- r2(t-ε)] and on postsynaptic spike w → w + r1(t)[A2+ + A3+ o2(t-ε)] (Eqs. 3–4). Slow homeostatic averaging of parameters (BCM-like threshold) is suggested to occur over ≈10 minutes or more.
brain_region_circuit Fitted and tested against hippocampal cultured neurons (Wang et al., 2005 data: pair, triplet, quadruplet protocols) and visual cortex slice data (Sjöström et al., 2001 pairing-frequency dependence). learning_type Associative, timing-dependent Hebbian learning that supports input selectivity and synaptic competition (BCM-like behavior) and accounts for nonlinear multispike integration.

synaptic_rule_name BCM (Bienenstock–Cooper–Munro) mapping
synaptic_rule_description Analytical expectation (Eq. 6): ⟨dw/dt⟩ = -A2- τ- ρ_x ρ_y - A3- τ- τ_x ρ_x ρ_y^2 + A2+ τ+ ρ_x ρ_y + A3+ τ+ τ_y ρ_x ρ_y^2. If A3- = 0 (minimal triplet), this becomes φ(ρ_y,θ)ρ_x consistent with BCM, where the threshold θ is proportional to long-time average ⟨ρ_y^p⟩ (p>1) — operationally implemented by allowing A2± to depend on ⟨ρ_y^p⟩ computed by low-pass filtering over ~10 minutes.
temporal_scale Fast spike statistics map to rate terms (instantaneous rates ρ_x, ρ_y). The sliding threshold θ depends on slow averaging of ⟨ρ_y^p⟩ performed with a time constant on the order of ≈10 minutes or longer (explicitly discussed by authors).
brain_region_circuit The mapping is general; authors illustrate functional consequences (synaptic competition, input selectivity) with the same hippocampal/visual-cortex–motivated parameters and a 100-afferent feedforward simulation.
connectivity_pattern Feedforward multi-afferent scenario used for illustration (100 inputs with shifting Gaussian drive) demonstrating competitive specialization (receptive-field formation) under BCM-like dynamics.
learning_type Competitive Hebbian learning leading to input selectivity and receptive-field development (rate-based), bridging spike-timing plasticity and rate-based theories.

multi_timescale_interaction Fast (ms) triplet/pair spike interactions generate rate-dependent terms; slow (≈10 min) averaging of postsynaptic firing rate yields a sliding threshold that controls sign and magnitude of plasticity, enabling stable long-term competition.
