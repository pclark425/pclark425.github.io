Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Adjuvant extension beyond 1 year will not materially improve DMFS compared with 1-year standard in stage III disease but will increase cumulative toxicity.
  2. Neoadjuvant response-adapted strategies (pCR/MPR) that de-escalate adjuvant therapy will achieve RFS comparable to or better than upfront surgery + 1-year adjuvant in randomized settings (as NADINA suggests).
  3. In metastatic practice, adopting PET/biopsy/ctDNA to guide early stop will reduce total PD-1 exposure by ≥6 months in ≥30% of treated patients without loss of 2-year PFS.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Adjuvant ctDNA-negative status after surgery may allow shorter adjuvant PD-1 (<1 year) without loss of RFS in selected patients.
  2. A 6-month adjuvant course in ultra-high-risk stage III with negative ctDNA may be non-inferior to 12 months; requires randomized validation.
