Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Short-cycle neoadjuvant regimens with early imaging (e.g., after 1 dose) and biomarker gating will minimize missed-surgery risk while preserving EFS benefit.
  2. In cohorts where TLND is omitted upfront, adjuvant-only PD-1 will still underperform neoadjuvant+adjuvant PD-1 due to loss of antigen load at therapy initiation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Node-sparing surgical approaches combined with neoadjuvant priming will further improve immune breadth and reduce morbidity without compromising control.
  2. Perioperative sequencing that preserves DLN (e.g., index-node biopsy only) may enhance priming versus immediate TLND while maintaining oncologic safety.

Negative Experiments

  1. Showing equal magnitude of systemic T-cell priming (clonal breadth, effector states) when PD-1 is started after TLND versus before would challenge the DLN-priming premise.
  2. Demonstrating that early events (progression/recurrence) are not reduced by neoadjuvant PD-1 in a matched-risk cohort would refute the kinetic sterilization component.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Direct human measurements quantifying DLN cross-priming differences (pre- vs post-TLND) are limited.
