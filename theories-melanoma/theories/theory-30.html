Description: The optimal anti-PD-1 duration in metastatic melanoma is a function of response depth and biomarker conversion, balancing marginal disease-control gains against cumulative toxicity and cost. CR generally requires 6–12 months total exposure; PR can stop at ~12 months if PET/ctDNA-converted, otherwise benefit accrues to ~18–24 months; SD rarely meets safe-stop criteria before 24 months without biomarker conversion. Beyond ~24 months, incremental benefit is small relative to rising late irAEs and cost.

Theory (Additional Details)

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. A PR cohort selected by PET CMR and ctDNA negativity at ~12 months will have noninferior 24-month off-therapy PFS compared with CR stoppers (difference ≤5% absolute).
  2. SD patients who achieve PET CMR and ctDNA negativity between 12–24 months will have ≥70% 24-month off-therapy PFS; those without conversion will have ≤50%.
  3. Stopping at 24 months among PET/ctDNA-converted PR/SD will not reduce 3-year OS versus continuing, but will reduce late irAE incidence by ≥15% absolute.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A minority of PET CMR, ctDNA-negative SD at 12 months can safely stop with close surveillance, especially if baseline tumor metabolic volume was low.
  2. ctDNA clearance kinetics (half-life/time-to-undetectable) will refine the duration windows and predict need for 18 vs 24 months in PR.
  3. Granzyme-B PET could substitute for FDG-PET in inflamed lesions to better discriminate true residual disease, enabling earlier safe stopping.

Negative Experiments

  1. If PET/ctDNA-selected PRs stopping at 12 months show >20% 24-month relapse, early stop is too permissive for PR and window must shift later.
  2. If continuing beyond 24 months post-conversion reduces 3-year OS by >5% absolute (i.e., harms accrue from stopping), the cap needs reevaluation.
  3. If SD patients with PET/ctDNA conversion still relapse >30% by 24 months off therapy, SD may require alternative strategies rather than duration alone.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Economic endpoints (ICER/QALYs) are not quantified here but are planned in DANTE and STOP-GAP; actual cost-effectiveness thresholds may refine duration windows. [e87.11] [e87.6] [e85.4] [e87.8]
