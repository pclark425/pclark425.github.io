Paper Title: Personalized response-directed surgery and adjuvant therapy after neoadjuvant ipilimumab and nivolumab in high-risk stage III melanoma: the PRADO trial

Paper Venue: Nature Medicine

Paper TL;DR: Results from the PRADO extension cohort of the OpACIN-neo trial show that pathologic response rate to neoadjuvant ipilimumab and nivolumab can be used as a criterion for personalization of further treatment in stage III nodal melanoma, with the potential to reduce treatment morbidity and increase patient quality of life.

follow_up_duration_median Median follow-up 28.1 months (IQR 25.0–33.8), minimum follow-up for patients alive 23.4 months.
immune_correlates_summary Baseline tumor PD-L1 tumor proportion score correlated with pathologic response (pRR 56% if PD-L1 <1%, 92% if 1–50%, 100% if >50%; P = 0.004). The paper cites preclinical and earlier clinical data supporting superior induction of anti-tumor immunity with neoadjuvant CPI but provides limited mechanistic new data in this report.
peripheral_immune_findings Not reported in the main manuscript (no data on peripheral TCR expansion, cytokines or blood immune subsets presented).
intratumoral_immune_findings PD-L1 expression in baseline tumor biopsies associated with pathologic response as above. No additional tumor immune microenvironment signatures (e.g., IFN-γ gene signatures) are reported in the main text.
tcr_repertoire_changes Not reported in this report; authors refer to planned/ongoing genetic and transcriptomic investigations comparing ILN and recurrent nodes.
antigen_spreading_or_epitope_spreading Not reported.
biomarker_predictors Baseline PD-L1 tumor proportion score (see PD-L1 association above). Presence of ≥2 PET-positive lymph nodes at baseline was enriched among the MPR patients who later recurred (all four MPR relapses had ≥2 PET-positive nodes and BRAF V600E). No TMB, TCR or IFN-signature data presented here.
mechanism_claims_by_authors Authors state neoadjuvant ipilimumab + nivolumab induces high pathologic response rates and that pathologic response is strongly associated with prolonged RFS; they cite preclinical and early clinical data suggesting neoadjuvant CPI produces superior anti-tumor immunity and survival benefit compared to adjuvant CPI, and they hypothesize that MPR identifies patients who may safely omit TLND/adjuvant while pNR may benefit from escalation.
safety_grade3plus_irAE Grade 3–4 immune-related adverse events within first 12 weeks: 22/99 = 22% (95% CI: 14–32%). Most frequent grade 3–4 events within 12 weeks: ALT increased 7%, AST increased 6%, diarrhea 5%/colitis 4%; overall up to data cutoff 30% had grade 3–4 irAEs, serum lipase increase 9% (later events included).
treatment_related_deaths No treatment-related deaths observed.
adjuvant_discontinuation_rate Of 21 pNR patients, 17 received adjuvant systemic therapy; 4 did not (3 due to irAEs, 1 lost to follow-up). Among all patients, some skipped planned adjuvant therapy due to toxicity but precise overall adjuvant discontinuation rates beyond pNR group not tabulated.
surgical_complications_or_delay TLND omission (ILN-only) associated with lower surgical morbidity: CTCAE surgical AE rate 46% (ILN-only, n=61) vs 84% (ILN+TLND, n=31) at week 12 (P < 0.001). Clavien–Dindo grades lower in ILN-only group (P < 0.001). TLND delayed in 5 (16%) patients; ILN resection delayed in 3 due to irAEs; no unexpected surgical complications reported.
subgroup_analyses Exploratory: pathologic response not associated with baseline tumor burden or most demographics. PD-L1 expression associated with pathologic response. All 4 MPR relapses had ≥2 PET-positive nodes and BRAF V600E (signal of worse outcome within MPR). Small subgroup sizes limit inference.
recurrence_patterns MPR relapses were mostly regional (3 regional, one progressed to M1a later); pPR had more distant recurrences (3 distant, 1 regional→distant); pNR relapses included regional and distant disease. Salvage surgery and adjuvant therapy were effective for regional recurrences.
resistance_mechanisms Authors speculate CPI-resistant tumor subclones and genetic/transcriptomic differences between ILN and recurrent nodes as possible mechanisms; they report planned investigations but present no molecular evidence in this paper.
counterevidence_or_null_results Co-primary endpoint (predefined feasibility threshold for 24-month RFS in MPR) was not met per protocol because >1 recurrence occurred in MPR (4 recurrences), producing a 24-month RFS of 93% (protocol expected near 97%); pPR patients had worse outcomes than MPR, suggesting pPR should not be treated like MPR. Non-randomized design prevents direct comparison with adjuvant-only strategies.
limitations_and_biases Single-arm, non-randomized design; relatively small numbers in pPR and pNR subgroups; indirect comparisons to historical controls; short-to-moderate follow-up (median 28.1 months) though many relapses occur within 12 months; possible sampling error since only ILN assessed (though prior data support concordance).
funding_and_conflicts Financial support from Bristol Myers Squibb; multiple authors disclosed advisory roles, consultancy fees, research funding from pharma companies (detailed in COI section).
