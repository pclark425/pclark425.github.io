Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1117

Name: Emergent Reasoning Threshold Theory: General Representation Bottleneck Law

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models can best perform strict logical reasoning.

Description: This theory proposes that the ability of language models to perform strict logical reasoning is fundamentally limited by a representational bottleneck: the model's internal representations must be sufficiently expressive to encode the logical structure of the task. When the logical structure exceeds the representational capacity, reasoning fails abruptly. This bottleneck is a function of both model architecture and the diversity of logical forms in the training data.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Representation Bottleneck Law (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language model → has_internal_representation_capacity → R, and
• reasoning task → requires_logical_structure → S, and
• S → less_than → R
THEN
• language model → can_encode → logical_structure_of_task, and
• language model → performs → strict_logical_reasoning_correctly

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs fail on tasks requiring representations (e.g., nested quantifiers, deep recursion) beyond their capacity.
  2. Architectural changes (e.g., increased context window, memory) can extend reasoning ability.
  3. Training on more diverse logical forms improves reasoning on structurally similar tasks.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Representation bottlenecks are known, but not in the context of emergent logical reasoning thresholds.

What Already Exists: Representation bottlenecks are discussed in deep learning, but not formalized for logical reasoning in LLMs.

What is Novel: The explicit link between representational capacity and logical reasoning threshold is new.

References:

  • Fodor & Pylyshyn (1988) Connectionism and Cognitive Architecture [Representation bottleneck in cognitive science]
  • Vaswani et al. (2017) Attention is All You Need [Transformer architecture, context window as bottleneck]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Increasing the representational capacity (e.g., context window, hidden size) will raise the logical reasoning threshold.
  2. Tasks requiring representations beyond the model's capacity will result in abrupt reasoning failures.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Novel architectures (e.g., external memory, modular networks) may eliminate or shift the bottleneck.
  2. Training on synthetic logical forms may allow models to generalize beyond their nominal representational capacity.

Negative Experiments

  1. If a model can perform strict logical reasoning on tasks requiring representations beyond its capacity, the law is challenged.
  2. If reasoning degrades gradually as representational demands increase, the bottleneck hypothesis is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some models show partial reasoning ability on tasks just beyond their representational capacity.
