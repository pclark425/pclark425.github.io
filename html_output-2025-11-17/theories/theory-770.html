Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-770

Name: Emergent Algorithmic Reasoning via Chain-of-Thought and Program Synthesis in Large Language Models

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform arithmetic.

Description: This theory posits that large language models (LLMs) develop emergent algorithmic reasoning capabilities for arithmetic by leveraging chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting and implicit program synthesis. Through exposure to vast textual data and in-context demonstrations, LLMs learn to decompose arithmetic problems into stepwise, interpretable procedures, effectively synthesizing and executing latent programs that generalize beyond memorized examples.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Emergence of Algorithmic Reasoning through Scale and Training (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → is_trained_on → large_corpus_with_arithmetic_and_reasoning_examples, and
• LLM → has_parameter_count → above_emergence_threshold
THEN
• LLM → exhibits → emergent_algorithmic_reasoning_for_arithmetic

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Larger LLMs (e.g., GPT-3, PaLM) show abrupt improvements in arithmetic and reasoning tasks at certain scale thresholds.
  2. Emergent abilities are not present in smaller models or those trained on limited data.
  3. Scaling laws show non-linear jumps in performance for algorithmic tasks.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: While emergent abilities are known, the explicit connection to algorithmic reasoning for arithmetic and the dual requirement of scale and data is a novel synthesis.

What Already Exists: Emergent abilities in LLMs have been observed, including for arithmetic and reasoning, as model scale increases.

What is Novel: This law explicitly links the emergence of algorithmic reasoning for arithmetic to both scale and the presence of reasoning/arithmetic data in the training corpus.

References:

  • Wei et al. (2022) Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models [Emergence in LLMs]
  • Kaplan et al. (2020) Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models [Scaling and performance jumps]
  • Srivastava et al. (2022) Beyond the Imitation Game: Quantifying and extrapolating the capabilities of language models [Emergence and scaling]

Statement 1: Chain-of-Thought as a Catalyst for Program Synthesis (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → is_prompted_with → chain_of_thought_examples_for_arithmetic
THEN
• LLM → decomposes → arithmetic_problem_into_stepwise_procedure, and
• LLM → synthesizes → implicit_program_for_arithmetic_solution

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Chain-of-thought prompting enables LLMs to solve multi-step arithmetic problems that are otherwise unsolvable with direct answer prompts.
  2. Stepwise reasoning increases accuracy and generalization in arithmetic tasks.
  3. LLMs can generate interpretable intermediate steps that mirror algorithmic procedures.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law extends the known effect of CoT by positing its role in latent program induction for arithmetic.

What Already Exists: Chain-of-thought prompting is known to improve reasoning and arithmetic in LLMs.

What is Novel: This law frames CoT as a mechanism for implicit program synthesis, not just reasoning.

References:

  • Wei et al. (2022) Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models [CoT for reasoning]
  • Nye et al. (2021) Show Your Work: Scratchpads for Intermediate Computation with Language Models [Stepwise reasoning and program-like behavior]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLMs above a certain scale will show abrupt improvements in multi-step arithmetic tasks when prompted with chain-of-thought.
  2. Providing explicit stepwise examples will enable LLMs to generalize to novel arithmetic operations not seen during training.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. LLMs may develop the ability to synthesize entirely novel algorithms for arithmetic operations (e.g., new base systems) from minimal examples.
  2. Emergent algorithmic reasoning may transfer to non-arithmetic domains (e.g., symbolic logic, program synthesis) with similar prompting.

Negative Experiments

  1. If LLMs at large scale fail to outperform smaller models on arithmetic reasoning, the theory is challenged.
  2. If chain-of-thought prompting does not improve generalization or stepwise decomposition, the theory's mechanism is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some LLMs may still fail on arithmetic tasks due to tokenization artifacts or lack of relevant training data.
  2. The theory does not explain failures on adversarial or ambiguous arithmetic prompts.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The theory synthesizes and extends existing findings into a unified account of arithmetic reasoning in LLMs.

References:

  • Wei et al. (2022) Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models [Emergence]
