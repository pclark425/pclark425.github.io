Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2328

Name: Network Disintegration Cascade Theory of Alzheimer's Disease

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the causes of Alzheimer's disease and effective detection methods.

Description: This theory posits that Alzheimer's disease is initiated by a cascade of failures in large-scale brain network connectivity, triggered by local molecular insults (e.g., amyloid, tau, inflammation). The breakdown of network hubs leads to global cognitive dysfunction, and the spatial-temporal pattern of network disintegration predicts symptom onset and progression. Early detection is possible by mapping network connectivity changes using advanced neuroimaging and computational modeling.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Network Hub Vulnerability Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• brain region → is → network hub, and
• brain region → is_exposed_to → molecular insult (amyloid, tau, inflammation)
THEN
• brain region → undergoes → early functional disconnection

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Default mode network hubs are among the first to show functional connectivity loss in AD.
  2. Amyloid and tau deposition is concentrated in network hubs.
  3. Network hub disruption precedes overt atrophy in imaging studies.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Network-based models exist, but this law formalizes the cascade from molecular insult to network failure.

What Already Exists: Network hub vulnerability is recognized in AD literature.

What is Novel: The explicit causal link from molecular insult to network disintegration as the primary driver is novel.

References:

  • Seeley et al. (2009) Neurodegenerative diseases target large-scale human brain networks [Network focus]
  • Buckner et al. (2009) Cortical hubs revealed by intrinsic functional connectivity [Hub vulnerability]

Statement 1: Network Disintegration Detection Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → shows → progressive loss of network connectivity (measured by fMRI/EEG/MEG)
THEN
• individual → is_at_high_risk_for → Alzheimer's disease

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Longitudinal imaging shows network connectivity loss precedes cognitive decline.
  2. Network disintegration patterns predict conversion from mild cognitive impairment to AD.
  3. Computational models can simulate network breakdown matching observed AD progression.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: Network imaging is not yet standard for early detection; this law formalizes its predictive value.

What Already Exists: Network connectivity loss is used as a research biomarker.

What is Novel: The law elevates network disintegration as a primary, rather than secondary, detection method.

References:

  • Brier et al. (2014) Functional connectivity and graph theory in preclinical Alzheimer's disease [Network detection]
  • Jones et al. (2016) Cascading network failure across the Alzheimer's disease spectrum [Network disintegration]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with early network hub disconnection will develop AD symptoms sooner than those without.
  2. Interventions that preserve or restore network connectivity will delay cognitive decline.
  3. Network disintegration patterns will predict the spatial-temporal progression of symptoms.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Non-invasive network mapping (e.g., EEG/MEG) will outperform molecular biomarkers in predicting AD onset.
  2. Some individuals may show network resilience, maintaining function despite molecular pathology.
  3. Artificially enhancing network connectivity may compensate for molecular damage in early AD.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding individuals with intact network connectivity who develop AD would challenge the theory.
  2. Demonstrating that molecular pathology without network disintegration leads to AD would falsify the cascade model.
  3. Interventions that restore network connectivity but do not affect symptoms would challenge the causal link.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases of AD with minimal network disintegration but severe molecular pathology.
