Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2424

Name: Cellular Proteostasis Capacity Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortex neurons to Aβ and tau pathology is determined by their intrinsic proteostasis capacity, including the efficiency of protein quality control systems (e.g., autophagy, ubiquitin-proteasome system, chaperones). Neurons with higher baseline proteostasis capacity can better clear or refold misfolded proteins, conferring resistance, while those with lower capacity accumulate toxic aggregates and degenerate.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: High Proteostasis Capacity Confers Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → has_high_proteostasis_capacity → True
THEN
• neuron → is_resistant_to → Aβ/tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with robust autophagy and proteasome activity show reduced Aβ/tau accumulation and degeneration.
  2. Upregulation of chaperone proteins protects neurons from Aβ/tau toxicity.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Proteostasis is a known factor, but its role in selective vulnerability of visual cortex neurons is new.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis impairment is implicated in neurodegeneration.

What is Novel: Explicit link between baseline proteostasis capacity and selective vulnerability in visual cortex is novel.

References:

  • Hipp et al. (2014) Proteostasis impairment in neurodegenerative diseases [proteostasis and neurodegeneration]
  • Ciechanover & Kwon (2015) Degradation of misfolded proteins in neurodegenerative diseases: therapeutic targets and strategies [proteostasis]

Statement 1: Proteostasis Overload Leads to Vulnerability (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → has_low_proteostasis_capacity → True, and
• neuron → is_exposed_to → Aβ/tau pathology
THEN
• neuron → undergoes_degeneration → True

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with impaired autophagy or proteasome function accumulate Aβ/tau and degenerate.
  2. Proteostasis overload precedes neuronal death in Alzheimer's models.

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law extends proteostasis theory to explain selective resistance/vulnerability in visual cortex.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis overload is known to cause cell death in neurodegeneration.

What is Novel: Application to selective vulnerability in visual cortex is novel.

References:

  • Hipp et al. (2014) Proteostasis impairment in neurodegenerative diseases [proteostasis and neurodegeneration]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Visual cortex neurons with higher expression of autophagy and proteasome genes will be more resistant to Aβ/tau pathology.
  2. Pharmacological enhancement of proteostasis will protect vulnerable visual cortex neurons from degeneration.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially reducing proteostasis capacity in resistant neurons will render them vulnerable to Aβ/tau pathology.
  2. Proteostasis enhancement in already resistant neurons will further increase their resistance or have no effect.

Negative Experiments

  1. If neurons with high proteostasis capacity are still vulnerable to Aβ/tau pathology, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If proteostasis enhancement does not protect vulnerable neurons, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some resistant neurons do not have higher proteostasis capacity, suggesting other protective mechanisms.
