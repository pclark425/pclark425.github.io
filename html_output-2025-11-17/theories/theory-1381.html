Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1381

Name: Model Capability Threshold Theory of Self-Reflection Efficacy: General Error Correction Law

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform self-reflection and improve their answer quality through multiple iterations of generate-then-reflect.

Description: This theory asserts that above the capability threshold, language models can identify and correct their own errors through self-reflection, leading to improved answer quality. Below the threshold, self-reflection is more likely to reinforce or propagate errors, as the model lacks the metacognitive ability to distinguish between correct and incorrect outputs.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Self-Reflection Enables Error Correction Above Threshold (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language_model → has_capability_level → L, and
• L → greater_than_or_equal_to → T, and
• model_output → contains_error → E
THEN
• self_reflection → reduces → E

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. High-capability models can identify and correct their own mistakes in multi-step reasoning tasks.
  2. Self-reflection in less capable models often fails to correct errors and may even reinforce them.
  3. Iterative self-reflection is most effective when the model can accurately evaluate its own outputs.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law formalizes the empirical trend as a conditional statement.

What Already Exists: Error correction via self-reflection is observed in large models.

What is Novel: The explicit conditional law linking error correction to a capability threshold is new.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Models above the threshold will show a measurable reduction in error rate after self-reflection.
  2. Models below the threshold will not show significant error reduction, or may even increase error rates.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. There may be intermediate regimes where some types of errors are corrected but others are not.
  2. Task-specific training may enable error correction below the general threshold.

Negative Experiments

  1. If models below the threshold consistently correct their own errors, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If models above the threshold fail to reduce errors through self-reflection, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases where external tools or feedback enable error correction in less capable models are not fully explained.
