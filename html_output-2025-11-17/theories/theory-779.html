Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-779

Name: Emergent Algorithmic Reasoning Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform arithmetic.

Description: With sufficient scale and training diversity, language models can develop emergent algorithmic reasoning capabilities that allow them to perform arithmetic by implicitly learning multi-step computation procedures, even in the absence of explicit programming or symbolic modules.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Scale-Driven Emergence Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language_model → has_large_scale → parameters_and_training_data, and
• training_data → contains → diverse_arithmetic_expressions
THEN
• language_model → develops → implicit_algorithmic_reasoning, and
• language_model → performs → multi-step_arithmetic_computation

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Very large LMs (e.g., GPT-4) show improved performance on multi-step arithmetic tasks, sometimes matching or exceeding smaller models with explicit arithmetic modules.
  2. Emergent abilities in LMs have been observed as model scale increases.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Emergence is known, but its application to algorithmic arithmetic reasoning is a novel, specific claim.

What Already Exists: Emergent abilities with scale are observed in LMs.

What is Novel: The explicit claim that algorithmic reasoning for arithmetic emerges at scale is a new, specific hypothesis.

References:

  • Wei et al. (2022) Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models [Emergence with scale]
  • Srivastava et al. (2022) Beyond the Imitation Game: Quantifying and extrapolating the capabilities of language models [Emergent reasoning]

Statement 1: Implicit Procedure Learning Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language_model → is_exposed_to → multi-step_arithmetic_examples
THEN
• language_model → learns → implicit_multi-step_computation_procedures, and
• language_model → applies → these_procedures_to_novel_arithmetic_expressions

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Chain-of-thought prompting enables LMs to solve arithmetic problems by generating intermediate steps, even for novel expressions.
  2. Some LMs can generalize to arithmetic expressions not seen during training, suggesting learned procedures.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Multi-step reasoning is known, but its application to arithmetic as implicit procedure learning is a novel focus.

What Already Exists: Chain-of-thought and multi-step reasoning are observed in LMs.

What is Novel: The claim that LMs learn implicit procedures for arithmetic, not just pattern matching, is a new, specific hypothesis.

References:

  • Wei et al. (2022) Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models [Chain-of-thought]
  • Nye et al. (2021) Show Your Work: Scratchpads for Intermediate Computation with Language Models [Intermediate computation]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Increasing model scale and arithmetic training diversity will improve generalization to novel arithmetic expressions.
  2. Prompting large models with chain-of-thought or intermediate steps will further boost arithmetic accuracy.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. There may be a threshold model size or data diversity at which algorithmic reasoning for arithmetic emerges abruptly.
  2. If models are trained on adversarial arithmetic curricula, they may develop novel, non-human-like computation strategies.

Negative Experiments

  1. If very large models fail to generalize to novel arithmetic expressions, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If chain-of-thought prompting does not improve arithmetic performance in large models, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some small models can perform well on arithmetic with explicit symbolic modules, which is not explained by this theory.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The theory is somewhat related to existing work but introduces a novel, specific mechanistic explanation for arithmetic performance.

References:

  • Wei et al. (2022) Emergent Abilities of Large Language Models [Emergence with scale]
