Theory (General Information)

Name: Iterative Human-AI Co-Evaluation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory proposes that the most effective evaluation of LLM-generated scientific theories arises from an iterative process in which human experts and AI systems collaboratively assess, critique, and refine candidate theories. The theory posits that this co-evaluation process leverages complementary strengths and mitigates the weaknesses of both humans and LLMs.

Statement 1: Iterative Refinement Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation process → is_iterative → involving human and AI feedback cycles
THEN
• theory quality → increases → with each cycle until convergence or consensus

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Iterative peer review and revision improve scientific theory quality.
  2. AI-assisted iterative refinement is effective in other creative and scientific domains.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

What is Novel: Formalization of iterative human-AI cycles for LLM theory evaluation.

References:

  • Shneiderman (2020) Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence: Reliable, Safe & Trustworthy [iterative human-AI processes]
  • Langley (2000) The computational support of scientific discovery [AI in iterative scientific discovery]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Human-AI co-evaluation will identify more subtle errors and creative insights than either alone.
  2. Iterative cycles will converge on higher-quality theories than single-pass evaluation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. The optimal number of human-AI cycles for maximal theory quality is unknown.
  2. Potential for human-AI disagreement to stall convergence is not well understood.

Negative Experiments

  2. If iterative refinement does not improve theory quality, the theory is undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence
