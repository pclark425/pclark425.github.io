Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-505

Name: Heterogeneous Proxytype Dual-Process Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the representational format of conceptual knowledge in brains at a functional level, based on the following results.

Description: Conceptual knowledge in brains is functionally represented by multiple coexisting representational formats (prototypes, exemplars, theory-like/causal structures, and symbolic/classical definitions), each stored in long-term memory. Context-sensitive tokenization in working memory selects the most relevant representation(s) for a given task. Fast, automatic, similarity-based categorization (Type 1/System 1) is implemented via conceptual spaces (prototypes/exemplars), while slow, deliberative, rule-based reasoning (Type 2/System 2) is implemented via symbolic ontologies and theory-like structures. Arbitration mechanisms (e.g., DELTA algorithm, Conceptual Coherence Threshold) mediate between representations, allowing flexible, context-dependent conceptual processing.

Statement 2: Proxyfication and Context Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• task_context → activates → concept
THEN
• working_memory → tokenizes → most_relevant_representation

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Proxytype theory: concepts are functionally represented as tokenizable proxies (elements of a distributed long-term network) that are activated in working memory to stand for a category; context-sensitive activation (proxyfication) is required. [e3472.4] [e3623.4]
  2. Empirical proxyfication errors in DUAL-PECCS (exemplar returned where humans preferred prototype) indicate the need for better selection heuristics. [e3623.5]

Existing Law Comparison

No existing law comparison provided.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Tasks that require rapid, typicality-based categorization will preferentially activate prototype/exemplar representations and show fast reaction times.
  2. Tasks that require rule-based, compositional, or exception handling will engage symbolic or theory-like representations and show slower, more deliberative processing.
  3. Manipulating context or task demands (e.g., by priming, instructions, or time pressure) will shift the balance between prototype/exemplar and symbolic/theory-like processing.
  4. Neural correlates of System 1 and System 2 processing will be dissociable (e.g., distributed similarity-based activation vs. focal symbolic/ontological activation).

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. In ambiguous or novel categorization tasks, the arbitration mechanism (e.g., DELTA) will produce measurable switching or conflict between representations, observable in neural or behavioral data.
  2. If the selection heuristics (e.g., similarity thresholds, coherence thresholds) are experimentally manipulated, the pattern of proxyfication errors and categorization outcomes will change systematically.
  3. In individuals with selective brain damage (e.g., to ATL hub or modality-specific cortices), the ability to use certain representational formats (e.g., prototypes, exemplars, symbolic) will be differentially impaired.
  4. Developmental or cross-cultural differences in the prevalence of representational formats will be observable, depending on experience and education.

Negative Experiments

  1. If only a single representational format is ever used across all tasks and contexts, the theory would be falsified.
  2. If dual-process dissociations (fast/slow, similarity/rule-based) are not observed in behavioral or neural data, the theory would be undermined.
  3. If context manipulations do not affect the selection of representational formats or proxyfication outcomes, the theory would be called into question.
  4. If no evidence for coexisting prototype, exemplar, and symbolic/theory-like representations is found in memory or neural data, the theory would be challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The precise neural mechanisms for arbitration, proxyfication, and context-sensitive selection among representations are not fully specified. [e3472.5] [e3623.5] [e3472.9] [e3472.10]
  2. Integration with distributed, connectionist, or geometric representations (e.g., conceptual spaces, neural population codes) is not fully detailed. [e3473.0] [e3459.0] [e3462.0] [e3475.0]
