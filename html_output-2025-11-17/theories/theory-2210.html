Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2210

Name: Evaluator-Process Coupling Theory: Information Flow Law for LLM-Generated Scientific Theories

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This general theory asserts that the effectiveness of LLM-generated scientific theories is governed by the bidirectional flow of information between the evaluator and the LLM. The theory posits that both the quantity and quality of information exchanged (including feedback, clarifications, and constraints) directly influence the epistemic robustness and creativity of the resulting theories, and that bottlenecks or asymmetries in this information flow can limit scientific discovery.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Bidirectional Information Flow Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluator → provides_information_to → LLM, and
• LLM → provides_information_to → evaluator
THEN
• theory_generation → is_enhanced_by → high_bandwidth_bidirectional_flow

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Collaborative problem-solving research shows that bidirectional communication increases creativity and solution quality.
  2. LLM prompting studies demonstrate that iterative clarification and feedback cycles improve scientific output.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law adapts collaborative communication principles to the LLM-evaluator context.

What Already Exists: Bidirectional communication is known to enhance collaborative problem-solving.

What is Novel: The formalization of bidirectional information flow as a law for LLM-generated scientific theory quality is new.

References:

  • Clark & Brennan (1991) Grounding in Communication [collaborative communication]
  • Franceschelli et al. (2023) Human-AI Co-Creation in Science [human-AI collaboration]

Statement 1: Information Bottleneck Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• information_flow → is_bottlenecked → in_either_direction
THEN
• theory_quality → is_reduced → True

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Studies in distributed cognition show that bottlenecks in information flow reduce group problem-solving effectiveness.
  2. LLM experiments indicate that limited feedback or clarification opportunities constrain theory novelty and accuracy.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law extends distributed cognition and communication bottleneck principles to LLM-based scientific discovery.

What Already Exists: Information bottlenecks are known to limit collaborative and distributed cognition.

What is Novel: The explicit application of information bottleneck effects to LLM-evaluator scientific theory generation is new.

References:

  • Hollan et al. (2000) Distributed Cognition: Toward a New Foundation for Human-Computer Interaction [distributed cognition]
  • Clark & Brennan (1991) Grounding in Communication [communication bottlenecks]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Increasing the frequency and depth of bidirectional exchanges between evaluator and LLM will improve the novelty and rigor of generated scientific theories.
  2. Introducing artificial bottlenecks (e.g., limiting feedback turns) will reduce theory quality.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. There may be optimal information flow rates that maximize theory quality for different scientific domains.
  2. In some cases, asymmetric information flow (e.g., more LLM-to-evaluator than evaluator-to-LLM) may yield unexpected creative breakthroughs.

Negative Experiments

  1. If theory quality does not decrease with imposed information bottlenecks, the law is falsified.
  2. If unidirectional communication (e.g., only evaluator-to-LLM) produces equally high-quality theories, the law is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases where LLMs generate high-quality theories with minimal or no evaluator input, possibly due to extensive pretraining.
