Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2188

Name: Iterative Human-AI Co-Evaluation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory posits that the most robust evaluation of LLM-generated scientific theories arises from an iterative process in which automated systems and human experts co-evaluate, critique, and refine candidate theories. The theory asserts that feedback loops between AI and human evaluators improve both the quality of theories and the reliability of evaluation metrics.

ConditionDetails

• LLM-generated theory → is_evaluated_by → automated_system, and

THEN

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Human-in-the-loop systems improve the reliability and acceptance of AI-generated outputs.
  2. Iterative peer review and revision are standard in scientific practice.

Existing Law Comparison

What Already Exists: Human-in-the-loop and iterative review are established in both AI and science.

What is Novel: Formalization of iterative, bidirectional feedback between AI and human experts for LLM-generated theory evaluation.

Statement 1: Metric Refinement via Human Feedback Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation_metric → is_applied_to → LLM-generated theory, and
• human_expert → provides_feedback_on → evaluation_metric
THEN
• evaluation_metric → is_refined → True

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Human feedback is used to calibrate and improve automated evaluation metrics in AI.
  2. Metrics that align with human judgment are more likely to be accepted and effective.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Iterative co-evaluation will yield higher expert acceptance rates for LLM-generated theories than automated or human-only evaluation.
  2. Metrics refined with human feedback will better predict expert judgments of theory quality.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. The optimal number of iterations for maximal theory quality may depend on the complexity of the domain.
  2. Some evaluation metrics may resist improvement even with extensive human feedback.

Negative Experiments

  1. If iterative co-evaluation does not improve theory quality or acceptance, the theory is undermined.
  2. If human feedback fails to improve metric alignment with expert judgment, the theory's assumptions are challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Potential for human bias or groupthink in iterative feedback loops is not addressed.
