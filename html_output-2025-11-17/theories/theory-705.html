Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-705

Name: Hierarchical Fourier-Modular Composition Theory of LLM Arithmetic

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform arithmetic.

Description: This theory proposes that LLMs perform arithmetic by hierarchically composing local Fourier-modular operations at each digit position, with global consistency enforced through attention and residual connections. Each digit is processed as a local phase-modular unit, and the LLM's architecture enables the propagation and resolution of carries and overflows through hierarchical, multi-layered transformations. This allows LLMs to generalize arithmetic rules across number lengths and to handle complex arithmetic tasks by recursive composition.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Local Phase-Modular Processing at Digit Level (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → processes → digit d at position p
THEN
• digit processing → is_implemented_as → local phase-modular transformation

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Layer-wise analysis of LLMs shows localized, periodic activation patterns at digit positions during arithmetic.
  2. LLMs can generalize single-digit addition and propagate carries, suggesting local modular computation.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Local modular computation is known, but its phase-based implementation in LLMs is novel.

What Already Exists: Local modular computation is used in digital circuits; neural networks can learn local rules.

What is Novel: The explicit use of local phase-modular transformations at each digit in LLMs is new.

References:

  • Weiss et al. (2021) Thinking Like Transformers: Neural Architectures for Arithmetic [local rules]
  • Geva et al. (2021) Transformer Feed-Forward Layers Are Key-Value Memories [local representations]

Statement 1: Hierarchical Composition and Carry Propagation (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → performs → multi-digit arithmetic, and
• digit-level operations → are → local phase-modular transformations
THEN
• LLM → propagates → carries and overflows via hierarchical attention and residual connections

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs can perform multi-digit arithmetic by composing single-digit rules and propagating carries across layers.
  2. Attention maps in LLMs show cross-digit dependencies during arithmetic tasks.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Hierarchical attention is known, but its use for modular phase carry propagation in arithmetic is novel.

What Already Exists: Hierarchical composition and attention are known in LLMs for sequence processing.

What is Novel: The use of hierarchical attention to propagate modular phase carries in arithmetic is new.

References:

  • Vaswani et al. (2017) Attention is All You Need [attention mechanisms]
  • Weiss et al. (2021) Thinking Like Transformers: Neural Architectures for Arithmetic [carry propagation]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Disrupting attention connections between digit positions will impair LLM performance on multi-digit arithmetic but not on single-digit tasks.
  2. LLMs will show increased errors in multi-digit arithmetic when residual connections are ablated.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If LLMs are trained with non-standard digit groupings (e.g., variable-length digits), hierarchical phase-modular composition may adapt or break down.
  2. If attention is artificially restricted to local windows, LLMs may fail to propagate carries correctly.

Negative Experiments

  1. If LLMs can perform multi-digit arithmetic without hierarchical attention or residual connections, the theory is challenged.
  2. If local phase-modular patterns are not observed at the digit level, the theory is weakened.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not address how LLMs handle arithmetic with non-numeric tokens or mixed data types.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: No prior work has unified hierarchical attention and phase-modular computation for LLM arithmetic.

References:

  • Vaswani et al. (2017) Attention is All You Need [attention mechanisms]
  • Weiss et al. (2021) Thinking Like Transformers: Neural Architectures for Arithmetic [carry propagation]
