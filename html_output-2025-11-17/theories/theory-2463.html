Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2463

Name: Intrinsic Molecular and Pathway Cohesiveness Theory of Neuronal Resistance (General Network Integration Model)

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory posits that the selective resistance or vulnerability of neuronal populations in the visual cortex to amyloid-β (Aβ) plaques and tau tangles is determined by the intrinsic cohesiveness and integration of their molecular and signaling pathway networks. Neurons with highly interconnected, redundant, and robust proteostasis, stress response, and metabolic pathways are more resistant to proteopathic stress, while those with fragmented or weakly integrated networks are more vulnerable.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Pathway Cohesiveness Confers Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_highly_cohesive → proteostasis_and_stress_response_pathways
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_resistant_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Single-cell transcriptomic and proteomic studies show that resistant neurons (e.g., certain visual cortex interneurons) have tightly co-expressed and functionally redundant chaperone, autophagy, and antioxidant gene networks.
  2. Network analysis reveals that resistant neurons have higher modularity and connectivity in proteostasis and stress response pathways compared to vulnerable neurons.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: While individual pathway roles are established, the network integration perspective is new.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis and stress response pathways are known to protect against protein aggregation.

What is Novel: The emphasis on network-level cohesiveness and integration as a determinant of resistance, rather than individual gene expression, is novel.

References:

  • Balch et al. (2008) Adapting proteostasis for disease intervention [Describes proteostasis but not network cohesiveness]
  • Mathys et al. (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [Cell-type differences, not network-level integration]

Statement 1: Network Fragmentation Increases Vulnerability (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_fragmented_or_sparse → proteostasis_and_stress_response_pathways
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_vulnerable_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Vulnerable visual cortex neurons (e.g., layer 2/3 pyramidal cells) show lower co-expression and connectivity among chaperone, autophagy, and antioxidant genes.
  2. Loss-of-function or reduced expression in key network nodes (e.g., HSPs, autophagy regulators) disproportionately affects vulnerable neurons.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: The law extends existing knowledge by introducing network fragmentation as a key factor.

What Already Exists: Loss of proteostasis and stress response function is associated with neurodegeneration.

What is Novel: The focus on network fragmentation and lack of redundancy as a determinant of selective vulnerability is novel.

References:

  • Kampinga & Bergink (2016) Heat shock proteins as potential targets for protective strategies in neurodegeneration [Reviews chaperone function, not network fragmentation]
  • Grubman et al. (2019) A single-cell atlas of entorhinal cortex from individuals with Alzheimer's disease [Cell-type differences, not network-level fragmentation]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Enhancing network integration of proteostasis and stress response pathways in vulnerable neurons will increase their resistance to Aβ and tau pathology.
  2. Disrupting pathway cohesiveness in resistant neurons will increase their vulnerability to aggregation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially engineering highly cohesive proteostasis networks in non-neuronal cells will confer resistance to Aβ and tau aggregation.
  2. Overexpression of a single pathway component in a fragmented network will not confer resistance unless network integration is also increased.

Negative Experiments

  1. If neurons with highly cohesive pathway networks are not more resistant to aggregation, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If increasing pathway network integration does not alter resistance, the theory would be undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The role of non-cell-autonomous factors (e.g., glial support, vascular health) is not directly addressed.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: While related to existing work on proteostasis, the network and integration specificity is new.

References:

  • Balch et al. (2008) Adapting proteostasis for disease intervention [Proteostasis, not network integration]
  • Mathys et al. (2019) Single-cell transcriptomic analysis of Alzheimer's disease [Cell-type differences, not network-level integration]
