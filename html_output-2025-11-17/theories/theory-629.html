Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-629

Name: Quantum Inequality Confinement Principle for Macroscopic FTL Spacetime Engineering

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how faster-than-light (FTL) spacecraft travel could be viable today using realistic scenarios and budgets, based on the following results.

Description: This theory posits that the fundamental barrier to constructing macroscopic faster-than-light (FTL) spacetimes—such as warp drives and traversable wormholes—using any known or plausible quantum field or classical matter is the quantum inequality (QI) confinement principle. This principle states that negative energy densities required for FTL spacetimes must be confined to regions whose thickness is limited by the local curvature scale and the QI sampling time, resulting in either Planck-scale (or subatomic) thicknesses for macroscopic throats/bubbles or requiring total negative energies vastly exceeding the mass-energy of the observable universe. The theory further asserts that all attempts to evade this barrier by geometric modification, field superposition, or alternative matter models ultimately fail unless new physics beyond semiclassical quantum field theory and general relativity is discovered.

ConditionDetails
IF
• spacetime geometry → requires → macroscopic region of negative energy density (exotic matter), and

THEN

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Any future attempt to construct a macroscopic FTL spacetime (warp bubble or wormhole) using known quantum fields or classical matter will find that the required negative energy must be confined to a region of thickness no greater than the local curvature scale, typically Planck-scale for meter-scale geometries.
  2. Experimental efforts to increase the wall thickness of a negative-energy region supporting a warp bubble will result in a proportional increase in the total negative energy required, quickly exceeding any practical energy budget.
  3. All macroscopic FTL spacetime proposals based on known physics will either require unphysical energy densities or fail to be constructible in practice.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If a new quantum field or interaction is discovered that can violate the QI bounds, it may allow for macroscopic FTL spacetimes with manageable energy requirements.
  2. If quantum gravity effects at the Planck scale modify the QI constraints, it may become possible to construct macroscopic FTL spacetimes without requiring Planck-scale confinement of negative energy.
  3. If a laboratory experiment demonstrates a macroscopic region of negative energy density with thickness much greater than the local curvature scale and without compensating positive energy, it would indicate a breakdown of the QI Confinement Principle.

Negative Experiments

  1. Demonstration of a macroscopic region of negative energy density (e.g., Casimir effect) with thickness much greater than the local curvature scale and without compensating positive energy would falsify the QI Confinement Law.
  2. Construction of a macroscopic traversable wormhole or warp bubble with total negative energy less than the mass-energy of a planet or star would call the No-Go Law into question.
  3. Observation of a stable, macroscopic FTL spacetime (warp bubble or wormhole) in nature or experiment, supported by known quantum fields or classical matter, would contradict the theory.
