Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-673

Name: Evaluator-Process Coupling Theory for LLM-Generated Scientific Theories

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories, based on the following results.

Description: This theory asserts that the reliability and validity of evaluating LLM-generated scientific theories depend not only on the evaluation metrics themselves, but also on the coupling between the evaluation process (e.g., prompting, reference use, chain-of-thought, few-shot examples, and human-in-the-loop) and the properties of the LLM-generated outputs. The theory posits that evaluation outcomes are highly sensitive to process variables such as prompt design, inclusion of reference answers, chain-of-thought elicitation, and the use of iterative or self-critique protocols. Furthermore, the theory claims that process-aware evaluation (i.e., evaluation protocols that adapt to the generation process and output type) is necessary for robust, generalizable assessment of LLM-generated scientific theories.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If prompt design, reference inclusion, or few-shot examples are changed in an LLM-based evaluation protocol, the evaluation outcome (e.g., agreement with human scores) will change significantly.
  2. If evaluation protocols are not matched to the generation process (e.g., do not account for chain-of-thought or iterative refinement), they will misjudge the quality of LLM-generated scientific theories.
  3. If human-in-the-loop or hybrid evaluation protocols are used, alignment with expert judgments and reliability will increase compared to fully automated LLM-based evaluation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If process-aware, adaptive evaluation protocols are developed that dynamically adjust to the generation process and output type, they may achieve human-level or superhuman reliability in evaluating LLM-generated scientific theories.
  2. If LLM-based evaluators are trained or fine-tuned with process-coupled data (e.g., including chain-of-thought, reference use, and iterative feedback), their robustness and generalizability may surpass current human-in-the-loop protocols.
  3. If hybrid protocols are scaled to large, open-ended scientific domains, they may enable safe, semi-autonomous scientific ideation with minimal human oversight.

Negative Experiments

  1. If prompt and reference variations do not significantly affect evaluation outcomes, the prompt and reference sensitivity law would be falsified.
  2. If process-agnostic evaluation protocols are as reliable as process-aware protocols, the process-output coupling law would be undermined.
  3. If fully automated LLM-based evaluation matches or exceeds human-in-the-loop or hybrid protocols in reliability and alignment, the human-in-the-loop law would be challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some evaluation settings (e.g., with highly constrained outputs or strong reference answers) may show low prompt or process sensitivity. [e6147.4] [e6011.2] [e6011.1]
  2. In some domains, LLM-based evaluators may outperform humans even without process-aware protocols, especially in low-level factuality detection. [e6153.0] [e6165.14]
