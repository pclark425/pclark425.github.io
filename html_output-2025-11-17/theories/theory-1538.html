New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Large-scale entangled quantum networks will enable remote state preparation with higher fidelity and over greater distances than currently possible.
  2. Engineered measurement protocols will allow for the synchronization of quantum events at spacelike separated locations.
  3. Quantum steering can be used to coordinate distributed quantum actuators for rapid, nonlocal reconfiguration.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  2. Novel quantum effects or loopholes in the no-signaling theorem may emerge at the scale of engineered networks.
  3. Quantum gravity effects may enhance or suppress nonlocal correlations at large scales.

Negative Experiments

  2. Demonstration that engineered measurement protocols cannot increase effective coordination speed beyond light speed would falsify the theory.
  3. Observation that quantum decoherence destroys entanglement at the required scales would undermine the theory.
