Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-724

Name: Pattern-Completion Theory of LLM Arithmetic

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform arithmetic.

Description: Language models perform arithmetic primarily by pattern completion: they match arithmetic queries to similar patterns encountered during training and generate the most probable completion, rather than executing explicit algorithmic computation. This theory posits that LLMs' arithmetic ability is a direct extension of their general pattern-matching and memorization mechanisms, and that their performance is tightly linked to the frequency and diversity of arithmetic patterns in their training data.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Pattern Matching Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• arithmetic_query → is_input_to → language_model, and
• arithmetic_query → is_similar_to → training_data_pattern
THEN
• language_model → outputs → completion_similar_to_training_pattern

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs are trained on next-token prediction and often output correct answers for arithmetic queries that are common in their training data.
  2. Performance drops for rare or out-of-distribution arithmetic queries, indicating reliance on pattern familiarity.
  3. LLMs can be prompted to output plausible but incorrect answers for arithmetic queries that are adversarially similar to training data.
  4. When LLMs are trained on synthetic corpora with systematically shifted arithmetic facts (e.g., '2+2=5'), they reproduce those shifted facts, indicating lack of true algorithmic understanding.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: While related to existing work on LLM memorization and pattern matching, this law explicitly applies the mechanism to arithmetic, which is not always made explicit in prior work.

What Already Exists: It is known that LLMs rely on pattern completion for many tasks, including factual recall and some arithmetic.

What is Novel: This law formalizes the idea that arithmetic in LLMs is not algorithmic but is a direct extension of their general pattern-completion mechanism, and explicitly predicts failure on novel or adversarially shifted queries.

References:

  • Brown et al. (2020) Language Models are Few-Shot Learners [Pattern completion and memorization in LLMs]
  • Carlini et al. (2021) Extracting Training Data from Large Language Models [Pattern memorization in LLMs]
  • Zhang et al. (2022) Can Language Models Do Arithmetic? [Empirical study of LLM arithmetic performance]

Statement 1: Frequency-Dependence Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• arithmetic_query → frequency_in_training_data → high
THEN
• language_model → arithmetic_accuracy → high

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs are more accurate on arithmetic facts (e.g., single-digit addition) that are common in text corpora.
  2. Accuracy drops for less frequent or more complex arithmetic queries.
  3. Fine-tuning LLMs on more examples of a specific arithmetic operation increases their accuracy on that operation.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: This is a direct extension of known frequency effects in LLMs, but its explicit application to arithmetic is less commonly formalized.

What Already Exists: Empirical studies show LLMs are more accurate on frequent facts.

What is Novel: This law generalizes the frequency-accuracy relationship specifically to arithmetic, suggesting a direct causal link and predicting the effect of fine-tuning.

References:

  • Zhang et al. (2022) Can Language Models Do Arithmetic? [Empirical study of LLM arithmetic performance]
  • Marcus & Davis (2020) GPT-3, Bloviator: OpenAI’s language generator has no idea what it’s talking about [Discussion of LLMs' factual and arithmetic limitations]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a language model is fine-tuned on a corpus with more examples of a specific arithmetic operation (e.g., 3-digit addition), its accuracy on that operation will increase.
  2. If an arithmetic query is phrased in a way that closely matches common training patterns, the model's accuracy will be higher.
  3. If a model is exposed to arithmetic queries with rare formats or phrasing, its accuracy will decrease.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If a language model is exposed to adversarially generated arithmetic queries that are structurally similar but semantically different from training data, it may produce plausible but incorrect answers.
  2. If a model is trained on a synthetic corpus with systematically shifted arithmetic facts (e.g., '2+2=5'), it will output those shifted facts, indicating lack of true algorithmic understanding.
  3. If a model is trained on a corpus with a mixture of correct and incorrect arithmetic facts, its outputs may reflect the statistical distribution of those facts.

Negative Experiments

  1. If a language model can solve arithmetic queries that are structurally novel and not present in the training data, this would challenge the pattern-completion theory.
  2. If a model can generalize to arbitrary-length arithmetic problems with high accuracy, this would suggest algorithmic reasoning rather than pattern completion.
  3. If a model can consistently reject adversarially shifted arithmetic facts, this would challenge the theory.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some LLMs show partial generalization to larger numbers or novel arithmetic queries, which is not fully explained by pattern completion alone.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: The theory is closely related to existing work on LLM memorization and pattern matching, but its explicit application to arithmetic and adversarial/fine-tuning scenarios is a novel synthesis.
