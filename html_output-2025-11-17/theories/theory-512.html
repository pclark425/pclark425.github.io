Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-512

Name: Calibration-Dependent LLM Probability Estimation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how LLMs can accurately measure the probability or likelihood of specific future real-world scientific discoveries, based on the following results.

Description: The ability of LLMs to accurately measure the probability of future real-world scientific discoveries is fundamentally limited by the calibration of their internal uncertainty estimates, which are affected by model architecture, training regime (especially RLHF/alignment), and the method of probability extraction (logit-based, verbalized, or ensemble-based). Proper calibration methods and careful extraction of uncertainty are necessary for trustworthy probabilistic forecasting.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Calibration Sensitivity Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → has undergone → alignment or RLHF post-training
THEN
• LLM → exhibits → degraded calibration of internal probability estimates compared to pre-trained models

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. GPT-4 Technical Report: Pre-trained GPT-4's predicted confidence (logprob) matches empirical correctness probability, but RLHF post-training reduces calibration quality. [e3725.2]
  2. Llama-2-70B-Chat: RLHF/chat fine-tuning worsens conditional-probability calibration; verbalized confidences can partially recover calibration. [e3735.4]

Statement 1: Probability Extraction Method Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → is evaluated → using direct logit-based probability extraction
THEN
• LLM → produces → probability estimates that may be miscalibrated, especially under distribution shift or for paraphrasable/multi-answer questions

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Answer logit (normalized log-probability) can be a competitive zero-shot baseline in some settings but fails to capture epistemic uncertainty robustly across distribution shifts, especially when paraphrase/multi-answer phenomena are present. [e3727.1]
  2. Logit-based probability approaches are limited by tokenization and prompt phrasing, and are not reliable calibrations of true outcome probabilities. [e3646.6] [e3734.3]

Statement 2: Verbalized Probability Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → is prompted → to verbalize its probability/confidence in natural language
THEN
• LLM → can produce → well-calibrated probability estimates, but only if properly fine-tuned or with sufficient in-context examples

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Verbalized probability (finetuned) outperforms logit-based and indirect-logit baselines on Multi-answer evaluation; few-shot in-context learning can approach finetuned calibration. [e3727.0]
  2. gpt-3.5-turbo and GPT-4: Verbalized probabilities (numeric or mapped linguistic expressions) are consistently better-calibrated than sampled conditional probabilities. [e3735.0] [e3735.1]
  3. Verbalized confidence estimates (ordinal) are not always well-calibrated; most models are not well-calibrated when asked to verbally report their confidence. [e3734.0] [e3734.1] [e3734.2]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a new LLM is trained with minimal RLHF or alignment, its internal log-probabilities will be better calibrated for correctness than a similar model with extensive RLHF.
  2. If a model is fine-tuned to verbalize probabilities using proper scoring rules, its verbalized confidence will be better calibrated than its raw logit-based probabilities.
  3. If a model is evaluated on a new domain with high paraphrasability or multiple valid answers, logit-based confidence will be less reliable than verbalized or ensemble-based confidence.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If a new calibration method is developed that combines logit-based, verbalized, and ensemble-based uncertainty, it may yield superior calibration across all domains, including scientific discovery forecasting.
  2. If a model is trained with RLHF specifically targeting calibration (rather than helpfulness or harmlessness), it may achieve both alignment and well-calibrated uncertainty.
  3. If LLMs are used to forecast the probability of unprecedented scientific discoveries, their calibration may degrade unless fine-tuned on similar out-of-distribution tasks.

Negative Experiments

  1. If RLHF-fine-tuned models are found to have better calibration than pre-trained models on forecasting tasks, the calibration sensitivity law would be challenged.
  2. If logit-based probability extraction is found to be more reliable than verbalized or ensemble-based methods on out-of-distribution scientific forecasting, the probability extraction method law would be called into question.
  3. If verbalized confidence is consistently miscalibrated even after fine-tuning, the verbalized probability law would be undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases where calibration is improved by methods not covered (e.g., contextual calibration, Bayesian alignment, or input clarification ensembling). [e3726.1] [e3646.0] [e3740.0]
  2. Domains where model calibration is not the limiting factor for forecasting accuracy (e.g., lack of relevant information or domain knowledge). [e3737.0] [e3643.0]
