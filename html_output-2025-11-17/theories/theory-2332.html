Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2332

Name: Proteostatic Imbalance and Clearance Failure Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the causes of Alzheimer's disease and effective detection methods.

Description: This theory proposes that Alzheimer's disease is fundamentally a disorder of proteostasis, where the balance between production, aggregation, and clearance of misfolded proteins (amyloid-beta, tau) is disrupted. The failure of clearance mechanisms—especially glymphatic and microglial pathways—leads to toxic accumulation, which triggers downstream neurodegeneration. Detection is most effective by assessing dynamic clearance capacity and proteostatic stress markers.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostatic Imbalance Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → increased production or aggregation of amyloid-beta and/or tau, and
• individual → has → impaired clearance mechanisms (e.g., glymphatic dysfunction, microglial senescence)
THEN
• individual → is_at_high_risk_for → toxic protein accumulation and Alzheimer's disease

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Impaired glymphatic clearance is associated with amyloid and tau accumulation.
  2. Microglial dysfunction leads to reduced clearance of misfolded proteins.
  3. Proteostatic stress markers are elevated in AD brains.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

What Already Exists: Amyloid and tau accumulation and clearance mechanisms are known in AD.

What is Novel: The law formalizes the dynamic balance and clearance failure as the central cause.

  • Heneka et al. (2015) Neuroinflammation in Alzheimer's disease [Microglial dysfunction]

Statement 1: Dynamic Clearance Detection Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → shows → reduced glymphatic flow or impaired microglial phagocytosis
THEN
• individual → can_be_detected_as → preclinical Alzheimer's disease

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Imaging of glymphatic flow and microglial activity can distinguish preclinical AD.
  2. Proteostatic stress markers in CSF and blood are elevated before clinical symptoms.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Clearance imaging is emerging, but its primacy for detection is novel.

What Already Exists: Clearance imaging and proteostatic markers are under investigation.

What is Novel: The law formalizes dynamic clearance assessment as a primary detection method.

References:

  • Heneka et al. (2015) Neuroinflammation in Alzheimer's disease [Microglial dysfunction]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with impaired glymphatic flow will accumulate amyloid and tau before clinical symptoms.
  2. Dynamic clearance imaging will predict AD risk better than static amyloid/tau measurements.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Enhancing glymphatic or microglial clearance will prevent or reverse early AD pathology.
  2. Some individuals with impaired clearance may not develop AD, indicating compensatory proteostatic mechanisms.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding individuals with impaired clearance but no protein accumulation or AD would challenge the theory.
  2. Demonstrating AD pathology in the presence of normal clearance would call the theory into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases of AD with normal clearance imaging and proteostatic markers.
