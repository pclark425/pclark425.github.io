Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1395

Name: Iterative Self-Reflection as Hierarchical Error Subspace Projection

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform self-reflection and improve their answer quality through multiple iterations of generate-then-reflect.

Description: This theory proposes that each self-reflection pass in a language model projects the output into a subspace that is increasingly orthogonal to previously identified error subspaces. The process is hierarchical: initial passes address gross errors, while subsequent passes target finer-grained or more subtle error components. This hierarchical projection mechanism enables the model to systematically reduce error overlap and approach a more accurate solution through successive refinement.

ConditionDetails

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs can address different types of errors at different reflection stages, suggesting a hierarchical correction process.
  2. Hierarchical error correction is a known principle in multi-stage signal processing and optimization.

  2. The improvement in output quality per reflection pass will decrease after a certain number of passes.
