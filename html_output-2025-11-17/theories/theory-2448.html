Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2448

Name: Hierarchical Network Stress and Selective Vulnerability Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the hierarchical organization of the visual cortex, with its ascending and descending projections, creates gradients of network stress that determine selective vulnerability to amyloid-β and tau pathology. Neurons at key hierarchical nodes (e.g., those integrating feedforward and feedback information) experience greater synaptic and metabolic stress, predisposing them to pathology, while neurons at the periphery or with unidirectional, less integrated roles are more resistant.

• neuronal_population → is_at_hierarchical_node → integration point (feedforward/feedback), and

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons at integration points in hierarchical networks are more vulnerable in AD and tauopathies.
  2. High synaptic and metabolic stress at these nodes increases risk of protein aggregation.
  3. Hierarchical network models predict early disruption at integrator nodes in neurodegeneration.

Statement 1: Peripheral Node Resistance Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails

• neuronal_population → is_at_hierarchical_periphery → True, and

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  2. Low network stress and synaptic activity reduce risk of protein aggregation.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Neurons at hierarchical integration points in the visual cortex will show greater amyloid-β and tau pathology than peripheral neurons.
  2. Disruption of feedforward/feedback integration will precede widespread pathology in visual cortex in AD models.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing network stress at peripheral nodes will increase their vulnerability to pathology.
  2. Rewiring hierarchical connections to redistribute network stress will alter the spatial pattern of pathology.

Negative Experiments

  1. If peripheral neurons show equal or greater pathology than hierarchical integrator nodes, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If network stress does not correlate with vulnerability, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence
