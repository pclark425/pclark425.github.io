Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1338

Name: Iterative Self-Optimization in Language Models

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform self-reflection and improve their answer quality through multiple iterations of generate-then-reflect.

Description: This theory posits that language models, when engaged in generate-then-reflect cycles, perform a form of internal optimization by iteratively updating their internal representations and output strategies based on self-evaluated feedback, leading to improved answer quality over multiple iterations.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Self-Evaluation Drives Representation Update (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language model → generates → initial answer, and
• language model → performs → self-reflection on answer
THEN
• language model → updates → internal representations to reduce identified errors

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Empirical studies show that models improve factual accuracy and reasoning when prompted to reflect and revise.
  2. Attention and hidden state analyses reveal shifts in focus and representation after reflection steps.
  3. Iterative refinement methods (e.g., Self-Refine, Reflexion) demonstrate measurable answer quality gains over multiple cycles.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: While iterative improvement is known, the theory formalizes the internal optimization process as a general principle.

What Already Exists: Iterative refinement and self-feedback are established in recent LLM research.

What is Novel: The explicit mechanism of internal representation update driven by self-evaluation is newly formalized here.

References:

  • Madaan et al. (2023) Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback [Iterative refinement]
  • Shinn et al. (2023) Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning [Self-reflection and improvement]

Statement 1: Convergence of Answer Quality Through Iteration (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language model → performs → multiple generate-then-reflect cycles
THEN
• answer quality → increases → with each cycle, up to a plateau

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Empirical results show diminishing returns in answer quality after several reflection cycles.
  2. Performance curves in iterative refinement studies show initial rapid improvement, then plateau.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The pattern is observed, but not previously formalized as a general law.

What Already Exists: Empirical observation of diminishing returns in iterative LLM refinement.

What is Novel: The formalization of this as a convergence law for answer quality is new.

References:

  • Madaan et al. (2023) Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback [Empirical evidence of diminishing returns]
  • Shinn et al. (2023) Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning [Iterative improvement and plateau]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Models will show measurable improvement in answer quality over the first few reflection cycles, with gains tapering off after a small number of iterations.
  2. Analysis of hidden states will reveal systematic changes after each reflection, corresponding to error correction.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Introducing explicit optimization objectives during reflection may accelerate convergence or raise the quality plateau.
  2. There may exist tasks or domains where iterative self-reflection leads to oscillation or degradation rather than convergence.

Negative Experiments

  1. If answer quality does not improve or worsens with additional reflection cycles, the theory is challenged.
  2. If internal representations do not change measurably after reflection, the mechanism of self-optimization is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases where reflection introduces new errors or hallucinations, leading to non-monotonic answer quality.
