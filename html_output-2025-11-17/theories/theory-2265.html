Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2265

Name: Multi-Dimensional Evaluation Theory for LLM-Generated Scientific Theories

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory posits that the evaluation of LLM-generated scientific theories requires a multi-dimensional framework that integrates criteria such as logical coherence, empirical testability, explanatory power, novelty, and ethical/societal impact. Each dimension is assessed independently and then synthesized to provide a holistic evaluation score, enabling both automated and human-in-the-loop assessment.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Multi-Dimensional Assessment Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM-generated theory → is_evaluated → by assessment system
THEN
• assessment system → must_score → theory on logical coherence, and
• assessment system → must_score → theory on empirical testability, and
• assessment system → must_score → theory on explanatory power, and
• assessment system → must_score → theory on novelty, and
• assessment system → must_score → theory on ethical/societal impact

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Scientific theory evaluation in philosophy and practice is multi-criteria (e.g., Kuhn, Lakatos, meta-science literature).
  2. LLMs can generate plausible but flawed or unethical theories, necessitating multi-faceted evaluation.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law is closely-related-to-existing in its criteria, but novel in its formalization and automation for LLM outputs.

What Already Exists: Multi-criteria evaluation is standard in philosophy of science and meta-science.

What is Novel: Application of a formal, multi-dimensional, partially automatable framework to LLM-generated theories.

References:

  • Kuhn (1962) The Structure of Scientific Revolutions [criteria for theory choice]

  • Ioannidis (2005) Why Most Published Research Findings Are False [meta-science, multi-criteria assessment]

Statement 1: Holistic Synthesis Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• theory → has_scores → on multiple evaluation dimensions
THEN
• evaluation system → must_synthesize → scores into holistic evaluation

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Meta-scientific frameworks often combine multiple criteria for overall assessment.
  2. Automated systems in other domains (e.g., grant review, peer review) use weighted synthesis of criteria.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law is somewhat-related-to-existing, but its application to LLM-generated theory evaluation is novel.

What Already Exists: Synthesis of multiple evaluation criteria is common in scientific and technical review.

What is Novel: Explicit formalization and automation for LLM-generated scientific theory evaluation.

References:

  • Bornmann & Daniel (2008) What do citation counts measure? [multi-criteria in research evaluation]
  • Kuhn (1962) The Structure of Scientific Revolutions [criteria synthesis in theory choice]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLM-generated theories that score highly across all dimensions will be more likely to be accepted by expert reviewers.
  2. Automated multi-dimensional scoring will correlate with human expert judgments of theory quality.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Some theories may score poorly on one dimension (e.g., empirical testability) but highly on others (e.g., explanatory power), leading to ambiguous overall evaluation.
  2. Novelty scoring may undervalue paradigm-shifting but initially implausible theories.

Negative Experiments

  1. If theories with high holistic scores are consistently rejected by experts, the synthesis law is flawed.
  2. If the system fails to distinguish between logically incoherent and coherent theories, the multi-dimensional assessment law is challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not address the dynamic evolution of theory quality as new evidence emerges.
