Statement 0: Causal Integrity of Positive-Energy Warp Bubbles (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• warp bubble → is_constructed_with → positive energy density, and
• bubble boundary → satisfies → global hyperbolicity
THEN
• warp bubble → does_not_induce → closed timelike curves (CTCs)

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  2. Global hyperbolicity is a sufficient condition for causal integrity in general relativity.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Positive-energy warp bubbles engineered with global hyperbolicity will not exhibit causality violations in laboratory tests.
  2. Energy input required to sustain a small-scale warp bubble will be measurable and consistent with energy conservation laws.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Scaling up the warp bubble may reveal new forms of energy leakage or boundary instabilities not present at small scales.
  2. Quantum effects at the bubble boundary may introduce novel phenomena not predicted by classical general relativity.

Negative Experiments

  1. Observation of closed timelike curves or causality violations in a positive-energy warp bubble would falsify the theory.
