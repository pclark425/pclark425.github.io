Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Distributed Number Encoding Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language_model → encodes → numbers_as_vectors
THEN
• arithmetic_operation → is_performed_by → vector_transformations

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Probing studies show that number magnitude and structure are recoverable from hidden states.
  2. Arithmetic performance persists even when tokenization is suboptimal, indicating distributed encoding.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law extends distributed representation theory to arithmetic computation in LMs.

What Already Exists: Distributed representations are foundational in neural networks.

What is Novel: The explicit mapping of arithmetic operations to compositional vector transformations in LMs is novel.

References:

  • Mikolov et al. (2013) Distributed Representations of Words and Phrases [Distributed representations]
  • Geva et al. (2020) Transformer Feed-Forward Layers Are Key-Value Memories [Distributed storage in LMs]

Statement 1: Compositional Transformation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• arithmetic_expression → is_encoded_in → distributed_vector_space
THEN
• language_model → applies → compositional_transformations_to_vectors, and
• language_model → outputs → approximate_arithmetic_result

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Hidden state trajectories during arithmetic tasks show compositional structure.
  2. Models can perform arithmetic on numbers outside the training set, indicating compositional generalization.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law formalizes compositionality as the mechanism for arithmetic in LMs.

What Already Exists: Compositionality in neural representations is a known phenomenon.

What is Novel: The explicit law connecting compositional vector transformations to arithmetic in LMs is new.

References:

  • Lake & Baroni (2018) Generalization without Systematicity: On the Compositional Skills of Sequence-to-Sequence Recurrent Networks [Compositionality in neural nets]
  • Geva et al. (2020) Transformer Feed-Forward Layers Are Key-Value Memories [Compositional storage]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Probing intermediate representations will reveal compositional encodings of arithmetic expressions.
  2. Interventions on vector representations can systematically alter arithmetic outputs.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Training on non-standard arithmetic (e.g., modular arithmetic) will induce novel compositional structures.
  2. Distributed representations may enable transfer of arithmetic skills to related symbolic tasks.

Negative Experiments

  1. If arithmetic performance collapses when distributed representations are disrupted, the theory is supported; if not, it is challenged.
  2. If models cannot generalize to novel arithmetic expressions despite distributed encoding, the theory is contradicted.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some arithmetic errors may arise from tokenization artifacts rather than representational limits.
