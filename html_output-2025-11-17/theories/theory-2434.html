Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2434

Name: Network Topology-Driven Selective Vulnerability Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory asserts that the selective vulnerability of visual cortical neurons to amyloid-β and tau pathology is determined by their position within the cortical network topology, specifically their participation in hub-like, integrative, or highly central motifs. Neurons that serve as network hubs or bridges for information flow are more likely to accumulate pathology due to increased exposure to trans-synaptic protein transfer, while peripheral or non-central neurons are more resistant.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: The law formalizes a network property as a determinant of resistance.

What Already Exists: Peripheral resistance is observed but not formalized as a network law.

What is Novel: The explicit network centrality-based resistance law is new.

References:

  • Seeley (2009) Neurodegenerative diseases target large-scale human brain networks [network hub vulnerability]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Neurons with high network centrality (hubs) will show earlier and more severe pathology in animal models of dementia.
  2. Disrupting hub connectivity will reduce the spread of amyloid-β and tau pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing the centrality of a resistant neuron population will increase its vulnerability.
  2. Altering network topology via optogenetic or genetic methods will change the pattern of vulnerability.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding that peripheral neurons are equally vulnerable as network hubs would challenge the theory.
  2. Demonstrating that network centrality does not correlate with pathology would call the theory into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some hub neurons may be resistant due to molecular or metabolic factors not captured by network topology.
