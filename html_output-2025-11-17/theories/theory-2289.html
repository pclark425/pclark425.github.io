Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2289

Name: Actionable Feedback as a Necessary Condition for Iterative Evaluation Improvement

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory posits that the presence of actionable feedback is a necessary precondition for the iterative improvement of evaluation protocols for LLM-generated scientific theories. Without actionable feedback, evaluators lack the information required to refine their criteria, processes, or judgments, resulting in stagnation or random drift rather than systematic improvement.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Necessity of Actionable Feedback for Iterative Improvement (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation process → is_iterative → True, and
• actionable feedback → is_absent → True
THEN
• evaluation process → fails_to_improve → systematically

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Iterative improvement in educational, organizational, and machine learning contexts consistently requires feedback that can be acted upon; absence of such feedback leads to stagnation.
  2. Meta-analyses in learning sciences show that without actionable feedback, repeated attempts do not yield systematic gains.

What is Novel: This law extends the necessity of actionable feedback specifically to the domain of LLM-generated scientific theory evaluation protocols.

Statement 1: Presence of Actionable Feedback Enables Systematic Evaluation Refinement (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation process → is_iterative → True, and
• actionable feedback → is_present → True
THEN
• evaluation process → improves → systematically

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Studies in peer review, education, and software engineering show that actionable feedback leads to measurable improvements in evaluation criteria and outcomes over iterations.
  2. Iterative refinement in machine learning (e.g., hyperparameter tuning) depends on actionable feedback from validation metrics.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Evaluation protocols for LLM-generated scientific theories that do not incorporate actionable feedback will show little to no systematic improvement over multiple iterations.
  2. Introducing actionable feedback into an iterative evaluation process will result in measurable improvements in evaluation accuracy and reliability.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. There may be a threshold of feedback actionability below which improvement is negligible, and above which improvement accelerates.
  2. The necessity of actionable feedback may be bypassed in rare cases by highly expert evaluators or extremely simple evaluation tasks.

Negative Experiments

  1. If evaluation protocols improve systematically in the absence of actionable feedback, the theory is challenged.
  2. If actionable feedback is present but no improvement occurs, the sufficiency of feedback for improvement is questioned.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Improvement due to external factors such as evaluator expertise, motivation, or unrelated training, independent of feedback.
