Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-747

Name: Tokenization Constraint Theory of LM Arithmetic

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform arithmetic.

Description: The ability of language models to perform arithmetic is fundamentally constrained by the tokenization scheme and the representational granularity of numbers. When numbers are tokenized into subword or character-level units, the model's ability to generalize arithmetic operations is limited by its capacity to learn and manipulate these representations, leading to systematic errors for large or rare numbers.

Statement 0: Tokenization Granularity Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• number → is_tokenized_as → multiple_subword_tokens, and
• arithmetic_problem → contains → number
THEN
• language_model → is_likely_to → make_tokenization_based_errors

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LMs make more errors on arithmetic with large or rare numbers that are split into multiple tokens.
  2. Performance is higher for numbers that are single tokens (e.g., small integers).

Statement 1: Representation Limitation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• arithmetic_problem → contains → numbers_with_unseen_token_combinations, and
• language_model → is_prompted_with → arithmetic_problem
THEN
• language_model → is_likely_to → fail_to_generalize_arithmetic

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LMs struggle with arithmetic involving numbers or digit patterns not seen during training.
  2. Generalization to novel number combinations is poor, especially for large numbers.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a model is retrained with a character-level tokenizer, arithmetic performance on large numbers will improve.
  2. If numbers are replaced with rare or OOD digit sequences, error rates will increase.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A hybrid tokenization scheme (mixing subword and character-level) may enable better arithmetic generalization without sacrificing language fluency.
  2. There may exist a tokenization scheme that enables near-perfect arithmetic in LMs without explicit algorithmic modules.

Negative Experiments

  1. If LMs with coarse-grained tokenization perform as well as character-level models on large-number arithmetic, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If tokenization changes do not affect arithmetic error rates, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some LMs with subword tokenization can perform well on arithmetic after fine-tuning, suggesting other factors can compensate for tokenization limitations.
