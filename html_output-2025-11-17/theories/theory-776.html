Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-776

Name: Compositional Pattern Matching Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform arithmetic.

Description: Language models perform arithmetic primarily by leveraging compositional pattern matching over their training data. Rather than executing explicit algorithmic computation, LMs learn statistical associations between input patterns (e.g., '12 + 7 =') and output patterns (e.g., '19'), and generalize to new arithmetic queries by recombining learned sub-patterns. This process is modulated by the frequency and diversity of arithmetic expressions in the training data, the model's capacity, and the representational structure of numbers and operators.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Pattern Frequency Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• arithmetic_expression → is_frequent_in → training_data
THEN
• language_model → performs → arithmetic_with_high_accuracy

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LMs are highly accurate on arithmetic facts (e.g., single-digit addition) that are common in training corpora.
  2. Performance drops on rare or out-of-distribution arithmetic expressions.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Pattern matching is established, but its direct, law-like connection to arithmetic accuracy is novel.

What Already Exists: Pattern matching and memorization in LMs are well-documented.

What is Novel: The explicit link between pattern frequency and arithmetic accuracy is a new, formalized claim.

References:

  • Weiss et al. (2021) Thinking Like Transformers: Neural Architectures for Algorithmic Reasoning [Pattern matching in LMs]
  • Zhang et al. (2022) Can Language Models Do Arithmetic? [Empirical evidence on frequency effects]

Statement 1: Compositional Generalization Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• arithmetic_expression → is_composed_of → familiar_subpatterns, and
• language_model → has_capacity → sufficient_for_composition
THEN
• language_model → generalizes → arithmetic_to_novel_combinations

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LMs can solve arithmetic problems by recombining known sub-patterns, e.g., using knowledge of '10 + 5' and '10 + 6' to infer '10 + 7'.
  2. Generalization is limited when sub-patterns are unfamiliar or when compositional depth increases.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Compositionality is discussed in literature, but its explicit law-like role in LM arithmetic is novel.

What Already Exists: Compositionality in neural networks is a known challenge.

What is Novel: The law formalizes compositional generalization as a mechanism for arithmetic in LMs.

References:

  • Lake & Baroni (2018) Generalization without Systematicity: On the Compositional Skills of Sequence-to-Sequence Recurrent Networks [Compositionality in neural nets]
  • Saxton et al. (2019) Analysing Mathematical Reasoning Abilities of Neural Models [Compositional generalization in arithmetic tasks]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a model is trained on a dataset with more diverse and frequent arithmetic expressions, its arithmetic accuracy will increase.
  2. If a model is tested on arithmetic expressions composed of familiar sub-patterns, it will generalize better than on entirely novel patterns.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If a model is trained on arithmetic with novel operators (e.g., '⊕'), it may generalize to new operator patterns if sub-patterns are similar.
  2. If a model is exposed to adversarially composed arithmetic expressions, its generalization may break down in unpredictable ways.

Negative Experiments

  1. If a model performs well on arithmetic expressions that are both rare and composed of unfamiliar sub-patterns, this would challenge the theory.
  2. If increasing the frequency of certain arithmetic patterns in training does not improve accuracy, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some LMs can perform multi-step arithmetic with chain-of-thought prompting, even when sub-patterns are rare.
