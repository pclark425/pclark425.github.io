Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1031

Name: Token-Level Probabilistic Reasoning for Spatial Puzzle Solving

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models solve puzzle games involving spatial knowledge, like Sudoku.

Description: This theory proposes that LLMs solve spatial puzzles by performing token-level probabilistic reasoning, where each token prediction is influenced by the likelihood of satisfying all known constraints. The model's next-token distribution is shaped by both local context (e.g., row, column, box in Sudoku) and global puzzle structure, enabling the model to incrementally build valid solutions through probabilistic inference.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Contextual Constraint Propagation (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• model → is_prompted_with → partial_puzzle_state
THEN
• model → propagates → constraints to next-token prediction

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs' token probabilities for valid moves are higher when local and global constraints are satisfied.
  2. Analysis of LLM logits shows sensitivity to constraint violations in spatial puzzles.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Contextual prediction is known, but its extension to constraint propagation in spatial puzzles is novel.

What Already Exists: LLMs use context for next-token prediction.

What is Novel: The law claims that context includes propagated spatial constraints in puzzle solving.

References:

  • Brown et al. (2020) Language Models are Few-Shot Learners [contextual prediction in LLMs]
  • Zhou et al. (2022) Can Language Models Solve Sudoku? [LLM performance on spatial puzzles]

Statement 1: Probabilistic Filtering of Invalid Moves (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• candidate_token → would_violate → puzzle_constraint
THEN
• model → assigns → lower probability to candidate_token

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Empirical studies show that LLMs assign lower probabilities to moves that would violate Sudoku rules.
  2. Sampling from LLMs produces more valid moves than random guessing, indicating probabilistic filtering.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Token probability assignment is known, but its use for constraint filtering in spatial puzzles is novel.

What Already Exists: LLMs assign probabilities to tokens based on context.

What is Novel: The law posits that this mechanism acts as a probabilistic filter for constraint satisfaction in spatial puzzles.

References:

  • Holtzman et al. (2020) The Curious Case of Neural Text Degeneration [token probability assignment]
  • Zhou et al. (2022) Can Language Models Solve Sudoku? [LLM performance on spatial puzzles]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLMs will assign lower probabilities to moves that violate spatial constraints, even in novel puzzles.
  2. Increasing the context window (e.g., with longer prompts) will improve constraint satisfaction in LLM outputs.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If LLMs are exposed to puzzles with probabilistic or 'soft' constraints, they may learn to balance constraint satisfaction probabilistically.
  2. LLMs may be able to generalize probabilistic filtering to multi-modal spatial puzzles (e.g., visual Sudoku) if given appropriate input representations.

Negative Experiments

  1. If LLMs assign high probability to constraint-violating moves, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If increasing context does not improve constraint satisfaction, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. LLMs may sometimes generate valid solutions by memorizing common patterns rather than probabilistic reasoning.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: While related to known LLM behaviors, the explicit link to probabilistic constraint filtering in spatial puzzles is new.
