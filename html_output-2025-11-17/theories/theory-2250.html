Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2250

Name: Iterative Human-AI Co-Evaluation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory proposes that the evaluation of LLM-generated scientific theories is most effective when conducted through an iterative, interactive process involving both human experts and AI systems. The process alternates between automated, large-scale screening and targeted human review, with feedback loops that refine both the evaluation criteria and the LLM's generative process.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Alternating Evaluation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM-generated theories → are_evaluated → at scale
THEN
• evaluation process → alternates → between automated screening and human expert review

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Human-in-the-loop systems outperform fully automated or fully manual systems in complex evaluation tasks.
  2. LLMs can generate large volumes of output, necessitating scalable filtering.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The general approach is known, but its structured application to LLM-generated theory evaluation is new.

What Already Exists: Human-in-the-loop and hybrid evaluation systems are established in AI and scientific peer review.

What is Novel: Formalization of alternating, iterative evaluation for LLM-generated scientific theories.

References:

  • Amershi et al. (2014) Power to the People: The Role of Humans in Interactive Machine Learning [human-in-the-loop systems]
  • Shneiderman (2020) Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence: Reliable, Safe & Trustworthy [hybrid evaluation]

Statement 1: Feedback Refinement Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation process → includes → human feedback
THEN
• evaluation criteria and LLM generation → are_refined → based on feedback

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Iterative feedback improves both model performance and evaluation accuracy in human-AI systems.
  2. Scientific peer review is an iterative process that refines both theories and evaluation standards.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The feedback principle is known, but its formalization for LLM-generated theory evaluation is new.

What Already Exists: Iterative feedback is standard in machine learning and scientific review.

What is Novel: Explicit feedback loop between human evaluation and LLM generation/evaluation criteria.

References:

  • Amershi et al. (2014) Power to the People [interactive ML]
  • Shneiderman (2020) Human-Centered AI [feedback in hybrid systems]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Iterative human-AI evaluation will yield higher-quality theory selection than either approach alone.
  2. Feedback from human reviewers will lead to measurable improvements in both LLM output and evaluation criteria over time.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. The optimal balance between automated and human review may vary by scientific domain.
  2. Feedback loops may introduce new biases or path dependencies in theory selection.

Negative Experiments

  1. If iterative human-AI evaluation does not outperform automated or manual evaluation, the theory is challenged.
  2. If feedback does not improve LLM output or evaluation accuracy, the feedback law is questioned.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not address the scalability of human review for extremely large theory sets.
