Name: Feedback Informativeness-Improvement Law

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Statement 1: Low Informativeness Feedback Yields Minimal or Erratic Improvement (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• actionable feedback → has_informativeness_level → low, and
• evaluation process → is_iterative → True
THEN
• evaluation process → improves → minimally or inconsistently

  1. Generic or unclear feedback in peer review and education is associated with minimal or unpredictable improvement.
  2. Algorithmic evaluation studies show that non-specific feedback leads to erratic or negligible change.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Providing highly informative, specific feedback to evaluators of LLM-generated scientific theories will result in greater and more consistent improvements in evaluation accuracy than providing less informative feedback.
  2. Evaluation protocols that rely on low-informativeness feedback will show minimal or unpredictable improvement over multiple iterations.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. There may be diminishing returns to informativeness, where extremely detailed feedback does not yield proportionally greater improvement.
  2. Overly informative feedback may overwhelm evaluators, leading to reduced improvement in some contexts.

Negative Experiments

  1. If low-informativeness feedback leads to substantial and consistent improvement, the theory's central claim is challenged.
  2. If highly informative feedback fails to produce greater improvement than less informative feedback, the informativeness-improvement link is questioned.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Improvement due to factors such as evaluator expertise, motivation, or external training, independent of feedback informativeness.
