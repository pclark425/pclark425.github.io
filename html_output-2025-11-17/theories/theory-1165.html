Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1165

Name: Hierarchical Preference Optimization and Adversarial Reasoning for Logical Generalization

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models can best perform strict logical reasoning.

Description: This theory proposes that robust logical reasoning in language models emerges from a hierarchical preference optimization process, where preferences are optimized at multiple levels of abstraction (from atomic logical steps to high-level reasoning strategies), combined with adversarial training using hard negatives that target both local and global reasoning errors. This multi-level approach enables models to generalize logical reasoning skills across diverse tasks and domains.

THEN

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  2. Preference optimization at different abstraction levels aligns model behavior with human reasoning strategies.

ConditionDetails

• language model → is_trained_with → hard negatives targeting both stepwise and global reasoning errors

• language model → reduces → susceptibility to adversarial reasoning attacks

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  2. Exposure to both local and global adversarial examples enhances discrimination of logical validity.
