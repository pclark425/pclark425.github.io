Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2450

Name: Activity-Projection Synergy Theory of Selective Vulnerability

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability of visual cortex neurons to amyloid-β and tau pathology is governed by the synergy between their intrinsic activity levels and the pathological state of their projection targets. Neurons with high baseline activity that project to regions with high amyloid/tau burden are most susceptible, due to increased metabolic demand and enhanced trans-synaptic spread of toxic proteins.

Statement 0: High Activity and Pathological Projections Synergistically Increase Vulnerability (qualitative)

• neuronal_population → has_high_intrinsic_activity → True, and
• neuronal_population → projects_to → region_with_high_amyloid_or_tau_pathology
THEN
• neuronal_population → has_maximal_vulnerability_to → amyloid-β and tau pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with high activity are more prone to amyloid-β and tau accumulation.
  2. Trans-synaptic spread of tau is enhanced by synaptic activity and projection to pathological regions.

Statement 1: Low Activity or Projections to Healthy Regions Confer Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_low_intrinsic_activity → True, and
• neuronal_population → projects_to → region_with_low_amyloid_or_tau_pathology
THEN

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with low activity are less prone to amyloid-β and tau accumulation.
  2. Projections to healthy regions are less likely to facilitate trans-synaptic spread.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Known individually, but their combined effect in visual cortex is new.

What Already Exists: Low activity and healthy projections are linked to resistance.

What is Novel: Explicit synergy of these factors in visual cortex vulnerability is novel.

References:

  • Bero et al. (2011) Neuronal activity regulates the regional vulnerability to amyloid-β deposition [Activity-dependent vulnerability]
  • de Calignon et al. (2012) Propagation of tau pathology in a model of early Alzheimer's disease [Trans-synaptic spread]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Visual cortex neurons with high activity and projections to pathological regions will show the earliest and most severe degeneration.
  2. Reducing activity in projection neurons or their targets will decrease vulnerability.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing activity in neurons projecting to healthy regions will not increase vulnerability unless the target becomes pathological.
  2. Blocking synaptic transmission in projections to pathological regions will reduce vulnerability even if intrinsic activity remains high.

Negative Experiments

  1. If high-activity neurons projecting to pathological regions do not show increased vulnerability, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If reducing activity or blocking projections does not alter vulnerability, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Molecular factors (e.g., gene expression, proteostasis) may independently modulate vulnerability.
