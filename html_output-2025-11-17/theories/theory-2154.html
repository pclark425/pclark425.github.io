Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2154

Name: Semantic Consensus Aggregation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how large language models (LLMs) can be used to distill theories from large numbers of scholarly papers, given a specific topic or query.

Description: This theory proposes that LLMs can distill scientific theories by aggregating semantic representations of claims, evidence, and arguments across a large number of papers. By mapping the semantic space of a field, LLMs can identify areas of consensus, controversy, and uncertainty, and synthesize these into structured theories that reflect the collective knowledge and debates within the literature.

ConditionDetails

• LLM → embeds → claims_and_evidence_from_papers, and
• embeddings → form → clusters_in_semantic_space
THEN
• LLM → identifies → areas_of_consensus_and_disagreement

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs and embedding models can cluster semantically similar claims and arguments.
  2. Semantic clustering is used to map scientific consensus and controversy.

Statement 1: Consensus Synthesis Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → identifies → high-density_clusters_of_semantically_similar_claims
THEN

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  2. Consensus synthesis is a key step in scientific review and meta-analysis.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLMs will be able to map the semantic landscape of a field and identify the main areas of consensus and controversy.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. LLMs may uncover latent consensus or emerging paradigms not yet recognized by human experts.
