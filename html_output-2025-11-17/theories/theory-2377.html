Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2377

Name: Molecular Connectivity and Extracellular Matrix Shielding Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortex neurons to Aβ/tau pathology is governed by the molecular composition of their synaptic connectivity and the properties of their local extracellular matrix (ECM). Neurons with high expression of specific cell adhesion molecules and sparse perineuronal net (PNN) coverage are more exposed to extracellular Aβ/tau aggregates, while those with dense, protective ECM structures are shielded from toxic species.

Statement 1: Dense ECM Confers Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuron → has_property → dense perineuronal net (PNN) coverage
THEN
• neuron → has_decreased_risk → Aβ/tau pathology accumulation

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Dense PNNs physically shield neurons from extracellular aggregates.
  2. Neurons with robust ECM are less likely to internalize toxic species.

Likely Classification: new

What Already Exists: Dense PNNs are known to confer neuroprotection.

What is Novel: The law's focus on ECM as a determinant of selective vulnerability to Aβ/tau in the visual cortex is novel.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Visual cortex neurons with sparse PNNs and high cell adhesion molecule expression will show early Aβ/tau pathology.
  2. Enhancing PNN density in these neurons will reduce pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially degrading PNNs in resistant neurons will increase vulnerability, even if cell adhesion molecule expression is low.
  2. Overexpressing cell adhesion molecules in PNN-rich neurons may not increase vulnerability if ECM shielding is sufficient.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding neurons with sparse PNNs and high cell adhesion molecule expression that remain resistant would challenge the theory.
  2. Observing that increasing PNN density does not confer protection would call the theory into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some neurons may be protected by intracellular mechanisms independent of ECM or adhesion molecules.
