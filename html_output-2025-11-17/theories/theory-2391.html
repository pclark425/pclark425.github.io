Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2391

Name: Intrinsic Molecular Signature Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory posits that the selective vulnerability or resistance of visual cortical neuronal populations to amyloid-β and tau pathology is determined by their intrinsic molecular and genetic profiles, including expression of proteostasis-related genes, synaptic activity patterns, and susceptibility to oxidative stress. Neurons with high expression of protective chaperones, efficient protein degradation machinery, and low baseline activity are resistant, while those with pro-aggregation profiles or high metabolic demand are vulnerable.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Single-cell transcriptomics reveals that resistant visual cortical neurons express higher levels of molecular chaperones and proteasome subunits.
  2. Experimental upregulation of proteostasis genes in vulnerable neurons confers resistance to amyloid-β and tau toxicity.
  3. Proteostasis impairment is a hallmark of vulnerable neuronal populations in Alzheimer's disease.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neuronal populations with high synaptic activity show increased amyloid-β production and tau phosphorylation.
  2. Visual cortical neurons with high metabolic demand are more susceptible to oxidative stress and protein aggregation.
  3. Reducing synaptic activity pharmacologically decreases amyloid-β and tau accumulation in animal models.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Overexpression of proteostasis genes in vulnerable visual cortical neurons will confer resistance to amyloid-β and tau pathology.
  2. Pharmacological reduction of synaptic activity in vulnerable regions will decrease pathology.
  3. Neuronal populations with low metabolic demand will show lower rates of protein aggregation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing synaptic activity in resistant populations will induce vulnerability.
  2. CRISPR-mediated knockdown of proteostasis genes in resistant populations will confer vulnerability.
  3. Manipulating oxidative stress response genes will alter vulnerability in unpredictable ways.

Negative Experiments

  1. If proteostasis gene expression does not correlate with resistance, the theory is challenged.
  2. If high synaptic activity does not increase vulnerability, the theory is weakened.
  3. If vulnerable populations are found with high proteostasis gene expression, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence
