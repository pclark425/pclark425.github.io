Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2346

Name: Proteostatic Collapse and Early Detection Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the causes of Alzheimer's disease and effective detection methods.

Description: This theory proposes that Alzheimer's disease is fundamentally a disorder of proteostasis, where the failure of protein quality control systems (autophagy, ubiquitin-proteasome, chaperones) leads to the accumulation of toxic protein aggregates (amyloid, tau, TDP-43, etc.). Early detection is possible by measuring proteostatic stress markers in CSF, blood, or via advanced imaging of protein clearance pathways.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostatic Failure Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → impaired autophagy, and
• individual → has → impaired ubiquitin-proteasome function, and
• individual → has → impaired chaperone activity
THEN
• individual → accumulates → toxic protein aggregates

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Impaired autophagy and proteasome function are observed in AD brains.
  2. Chaperone proteins are downregulated in AD and correlate with aggregate burden.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: While each system is studied, their combined failure as a central cause is a new synthesis.

What Already Exists: Protein aggregation and impaired clearance are known in AD.

What is Novel: The explicit requirement for collapse of all three proteostatic systems as a necessary cause is novel.

References:

  • Ciechanover (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin-proteasome system in neurodegeneration [proteostasis in AD]
  • Nixon (2013) The role of autophagy in neurodegenerative disease [autophagy in AD]

Statement 1: Proteostatic Stress Biomarker Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → has → early proteostatic stress
THEN
• CSF and blood markers (e.g., ubiquitin, LC3, chaperones) → are_elevated → before clinical symptoms, and
• PET imaging of protein clearance pathways → shows → reduced activity in preclinical AD

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. CSF and blood markers of proteostatic stress are elevated in early AD.
  2. PET imaging of autophagy and proteasome activity is emerging and shows early changes in AD models.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: While markers are known, their use for preclinical detection is a new proposal.

What Already Exists: Some proteostatic markers are studied in AD.

What is Novel: The law that these markers rise before symptoms and can be used for early detection is novel.

References:

  • Nixon (2013) The role of autophagy in neurodegenerative disease [autophagy in AD]
  • Ciechanover (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin-proteasome system in neurodegeneration [proteostasis in AD]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Individuals with early elevations in proteostatic stress markers will develop AD symptoms within a predictable time window.
  2. Interventions that restore proteostasis will reduce aggregate burden and slow disease progression.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Novel PET tracers for autophagy or proteasome activity will identify preclinical AD before amyloid or tau PET changes.
  2. Genetic variants in proteostasis genes will predict AD risk independently of amyloid or tau burden.

Negative Experiments

  1. If individuals with proteostatic collapse do not develop AD, the theory is challenged.
  2. If proteostatic stress markers do not precede symptoms or aggregate accumulation, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases of AD with normal proteostatic function on available assays.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This theory integrates known mechanisms into a new, proteostasis-centric framework for causation and detection.

References:

  • Ciechanover (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin-proteasome system in neurodegeneration [proteostasis in AD]
