Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1404

Name: Iterative Self-Alignment Theory of Language Model Reflection

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform self-reflection and improve their answer quality through multiple iterations of generate-then-reflect.

Description: This theory posits that language models improve answer quality through a process of iterative self-alignment, in which each reflection step acts as a feedback loop. The model compares its output to internalized standards of correctness, coherence, and relevance, and adjusts subsequent generations to better align with these standards. This process is analogous to a form of self-supervised learning occurring at inference time, leveraging the model's own knowledge and reasoning capabilities.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Internalized Standard Comparison (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language model → generates → answer, and
• language model → reflects_on → answer
THEN
• language model → compares → answer to internalized standards (correctness, coherence, relevance)

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Reflection outputs often include explicit or implicit evaluation of answer quality, referencing correctness, coherence, or relevance.
  2. Empirical studies (e.g., Reflexion, Self-Refine) show that models can self-critique and identify flaws in their own outputs.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: While self-critique is known, the idea of internalized, implicit standards guiding reflection is novel.

What Already Exists: LMs can be prompted to critique or evaluate outputs.

What is Novel: This law posits that models use internalized standards, not just explicit prompts, to guide reflection.

References:

  • Shinn et al. (2023) Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning [reflection and self-correction]
  • Madaan et al. (2023) Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback [reflection and answer revision]

Statement 1: Iterative Feedback Loop for Self-Improvement (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language model → reflects_on → answer, and
• reflection → identifies → misalignment with internalized standards
THEN
• language model → generates → revised answer with improved alignment

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Multiple-iteration reflection (e.g., in Reflexion, Self-Refine) leads to improved answer quality over time.
  2. Empirical results show that iterative self-reflection outperforms single-pass generation.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The feedback loop concept is present in some work, but the explicit link to internalized standards is novel.

What Already Exists: Iterative refinement and self-feedback are used in recent LM research.

What is Novel: The law formalizes the process as a feedback loop driven by internal standards.

References:

  • Shinn et al. (2023) Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning [reflection and self-correction]
  • Madaan et al. (2023) Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback [reflection and answer revision]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a model is allowed to reflect iteratively, answer quality will improve up to a point of diminishing returns.
  2. If internalized standards are made explicit (e.g., via prompting), the model's reflection will more closely match human evaluation criteria.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If a model's internalized standards are misaligned with human standards, iterative reflection may reinforce errors.
  2. If models are trained to develop more sophisticated internal standards, the effectiveness of reflection may increase dramatically.

Negative Experiments

  1. If iterative reflection does not improve answer quality, the feedback loop law is undermined.
  2. If models do not reference or use internalized standards during reflection, the theory is challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases where reflection leads to overfitting to spurious internal standards are not fully explained.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The theory synthesizes and extends existing work by formalizing the role of internal standards in iterative self-alignment.

References:

  • Shinn et al. (2023) Reflexion: Language Agents with Verbal Reinforcement Learning [reflection and self-correction]
  • Madaan et al. (2023) Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback [reflection and answer revision]
  • Wei et al. (2022) Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models [reasoning and self-improvement via prompting]
