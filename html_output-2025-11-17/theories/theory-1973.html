Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1973

Name: LLMs as Meta-Scientific Law Extractors

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how large language models (LLMs) can be used to distill qualitative laws from large numbers of scholarly input papers.

Description: This theory proposes that LLMs, when trained on a sufficiently broad and deep corpus of scientific literature, can extract meta-scientific laws—general principles about how scientific laws themselves are formulated, evolve, and are applied across domains. These meta-laws govern the structure, transferability, and limitations of domain-specific laws, and LLMs can make these explicit by synthesizing patterns in the language and logic of scientific discourse.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Meta-Law Extraction via Discourse Analysis (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → is_trained_on → diverse_scientific_discourse, and
• scientific_discourse → contains → patterns_of_law_formulation_and_application
THEN
• LLM → can_extract → meta-laws_about_law_structure_and_transferability

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs have demonstrated the ability to summarize and abstract scientific argumentation structures.
  2. Meta-scientific analysis (e.g., Kuhn's paradigms) has been performed by human scholars; LLMs can automate and generalize this process.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: No prior work documents LLMs extracting meta-scientific laws; this is a new theoretical proposal.

What Already Exists: Meta-scientific analysis is a human scholarly activity; LLMs have summarized argumentation but not extracted meta-laws.

What is Novel: Automated extraction of meta-laws about scientific law structure and transferability by LLMs is novel.

References:

  • Kuhn (1962) The Structure of Scientific Revolutions [Meta-scientific analysis, not LLMs]
  • Bommasani et al. (2021) On the Opportunities and Risks of Foundation Models [LLM summarization, not meta-law extraction]

Statement 1: Transferability Constraint Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → identifies → law_in_domain_A, and
• law_in_domain_A → has_structural_similarity → law_in_domain_B
THEN
• LLM → can_predict → conditions_for_law_transferability_between_domains

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs have been shown to identify analogies and structural similarities between laws in different domains.
  2. Transferability of scientific laws is a topic in philosophy of science, but not automated by LLMs.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law extends philosophical concepts to automated LLM-based synthesis, which is not previously documented.

What Already Exists: Transferability of laws is discussed in philosophy of science; LLMs have not been shown to automate this process.

What is Novel: LLMs predicting explicit conditions for law transferability is novel.

References:

  • Kuhn (1962) The Structure of Scientific Revolutions [Transferability of paradigms, not LLMs]
  • Gentner (1983) Structure-mapping: A theoretical framework for analogy [Analogy, not law transferability prediction]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLMs will identify that conservation laws in physics and accounting principles in economics share structural features and predict when such laws can be transferred or analogized.
  2. Given corpora on the evolution of scientific theories, LLMs will extract meta-laws about the conditions under which scientific laws are revised or replaced.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. LLMs may discover a meta-law predicting the rate of law evolution in rapidly advancing fields versus mature fields.
  2. A meta-law extracted by an LLM may predict the emergence of new scientific paradigms before they are recognized by the scientific community.

Negative Experiments

  1. If LLMs cannot extract any meta-laws from scientific discourse, the theory is challenged.
  2. If the predicted conditions for law transferability do not match historical cases, the theory is undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The ability of LLMs to handle non-textual or mathematical representations of laws is not addressed.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: No prior work documents LLMs extracting or predicting meta-scientific laws.

  • Kuhn (1962) The Structure of Scientific Revolutions [Meta-scientific analysis, not LLMs]
  • Bommasani et al. (2021) On the Opportunities and Risks of Foundation Models [LLM summarization, not meta-law extraction]
