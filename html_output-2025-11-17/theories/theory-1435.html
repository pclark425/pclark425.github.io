Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1435

Name: Iterative Self-Alignment Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how language models perform self-reflection and improve their answer quality through multiple iterations of generate-then-reflect.

Description: This theory posits that language models, when engaged in generate-then-reflect cycles, iteratively align their outputs with an internalized standard of answer quality. Each cycle leverages both the model's prior knowledge and the feedback from self-reflection to reduce error and bias, resulting in a convergent process toward higher-quality, more human-aligned responses. The process is governed by the interplay between the model's uncertainty, the informativeness of its self-critiques, and the diversity of its internal representations.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Iterative Error Reduction Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language model → performs → generate-then-reflect cycle, and
• reflection → provides → informative critique
THEN
• model output error → decreases → relative to prior iteration

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Empirical studies show that iterative self-reflection reduces factual and logical errors in LLM outputs.
  2. Self-Refine and similar frameworks demonstrate improved answer quality after multiple reflection cycles.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: While iterative improvement is a known concept, its application to LLM self-reflection and alignment is novel.

What Already Exists: Iterative refinement and error reduction are known in optimization and some LLM prompting strategies.

What is Novel: The explicit framing of self-reflection as an error-reducing alignment process in LLMs is new.

References:

  • Madaan et al. (2023) Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback [Iterative improvement in LLMs]
  • Wang et al. (2023) Self-Consistency Improves Chain of Thought Reasoning in Language Models [Ensembling and error reduction]

Statement 1: Convergence to Internal Quality Standard Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• language model → performs → multiple generate-then-reflect cycles
THEN
• model outputs → converge toward → internalized standard of answer quality

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Repeated self-reflection leads to diminishing changes in output, suggesting convergence.
  2. Empirical results show that after several cycles, further reflection yields minimal improvement.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law adapts convergence from optimization to the context of LLM self-reflection.

What Already Exists: Convergence is a known property in iterative algorithms.

What is Novel: The concept of an internalized quality standard as the attractor for LLM self-reflection is new.

References:

  • Madaan et al. (2023) Self-Refine: Iterative Refinement with Self-Feedback [Empirical convergence]
  • Uesato et al. (2022) Solving Math Word Problems with Process Supervision [Process supervision and convergence]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a language model is allowed to perform more generate-then-reflect cycles, the rate of improvement in answer quality will decrease over time.
  2. Reflection cycles with more informative critiques will accelerate convergence to higher-quality answers.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If the internalized standard is misaligned with human preferences, repeated reflection may entrench undesirable biases.
  2. Introducing external feedback into the reflection loop may shift the convergence point to a new quality standard.

Negative Experiments

  1. If error rates do not decrease with additional reflection cycles, the theory is challenged.
  2. If outputs do not converge (i.e., continue to change significantly after many cycles), the theory is falsified.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases where reflection cycles introduce new errors or hallucinations not present in the initial output.
