Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1589

Name: Metric Engineering for FTL Spacecraft

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how faster-than-light (FTL) spacecraft travel could be viable today using realistic scenarios and budgets.

Description: This theory posits that faster-than-light (FTL) travel can be achieved by engineering spacetime metrics around a spacecraft using currently plausible, though highly challenging, energy densities and advanced metamaterials. By locally contracting and expanding spacetime (as in the Alcubierre drive concept), a spacecraft could effectively move between two points faster than light would in normal spacetime, without violating local causality or special relativity. The theory explores how near-term advances in energy generation, negative energy density analogues, and spacetime manipulation could make such a drive feasible within realistic budgets.
