Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Superconducting and superfluid materials can maintain quantum coherence over macroscopic distances with modest energy input.

  3. Current high-energy facilities (e.g., particle accelerators, fusion reactors) can deliver energy densities comparable to those required for small-scale spacetime engineering experiments.

  1. Optimized geometric configurations of negative energy regions will require orders of magnitude less energy than previously estimated for FTL spacetime engineering.
  2. Superconducting or topologically engineered materials can be used to construct small-scale spacetime engineering testbeds with current laboratory equipment.
  3. Quantum state engineering of boundary materials will measurably reduce the energy input required to sustain negative energy regions.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. A prototype FTL spacetime device (e.g., a micro-warp bubble) can be constructed and tested in a high-energy laboratory within the next decade.
  2. Material advances will enable kilometer-scale negative energy regions to be engineered with energy budgets comparable to those of large-scale fusion projects.
  3. Quantum state engineering will allow for the dynamic control of spacetime curvature in real time, enabling on-demand FTL propulsion.

Negative Experiments

  1. If geometric optimization and quantum state engineering do not reduce energy requirements below current theoretical limits, the theory is called into question.
  2. If attempts to construct macroscopic FTL spacetime devices with current technology consistently fail, the theory's practical viability is undermined.
  3. If material quantum coherence cannot be maintained at the required scales, the theory's predictions will not be realized.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The effects of large-scale quantum coherence on gravitational stability and safety are not fully addressed.
  2. Potential unknown interactions between engineered negative energy regions and the environment (e.g., radiation, cosmic rays) are not considered.
