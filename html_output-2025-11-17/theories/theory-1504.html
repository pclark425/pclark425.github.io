Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1504

Name: Hierarchical Precision-Weighted Arbitration for Conceptual Format Selection

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the representational format of conceptual knowledge in brains at a functional (not neural) level.

Description: This theory proposes that the arbitration among representational formats for conceptual knowledge operates hierarchically: at higher levels, broad format classes (e.g., symbolic vs. sensorimotor) are selected based on precision, while at lower levels, sub-formats (e.g., visual vs. motor within sensorimotor) are further selected using the same mechanism. This enables both coarse and fine-grained adaptation to context.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Hierarchical Arbitration Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• conceptual task → requires → format selection
THEN
• arbitration → proceeds_hierarchically → from broad to specific formats, and
• at each level → format_with_highest_precision → is selected

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neuroimaging shows sequential activation from higher-order control regions to modality-specific areas during concept retrieval.
  2. Behavioral studies show that people first choose between broad strategies (e.g., verbal vs. visual) before selecting specific sub-strategies.
  3. Computational models of hierarchical arbitration in action selection support this mechanism.

Statement 1: Sub-Format Arbitration Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• broad format → is_selected → for conceptual processing, and
• sub-format → has_highest_precision → within broad format
THEN
• sub-format → dominates → conceptual processing

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Within sensorimotor formats, visual or motor sub-formats are differentially recruited depending on task demands.
  2. Neuroimaging shows that, after initial selection of a broad format, more specific modality areas are activated.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Sub-format selection is known, but its formalization as a precision-weighted hierarchical process is new.

What Already Exists: Sub-format selection within broad representational classes is observed.

What is Novel: Precision-weighted arbitration at multiple hierarchical levels is novel.

References:

  • Badre & D'Esposito (2009) Is the rostro-caudal axis of the frontal lobe hierarchical? [hierarchical control]
  • Barsalou (2008) Grounded cognition [multiple formats, context effects]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Disrupting higher-order control regions will impair broad format selection but not sub-format selection.
  2. Manipulating the precision of sub-formats (e.g., visual vs. motor) will only affect processing after the broad format is selected.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Training to increase sub-format precision will lead to reorganization of the arbitration hierarchy.
  2. In ambiguous tasks, arbitration may oscillate between hierarchical levels, leading to increased cognitive load.

Negative Experiments

  1. If sub-format selection occurs independently of broad format selection, the theory is undermined.
  2. If hierarchical neural activation is not observed during conceptual processing, the theory is undermined.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not specify how arbitration signals are communicated between hierarchical levels.
