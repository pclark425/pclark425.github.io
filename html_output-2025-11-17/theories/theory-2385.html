Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2385

Name: Network-Connectivity-Driven Selective Vulnerability Theory: Activity-Dependent Propagation

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This general theory proposes that the selective vulnerability of visual cortical neurons to Aβ and tau pathology is driven by their network connectivity in combination with their activity-dependent synaptic properties. Neurons that are both highly connected and exhibit high levels of synaptic activity are more likely to facilitate the trans-synaptic propagation of pathological proteins, leading to selective vulnerability.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Activity-Connectivity Synergy in Pathology Spread (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_high_synaptic_activity → True, and
• neuronal_population → has_high_connectivity → True
THEN
• neuronal_population → facilitates_trans_synaptic_spread_of → Aβ_and_tau_pathology, and
• neuronal_population → is_selectively_vulnerable_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Trans-synaptic spread of tau and Aβ is enhanced by neuronal activity in animal models.
  2. Highly active, highly connected neurons are early sites of pathology in Alzheimer's disease.
  3. Increased synaptic activity accelerates the propagation of misfolded proteins.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Somewhat related to existing work, but novel in its explicit, conditional formulation and focus on synergy.

What Already Exists: Activity-dependent propagation of tau and Aβ is established in animal models.

What is Novel: This law formalizes the synergy between network connectivity and activity in determining selective vulnerability.

References:

  • Wu (2016) Neuronal activity enhances tau propagation and tau pathology in vivo [Activity-dependent spread]
  • de Calignon (2012) Propagation of tau pathology in a model of early Alzheimer's disease [Trans-synaptic spread]

Statement 1: Low Activity and Connectivity Confer Resistance (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_low_synaptic_activity → True, and
• neuronal_population → has_low_connectivity → True
THEN
• neuronal_population → is_selectively_resistant_to → Aβ_and_tau_pathology

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Neurons with low activity and low connectivity are less likely to accumulate Aβ and tau pathology.
  2. Inhibitory interneurons, which often have lower activity and connectivity, are relatively spared in early Alzheimer's pathology.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: Somewhat related to existing work, but novel in its explicit, conditional, and combinatorial formulation.

What Already Exists: Relative resistance of low-activity, low-connectivity neurons is observed in neuropathological studies.

What is Novel: This law links resistance explicitly to the combination of activity and connectivity.

References:

  • Roussarie (2020) Selective Neuronal Vulnerability in Alzheimer's Disease: A Network-Based Analysis [Interneuron resistance]
  • Wu (2016) Neuronal activity enhances tau propagation and tau pathology in vivo [Activity-dependent spread]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Reducing synaptic activity in highly connected neurons will decrease their vulnerability to Aβ and tau pathology.
  2. Neurons with both low activity and low connectivity will remain resistant even in the presence of high extracellular Aβ or tau.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Artificially increasing synaptic activity in a low-connectivity neuron population will not increase vulnerability unless connectivity is also increased.
  2. Simultaneous manipulation of both activity and connectivity is required to shift a resistant population to a vulnerable one.

Negative Experiments

  1. Finding that highly active, highly connected neurons are resistant to pathology would challenge the theory.
  2. Observing that low-activity, low-connectivity neurons are among the earliest to accumulate pathology would contradict the theory.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Intrinsic molecular or genetic factors that confer vulnerability or resistance independent of activity or connectivity are not addressed.
  2. The role of glial cells and non-neuronal elements in modulating vulnerability is not considered.
