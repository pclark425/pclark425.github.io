Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2276

Name: Evaluator-LLM Co-Adaptation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory proposes that the process of evaluating LLM-generated scientific theories is inherently co-adaptive: evaluators and LLMs mutually influence each other's outputs and standards over time. This co-adaptation can lead to emergent contamination, where both parties converge on shared heuristics, biases, or expectations, potentially reducing the diversity and originality of generated theories and their evaluations.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Mutual Adaptation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → receives_feedback_from → evaluator, and
• evaluator → adjusts_criteria_based_on → LLM outputs
THEN
• LLM and evaluator → co-adapt → shared heuristics and biases

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Human-in-the-loop ML systems show mutual adaptation, with both models and users shifting behavior over time.
  2. RLHF can cause LLMs to align with evaluator preferences, while evaluators may recalibrate based on LLM output quality.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law extends co-adaptation theory to a new, high-stakes context.

What Already Exists: Co-adaptation is observed in interactive ML and human-computer interaction.

What is Novel: The formalization of co-adaptation as a source of contamination in LLM scientific theory evaluation is new.

References:

  • Amershi et al. (2014) Power to the People: The Role of Humans in Interactive Machine Learning [co-adaptation in ML]
  • Ouyang et al. (2022) Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback [RLHF and alignment]

Statement 1: Emergent Convergence Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM and evaluator → engage_in → repeated evaluation-feedback cycles
THEN
• generated theories and evaluations → converge_on → shared plausibility and originality heuristics

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Iterative feedback in RLHF and interactive ML leads to convergence on shared standards, sometimes at the expense of diversity.
  2. Empirical studies show that repeated human-model interaction can reduce output diversity.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law is a novel extension of convergence theory to this domain.

What Already Exists: Convergence in interactive systems is known, but not formalized for LLM scientific theory evaluation.

What is Novel: The explicit link to loss of diversity and originality in scientific theory generation is new.

References:

  • Ouyang et al. (2022) Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback [RLHF and bias]
  • Amershi et al. (2014) Power to the People: The Role of Humans in Interactive Machine Learning [feedback loops in ML]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Repeated evaluation-feedback cycles will result in LLM outputs and evaluator standards that are increasingly similar over time.
  2. Diversity and originality of generated theories will decrease as co-adaptation progresses.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Introducing randomization or adversarial feedback may disrupt convergence and preserve diversity.
  2. There may exist optimal co-adaptation protocols that maximize both alignment and originality.

Negative Experiments

  1. If co-adaptation does not occur despite repeated feedback, the mutual adaptation law is falsified.
  2. If diversity and originality do not decrease with repeated cycles, the emergent convergence law is challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not address the impact of multiple, independent evaluators or ensemble evaluation.
