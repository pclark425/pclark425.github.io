New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Application of intense, coherent electromagnetic fields to a vacuum region will produce measurable nonlinear QED effects and possibly transient changes in light propagation.
  2. Quantum coherence in engineered materials will enhance the stability and duration of induced topological features.
  3. Mesoscopic-scale experiments will reveal collective quantum effects not predicted by single-particle models.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Controlled creation of a micro-wormhole or spacetime shortcut will allow a test signal to traverse a region in less time than light would in normal spacetime.
  2. Emergent spacetime topology manipulation will produce observable gravitational lensing or time dilation signatures in laboratory settings.
  3. Quantum coherence may enable stabilization of topological features long enough for practical FTL transit.

Negative Experiments

  1. Failure to observe any topological or metric changes under intense, coherent fields would falsify the theory.
  2. If quantum coherence does not enhance the stability of engineered spacetime features, the theory would be called into question.
  3. If all observed effects remain within the predictions of standard QED and general relativity, the theory would be unsupported.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  2. The energy and coherence requirements for stabilizing topological features are not fully quantified.
