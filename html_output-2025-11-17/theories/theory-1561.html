Name: Positive-Energy Warp Bubble Realizability via Non-Natário-Class Metric Engineering (General Framework)

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how faster-than-light (FTL) spacecraft travel could be viable today using realistic scenarios and budgets.

Description: This theory posits that faster-than-light (FTL) travel can be achieved by engineering spacetime metrics that produce a 'warp bubble' using only positive energy densities, specifically by departing from the Natário class of metrics and leveraging advanced matter-geometry coupling. The theory suggests that, by exploiting non-Natário-class solutions to Einstein's field equations, it is possible to create a localized region of spacetime that moves superluminally relative to the external universe, while the local spacetime within the bubble remains subluminal. The approach relies on the manipulation of stress-energy tensors through advanced materials and field configurations, potentially realizable with near-future technology and realistic budgets.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a non-Natário-class metric is engineered in a laboratory setting with positive energy densities, a localized spacetime distortion consistent with a warp bubble will be observed.
  2. Advanced metamaterials with tailored electromagnetic and gravitational responses can be used to approximate the required stress-energy tensor for a small-scale warp bubble.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Scaling up the positive-energy warp bubble to macroscopic (spacecraft) sizes will remain feasible as material science advances, without encountering insurmountable quantum instabilities.
  2. The engineered warp bubble will not produce catastrophic causality violations or observable Hawking-like radiation at the bubble boundary.

Negative Experiments

  1. Attempts to engineer a non-Natário-class metric with only positive energy fail to produce any measurable spacetime distortion.
  2. Material systems designed to produce the required stress-energy tensor profile are found to be fundamentally unstable or impossible to construct.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  2. The effect of the Earth's gravitational field on small-scale warp bubbles is not considered.
