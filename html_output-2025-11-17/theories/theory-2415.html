Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2415

Name: Intrinsic Molecular Resilience and Pathway Cohesiveness Theory (General Formulation 2: Network Homeostasis Perspective)

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why certain neuronal populations within the visual cortex are selectively vulnerable or resistant to amyloid-β plaques and tau protein tangles in dementia.

Description: This theory proposes that the selective vulnerability of visual cortex neurons to Aβ and tau pathology is governed by their capacity to maintain network homeostasis under proteotoxic stress. Neurons with robust, redundant, and dynamically adaptable molecular networks (including chaperones, autophagy, and synaptic maintenance) can buffer against pathological insults, while those with rigid or poorly connected networks are prone to collapse, leading to selective degeneration.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Resilient neurons show upregulation of alternative chaperones and autophagy genes when primary pathways are inhibited.
  2. Network analysis demonstrates that resilient neurons have multiple redundant connections among protective pathways.
  3. Single-cell transcriptomics reveal that resilient neurons can compensate for loss of one pathway by upregulating others.

Statement 1: Network Rigidity Increases Susceptibility (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• neuronal_population → has_low_redundancy_in → protective_networks, and
• neuronal_population → cannot_adaptively_upregulate → protective_pathways
THEN
• neuronal_population → fails_to_maintain_homeostasis_under → Aβ_and_tau_stress, and
• neuronal_population → is_vulnerable_to → degeneration

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Vulnerable neurons lack compensatory upregulation of alternative protective pathways when stressed.
  2. Network analysis shows fewer redundant connections in these neurons.
  3. Proteomic data indicate that rigid networks are less able to adapt to proteotoxic stress.

What Already Exists: Lack of redundancy is a known risk factor for system failure in biology.

What is Novel: Direct application to selective vulnerability in the visual cortex and dementia is novel.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Neurons with higher network redundancy (as measured by network analysis of transcriptomic/proteomic data) will show less degeneration in dementia models.
  2. Inducing network rigidity (e.g., by silencing redundant protective genes) will increase vulnerability to Aβ and tau pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Pharmacological or genetic interventions that increase network redundancy will confer resistance to Aβ and tau pathology in human neurons.
  2. Network rigidity may be reversible in early stages, and restoring redundancy could halt or reverse pathology.

Negative Experiments

  1. If neurons with high network redundancy are not more resistant to pathology, the theory would be challenged.
  2. If increasing network rigidity does not increase vulnerability, the theory would be called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The influence of cell-extrinsic factors (e.g., glial support, vascular supply) is not directly addressed.
