Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2363

Name: Proteostatic Failure and Propagation Theory of Alzheimer's Disease

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the causes of Alzheimer's disease and effective detection methods.

Description: This theory proposes that Alzheimer's disease is fundamentally a disorder of proteostasis, where the failure of protein quality control systems (e.g., autophagy, ubiquitin-proteasome system) leads to the accumulation and propagation of misfolded proteins (amyloid-beta, tau) in a prion-like manner. The spread of these aggregates through neural circuits drives neurodegeneration. Early detection is possible by identifying peripheral or central markers of proteostatic stress and misfolded protein seeds.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Proteostatic Collapse Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• individual → exhibits → impaired protein quality control (e.g., reduced autophagy, proteasome activity)
THEN
• individual → accumulates → misfolded amyloid-beta and tau proteins

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. AD brains show reduced autophagy and proteasome activity.
  2. Genetic or pharmacological impairment of proteostasis in animal models accelerates amyloid and tau pathology.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The centrality of proteostatic collapse is a novel theoretical emphasis.

What Already Exists: Proteostasis impairment is recognized in AD, but not as the central, initiating event.

What is Novel: This law elevates proteostatic failure as the primary cause of misfolded protein accumulation.

References:

  • Ciechanover & Kwon (2015) Proteostasis and the ubiquitin-proteasome system in neurodegeneration [Proteostasis in AD]
  • Hara et al. (2006) Suppression of basal autophagy in neural cells causes neurodegenerative disease in mice [Autophagy and neurodegeneration]

Statement 1: Prion-like Propagation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• misfolded protein seeds → are present in → neural tissue
THEN
• misfolded protein seeds → induce → spread of pathology along neural circuits

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Experimental injection of misfolded tau or amyloid-beta seeds induces spread of pathology in animal models.
  2. Human AD brains show stereotyped progression of tau pathology along neural networks.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: The prion-like propagation model is emerging but not yet universally formalized as a law.

What Already Exists: Prion-like propagation of tau and amyloid is increasingly recognized.

What is Novel: This law formalizes prion-like spread as a core mechanism of AD progression.

References:

  • Jucker & Walker (2013) Self-propagation of pathogenic protein aggregates in neurodegenerative diseases [Prion-like spread]
  • Braak & Braak (1991) Neuropathological stageing of Alzheimer-related changes [Stereotyped progression]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Early markers of proteostatic stress (e.g., increased ubiquitinated proteins in CSF or blood) will predict AD risk.
  2. Interventions that enhance autophagy or proteasome activity will slow or prevent AD pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Peripheral detection of misfolded protein seeds will enable preclinical AD diagnosis.
  2. Blocking prion-like propagation will halt disease progression even after pathology has begun.

Negative Experiments

  1. If proteostatic impairment does not precede amyloid/tau accumulation, the theory is challenged.
  2. If blocking prion-like propagation does not slow disease, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some AD cases show minimal amyloid or tau pathology.
