Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-1924

Name: Instruction Template Dominance and Representational Overfitting Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how problem presentation format affects LLM performance.

Description: This theory posits that instruction-tuned LLMs overfit their internal representations to dominant instruction templates seen during training, leading to a form of representational bias. This overfitting causes the model to preferentially activate response pathways associated with dominant templates, even when alternative templates or semantically equivalent instructions are presented.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Representational Overfitting Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• instruction_template → is_dominant_in_training → True, and
• LLM → is_instruction_tuned → True
THEN
• LLM_internal_representation → is_overfit_to → dominant_template, and
• LLM_response_pathways → are_preferentially_activated_by → dominant_template

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs show strong performance on tasks with templates that are frequent in their instruction-tuning data, but performance degrades on rare or unseen templates.
  2. Analysis of LLM activations shows clustering by template structure rather than by task semantics.
  3. Template paraphrasing can lead to unexpected drops in accuracy, even when the underlying task is unchanged.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law extends the concept of overfitting to the representational level of instruction templates in LLMs.

What Already Exists: Overfitting to training data is a known phenomenon in machine learning.

What is Novel: The explicit claim that LLMs overfit internal representations to instruction templates, not just task content.

References:

  • Zhao et al. (2021) Calibrate Before Use: Improving Few-Shot Performance of Language Models [discusses calibration and prompt sensitivity]
  • Webson & Pavlick (2022) Do Prompt-Based Models Really Understand the Meaning of Their Prompts? [prompt sensitivity, not representational overfitting]

Statement 1: Template-Induced Response Bias Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• input_prompt → is_structurally_similar_to → dominant_template
THEN
• LLM_output → is_biased_toward → dominant_template_response_pattern

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs tend to produce responses that match the style and structure of dominant templates, even when the task could be answered differently.
  2. When given ambiguous prompts, LLMs default to the most common template-associated response.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law formalizes the mechanism by which template structure, not just content, induces output bias.

What Already Exists: Response bias due to training data distribution is known.

What is Novel: The explicit link between template structure and response bias at the output level.

References:

  • Perez et al. (2021) True Few-Shot Learning with Language Models [prompt format effects]
  • Min et al. (2022) Rethinking the Role of Demonstrations: What Makes In-Context Learning Work? [prompt structure effects]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. If a new template is introduced that is structurally similar to a dominant template, the LLM will produce responses matching the dominant template's style.
  2. If the distribution of templates in training is balanced, representational overfitting and response bias will decrease.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. If LLMs are trained with adversarially designed templates to disrupt representational overfitting, they may develop more flexible internal representations.
  2. If LLMs are probed with neuro-symbolic methods, template-induced clustering in internal activations will be observable.

Negative Experiments

  1. If LLMs do not show clustering of internal representations by template structure, the theory is challenged.
  2. If response bias does not correlate with template dominance in training, the theory is refuted.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Some LLMs with extensive data augmentation show reduced template-induced bias.

Existing Theory Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This theory extends known ML concepts to the specific context of instruction template dominance in LLMs.

References:
