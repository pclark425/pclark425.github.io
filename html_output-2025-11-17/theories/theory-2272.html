Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2272

Name: Evaluation Integrity and Contamination Theory (Information Flow Perspective)

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory posits that the integrity of LLM-generated scientific theory evaluation is determined by the control and traceability of information flow between generation, evaluation, and dissemination stages. It introduces laws about information leakage, feedback loops, and the propagation of contamination, and predicts that robust information barriers and audit trails are necessary to ensure valid scientific outcomes.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Information Leakage Contamination Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation process → receives_information_from → generation process, and
• information → is_uncontrolled → True
THEN
• evaluation outcome → is_contaminated_by → generation process

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Uncontrolled information flow between experimenters and evaluators leads to experimenter bias.
  2. Audit trails and information barriers are used in clinical trials to prevent contamination.
  3. Meta-research shows that feedback from evaluation to generation can bias subsequent theory development.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law is a novel application and formalization of information flow concepts to the LLM scientific theory evaluation context.

What Already Exists: Information leakage and experimenter bias are known in experimental design.

What is Novel: The explicit formalization of information flow laws for LLM-generated theory evaluation is new.

References:

  • Kaptchuk (2003) The double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled trial: gold standard or golden calf? [information barriers in evaluation]
  • Ioannidis (2005) Why most published research findings are false [experimenter bias and information leakage]

Statement 1: Auditability-Integrity Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation process → has_audit_trail → True
THEN
• integrity_of_evaluation → is_increased → True

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Audit trails in clinical and computational research increase trust and reproducibility.
  2. Traceable evaluation processes allow for post-hoc detection of contamination.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: closely-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law is a direct extension of auditability principles to a new context, with explicit formalization.

What Already Exists: Auditability is a recognized principle in scientific and regulatory evaluation.

What is Novel: The explicit linkage of auditability to integrity in LLM-generated theory evaluation is new.

References:

  • Goodman et al. (2016) What does research reproducibility mean? [audit trails and reproducibility]
  • Begley & Ellis (2012) Raise standards for preclinical cancer research [auditability and integrity]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLM-generated theories evaluated with full audit trails will have higher post-hoc reproducibility and lower rates of undetected contamination.
  2. Information barriers between generation and evaluation teams will reduce the incidence of evaluation bias.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Automated audit trails (e.g., blockchain-based) may outperform human-managed trails in preventing contamination.
  2. Novel forms of information leakage unique to LLM workflows may emerge, requiring new forms of auditability.

Negative Experiments

  1. If audit trails do not increase evaluation integrity, the auditability-integrity law is falsified.
  2. If information leakage does not increase contamination, the information leakage contamination law is challenged.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The impact of adversarial attacks on audit trails or information barriers is not addressed.
  2. The role of implicit, non-documented information transfer (e.g., via shared team culture) is not considered.
