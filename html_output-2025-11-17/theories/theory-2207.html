Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2207

Name: Evaluator-Process Coupling Theory for LLM-Generated Scientific Theories (Meta-Epistemic Formulation)

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory asserts that the epistemic reliability of LLM-generated scientific theories is not solely a function of the LLM's internal knowledge or the evaluator's expertise, but arises from the meta-level interaction between the generative and evaluative processes. The theory posits that the epistemic status (truth, plausibility, novelty) of LLM-generated theories is best understood as a property of the coupled system, rather than of either component in isolation.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Meta-Epistemic Coupling Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → has_internal_knowledge → K_LLM, and
• evaluator → has_domain_expertise → K_eval, and
• LLM → is_coupled_with → evaluator
THEN
• epistemic_status_of_theory → is_function_of → meta-level_interaction(K_LLM, K_eval, coupling_dynamics)

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs can generate plausible but incorrect theories without evaluator oversight; evaluators can miss novel insights without LLM generativity.
  2. Meta-level frameworks in epistemology emphasize the importance of process interaction for knowledge validation.
  3. Empirical studies show that LLM outputs are more reliable when subject to expert review, and that expert review is more productive when novel hypotheses are generated by LLMs.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law extends meta-epistemic ideas to the LLM-evaluator context, which is not previously formalized.

What Already Exists: Meta-epistemic theories exist in philosophy, and LLMs are known to have knowledge limitations.

What is Novel: The explicit modeling of epistemic status as a function of LLM-evaluator meta-level coupling is new.

References:

  • Goldman (1999) Knowledge in a Social World [meta-epistemic frameworks, but not LLMs]
  • Bender et al. (2021) On the Dangers of Stochastic Parrots [LLM knowledge limitations]

Statement 1: Epistemic Emergence Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → is_evaluated_by → multiple_evaluators, and
• evaluators → have_diverse_expertise → True
THEN
• epistemic_status_of_theory → emerges_from → collective_coupling_dynamics

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Crowdsourced evaluation and peer review can improve the epistemic reliability of scientific outputs.
  2. Diversity in evaluation leads to more robust consensus in scientific communities.
  3. Studies in collective intelligence show that group diversity and interaction structure affect the quality of scientific consensus.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: new

Explanation: This law generalizes collective epistemology to the LLM-evaluator system.

What Already Exists: Collective epistemology and peer review are established, but not in the LLM context.

What is Novel: The law that epistemic status emerges from the collective coupling of LLMs and diverse evaluators is new.

References:

  • Longino (2002) The Role of Social Interactions in Scientific Knowledge [collective epistemology]
  • Bohannon (2013) Who's Afraid of Peer Review? [peer review and evaluation]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLM-generated theories evaluated by a diverse panel of experts will have higher epistemic reliability than those evaluated by a single expert or by the LLM alone.
  2. The epistemic status of a theory will change as the composition and interaction dynamics of the evaluator group change.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. There may be emergent epistemic properties (e.g., radical novelty, paradigm shifts) that only arise from certain configurations of LLM-evaluator coupling.
  2. The system may exhibit epistemic path-dependence, where early evaluator feedback irreversibly shapes the trajectory of theory development.

Negative Experiments

  1. If the epistemic reliability of LLM-generated theories is unchanged by the diversity or interaction of evaluators, the theory is falsified.
  2. If meta-level coupling does not affect the truth or plausibility of generated theories, the theory is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. Cases where LLMs or evaluators independently produce highly reliable theories without coupling.
