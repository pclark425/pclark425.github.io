Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2202

Name: Iterative Human-AI Co-Evaluation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Description: This theory proposes that the evaluation of LLM-generated scientific theories is most robust when conducted through iterative cycles of human and AI assessment. Each cycle refines the theory by leveraging complementary strengths: AI for breadth and pattern recognition, humans for deep domain expertise and contextual judgment. The process converges toward higher-quality, more reliable scientific theories.

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Iterative Co-Evaluation Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• theory → is_generated_by → LLM, and
• evaluation_process → is_iterative → True, and
• evaluation_process → involves → [human, AI]
THEN
• theory_quality → increases_with → number_of_evaluation_cycles

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Human-in-the-loop systems improve AI outputs in scientific and technical domains.
  2. Iterative peer review and revision cycles are standard in scientific practice.
  3. AI can rapidly identify inconsistencies or gaps, while humans provide contextual and ethical judgment.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law synthesizes known practices into a formal, iterative process for LLM-generated scientific theories.

What Already Exists: Human-in-the-loop and iterative review are established in both AI and science.

What is Novel: Formalizes iterative, hybrid evaluation as a law for LLM-generated theory assessment.

References:

  • Amershi et al. (2014) Power to the People: The Role of Humans in Interactive Machine Learning [human-in-the-loop AI]
  • Bornmann (2011) Scientific peer review [iterative review in science]

Statement 1: Complementarity Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation_process → involves → [human, AI]
THEN
• evaluation_outcome → is_superior_to → human_only_or_AI_only_evaluation

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Studies show that hybrid human-AI teams outperform either alone in complex tasks.
  2. AI can process large volumes of literature, while humans can assess nuance and context.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: This law extends the complementarity principle to a new domain: LLM-generated scientific theory evaluation.

What Already Exists: Complementarity of human and AI strengths is recognized in collaborative systems.

What is Novel: Applies this principle specifically to the evaluation of LLM-generated scientific theories.

References:

  • Kamar (2016) Directions in Hybrid Intelligence: Complementing AI Systems with Human Intelligence [hybrid systems]
  • Amershi et al. (2014) Power to the People: The Role of Humans in Interactive Machine Learning [human-AI collaboration]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Iterative human-AI evaluation cycles will produce higher-quality LLM-generated theories than single-pass or single-agent evaluation.
  2. Hybrid evaluation teams will identify more errors and improvements than either humans or AI alone.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. The optimal number of evaluation cycles for maximal theory quality is unknown and may vary by domain.
  2. In some domains, human bias may reduce the benefits of hybrid evaluation.

Negative Experiments

  1. If iterative human-AI evaluation does not improve theory quality over single-pass evaluation, the theory is undermined.
  2. If hybrid teams do not outperform human-only or AI-only teams, the complementarity law is called into question.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The impact of adversarial or malicious actors in the evaluation loop is not addressed.
