Name: Systemic Contamination Propagation Theory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how to evaluate LLM-generated scientific theories.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Contamination Cascade Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation system → shares → evaluators or protocols with contaminated systems
THEN
• contamination → propagates_to → connected evaluation systems

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. In social science, shared evaluators can transmit bias across studies.
  2. Feedback loops in ML evaluation can amplify initial contamination.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law generalizes known propagation dynamics to a new domain.

What Already Exists: Contamination propagation is recognized in epidemiology and social network analysis.

What is Novel: Application to LLM evaluation systems and explicit modeling of feedback and protocol reuse.

References:

  • Christakis & Fowler (2007) The Spread of Obesity in a Large Social Network [contagion in networks]
  • Sculley et al. (2018) Winner’s Curse? On Pace, Progress, and Empirical Rigor [feedback loops in ML evaluation]

Statement 1: Compounding Bias Law (quantitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• evaluation system → undergoes → multiple rounds of contaminated evaluation
THEN
• systemic bias → increases → nonlinearly with each round

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Iterative evaluation with feedback can amplify initial biases, as seen in peer review and ML model selection.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law extends bias amplification concepts to a new, LLM-specific context.

What Already Exists: Bias amplification is known in iterative social and computational systems.

What is Novel: Quantitative modeling of compounding bias in LLM theory evaluation.

References:

  • Mehrabi et al. (2021) A Survey on Bias and Fairness in Machine Learning [bias amplification]
  • Ioannidis (2005) Why Most Published Research Findings Are False [systemic bias in science]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. Evaluation systems with shared evaluators or protocols will show correlated contamination patterns.
  2. Repeated contaminated evaluations will result in greater divergence from ground truth over time.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. The rate of contamination propagation may depend on network structure and evaluator overlap in complex ways.
  2. Novel feedback mitigation strategies may disrupt the compounding of bias.

Negative Experiments

  1. If contamination does not propagate through shared evaluators or protocols, the theory is challenged.
  2. If bias does not increase with repeated contaminated evaluation, the compounding law is falsified.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The theory does not address one-off contamination events that do not propagate.

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The theory adapts and extends network and bias propagation concepts to a new, LLM-specific context.

References:
