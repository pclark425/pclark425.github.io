Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2122

Name: LLM-Enabled Scholarly Theory Synthesis Framework

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of how large language models (LLMs) can be used to distill theories from large numbers of scholarly papers, given a specific topic or query.

Description: This theory posits that large language models (LLMs) can synthesize high-level scientific theories from large corpora of scholarly papers by leveraging their ability to semantically align, abstract, and generalize across diverse textual evidence. The LLM acts as a meta-analyst, identifying patterns, contradictions, and convergences in the literature to propose new, testable theoretical frameworks relevant to a given query.

Theory (Details)

Theory Statements

Statement 0: Semantic Pattern Generalization Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → processes → large_corpus_of_scholarly_papers, and
• papers → contain → semantically_related_findings
THEN
• LLM → abstracts → generalized_theoretical_patterns

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs have demonstrated the ability to perform semantic clustering and abstraction across diverse textual sources, as seen in multi-document summarization and meta-analytic tasks.
  2. Meta-analyses in science rely on identifying convergent findings across studies, a process LLMs can automate at scale.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law extends known human meta-analytic and LLM summarization capabilities to the automated, theory-level synthesis domain.

What Already Exists: Meta-analysis and systematic review methods exist for human-driven synthesis; LLMs have shown semantic abstraction in summarization.

What is Novel: Automated, LLM-driven synthesis of generalized theoretical patterns from large, heterogeneous corpora is novel.

References:

  • Beltagy et al. (2020) Longformer: The Long-Document Transformer [LLMs for long-context summarization]
  • Ioannidis (2016) The Mass Production of Redundant, Misleading, and Conflicted Systematic Reviews and Meta-analyses [Meta-analysis in science]

Statement 1: Contradiction and Convergence Detection Law (qualitative)

ConditionDetails
IF
• LLM → analyzes → multiple_papers_on_topic, and
• papers → contain → conflicting_or_convergent_evidence
THEN
• LLM → identifies → areas_of_theoretical_contradiction_or_convergence, and
• LLM → proposes → refined_or_novel_theoretical_frameworks

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. LLMs can perform contradiction detection and evidence aggregation, as shown in claim verification and fact-checking tasks.
  2. Scientific progress often arises from resolving contradictions and synthesizing convergent evidence.

Existing Law Comparison

Likely Classification: somewhat-related-to-existing

Explanation: The law builds on existing NLP and scientific synthesis methods, but applies them in a novel, automated, theory-building context.

What Already Exists: Contradiction detection and evidence aggregation are known in NLP and scientific practice.

What is Novel: Automated, LLM-driven identification and synthesis of theoretical contradictions/convergences at scale is novel.

References:

  • Thorne et al. (2018) FEVER: a large-scale dataset for Fact Extraction and VERification [Claim verification with LLMs]
  • Kuhn (1962) The Structure of Scientific Revolutions [Role of contradiction in theory change]

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  1. LLMs will be able to generate high-level theoretical summaries that align with or extend existing human meta-analyses.
  2. LLMs will identify both consensus and points of contention in the literature, enabling more targeted hypothesis generation.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. LLMs may synthesize entirely novel theoretical frameworks that have not been previously articulated by human experts.
  2. LLMs could reveal latent, cross-disciplinary theoretical connections that are not apparent to domain specialists.

Negative Experiments

  1. If LLMs fail to produce coherent or accurate theoretical syntheses from large corpora, the theory is challenged.
  2. If LLM-generated theories do not align with or meaningfully extend human meta-analyses, the theory is falsified.

Unaccounted for Evidence

  1. The impact of domain-specific jargon and implicit assumptions on LLM synthesis accuracy is not fully addressed.
  2. Potential for LLMs to propagate or amplify existing biases in the literature is not explicitly modeled.
