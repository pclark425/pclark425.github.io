Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-2322

Name: Dynamic Peripheral-Central Biomarker Flux Theory: Systemic Modulation Subtheory

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of the causes of Alzheimer's disease and effective detection methods.

Description: This subtheory proposes that systemic factors—such as vascular health, sleep, inflammation, and peripheral organ function—modulate the rate and directionality of CNS-peripheral biomarker flux. These systemic influences can either exacerbate or mitigate Alzheimer's disease risk and progression by altering the efficiency of clearance pathways (e.g., glymphatic, hepatic, renal) and transporter expression/function. Thus, interventions targeting systemic health can indirectly affect both CNS pathology and the reliability of peripheral biomarker-based diagnostics.

  2. Peripheral 'sink' effect influences CNS-to-periphery biomarker gradients.

New Predictions (Likely outcome)

  2. Patients with liver or kidney dysfunction will have elevated peripheral biomarker levels, decoupled from CNS pathology.

New Predictions (Unknown outcome/high-entropy)

  1. Targeted interventions to enhance peripheral sink capacity will lower CNS amyloid/tau by increasing flux.
  2. Systemic anti-inflammatory therapies will indirectly reduce AD risk by restoring biomarker flux efficiency.

Negative Experiments

Unaccounted for Evidence
