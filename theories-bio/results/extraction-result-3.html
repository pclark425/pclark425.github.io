name_full Conformational Space Annealing
brief_description A global optimization/metaheuristic for protein conformational search that maintains and evolves a diverse bank of minimized conformations, gradually focusing search by reducing a similarity cutoff (D_cut) to sample many distinct low-energy families.
citation_title Energy-based de novo protein folding by conformational space annealing and an off-lattice united-residue force field: Application to the 10-55 fragment of staphylococcal protein A and to apo calbindin D9K
mention_or_use use
algorithm_name Conformational Space Annealing (CSA)
algorithm_description CSA maintains a bank (population) of energy-minimized conformations initialized from a set of randomly generated minimized structures. In each iteration a subset of bank conformations (seeds) is selected (preferring diversity); each seed is modified by replacing contiguous segments of its variables (torsions/angles) with corresponding segments from other bank members or the initial bank to produce trial conformations. Each trial is locally energy-minimized independently (highly parallelizable). For each trial α, the algorithm finds the closest bank conformation A using a distance metric D (sum of absolute differences of variable angles). If D_{αA} < D_cut (current similarity threshold), α replaces A only if its energy is lower; if D_{αA} > D_cut and α's energy is lower than the highest-energy bank member B, α replaces B. The search is progressive: D_cut is initialized large to cover conformational space, then gradually reduced (annealed) so the search narrows to low-energy regions. Rounds repeat until seeds exhausted; additional random minimized conformations can be injected if needed.
algorithm_type global optimization / stochastic metaheuristic (population-based)
is_deterministic False
underlying_principles Global energy minimization under the thermodynamic hypothesis (Anfinsen); population-based exploration with controlled diversity; local energy minimization combined with sampling of torsional space; uses a distance metric over conformation variables to maintain diversity.
force_field_or_energy_function Used with UNRES in this paper; previously tested with ECEPP/3 in earlier work (referenced).
computational_complexity Not stated as formal complexity; highly parallelizable because local minimizations are independent. Practical runtimes reported: for the 46-residue protein A CSA found a proposed GMEC after average ~14 hours on 32 IBM SP2 processors (one run: 12 h to get E = -157.103 kcal/mol; a follow-up found -157.347 kcal/mol in 8 h). An average CSA run took ~80 minutes on 32 processors to reach energies comparable to best MCM runs. Earlier tests (with ECEPP/3) found GMEC of [Met5]enkephalin in 36 s using 16 processors.
protein_types_tested Applied here to a 46-residue three-helix bundle (10-55 fragment of protein A) and a 75-residue four-helix bundle (apo calbindin D9K); previously tested on small peptides (e.g., pentapeptide, melittin fragment) with other force fields.
performance_metrics Found proposed GMEC for protein A with energy E = -157.347 kcal/mol; Cα RMSD of GMEC from NMR native = 3.8 Å; other native-like conformations found with RMSD as low as 2.1 Å (energy -155.164 kcal/mol, ~2.2 kcal/mol above GMEC). For apo calbindin D9K CSA produced a mirror-image lowest-energy conformation with Cα RMSD 8.7 Å (whole), 4.8 Å over residues 22–75; found native-like family member with Cα RMSD 4.4 Å (energy ~1 kcal/mol higher than that mirror-image minimum); best RMSD found 3.9 Å but ~7 kcal/mol higher in energy than lowest-energy conformation. Also reported positive correlation (r = 0.83) between UNRES energy and RMSD in final bank for protein A.
benchmark_datasets This paper: two specific proteins from the PDB/NMR literature (10-55 fragment of protein A; apo calbindin D9K PDB ID 1CLB). Earlier CSA tests referenced on standard peptide test cases (e.g., [Met5]enkephalin, melittin fragment) and ECEPP/3 force field.

is_interpretable True
key_assumptions Assumes energy surface can be explored effectively by population-based sampling plus local minimization; diversity maintenance via D_cut enables coverage of distinct low-energy families; assumes the employed energy function meaningfully ranks native-like conformations.
limitations As a search method, its success depends on the accuracy of the force field; may find mirror-image families or multiple low-energy folds due to force-field approximations; computational cost scales with protein size and required local minimizations (though amenable to parallelization). Finding exact GMEC for much larger proteins is much harder than finding native-like low-energy conformations.

CSA found -157.347 kcal/mol (GMEC) and reached comparable energies more quickly (about 80 min on 32 processors to reach -154.98 kcal/mol). CSA also consistently converged to the same low-energy folds across independent runs, unlike MCM which was less consistent.

algorithm_name UNRES force field (united-residue mean-field energy function)
algorithm_description UNRES models a polypeptide as a virtual-bond chain of Cα atoms with associated united side chains (SC) and peptide groups (p) placed between consecutive Cα atoms. All virtual-bond lengths are fixed; primary variables are virtual-torsional angles γ plus variable bond and side-chain angles. The total energy U is a weighted sum of terms: side-chain—side-chain mean free energy (hydrophobic/hydrophilic, implicit-solvent contributions), excluded-volume side-chain–peptide interactions (U_{SC p}), peptide-group interaction potential (U_{p p}, accounting for electrostatics and backbone hydrogen bonding), torsional energies (U_{tor}), bond-angle bending (U_b), side-chain rotamer energy (U_{rot}), and a multibody cooperative correction term (U_{corr}). Weights (w) were calibrated by optimizing Z-score of a calibration protein and by Boltzmann inversion of distribution/correlation functions from a dataset of 195 high-resolution nonhomologous PDB structures.
