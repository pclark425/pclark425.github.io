quantitative_difference Peak timing: humans — peak expression/protein/synaptic density around ~4–8 years (mRNA module 1 peaks ~5 yr; protein SYP/DLG4 peak 4–8 yr; electron microscopy synaptic density peak ~4 yr); chimpanzees and macaques — peak within first year of life (shortly after birth, <1 yr). Exact synapse-per-area counts are plotted in the paper (synaptic density reported per 100 μm^2) but raw numeric densities are not provided in the main text; statistical tests show human infant (0–2 mo) PFC synaptic density is significantly lower than in chimpanzees and macaques (one-sided Wilcoxon P = 0.016 and P = 0.018 respectively).
evolutionary_explanation Authors propose the delay is an evolutionary change on the human lineage (after divergence from chimpanzees) that may have been positively selected to extend a period of cortical plasticity for social/cultural learning during an extended childhood; positive-selection signature upstream of MEF2A suggests recent selection on regulatory elements linked to this timing change and possibly after the human–Neanderthal split.
functional_purpose Extending the period of synaptogenesis in PFC may prolong cortical plasticity during early childhood (approx. 3–7 yr), facilitating extended social learning, cultural transmission, development of higher-order cognitive functions (abstract thinking, social behavior, self-regulation), and possibly sustaining higher synaptic activity in adulthood.
evidence_type Comparative mRNA microarray time series, RNA-seq time series, metabolite measurements (GC–MS), Western blot, label-free proteomics, and direct ultrastructural quantification by transmission electron microscopy (synapse counts); statistical time-shift analysis (dynamic time warping).
human_specific_or_enriched False
