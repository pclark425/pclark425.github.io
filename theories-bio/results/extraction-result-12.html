name_short Nested sampling
name_full Nested sampling (Skilling 2006)
brief_description A Bayesian sampling algorithm that explores likelihood-constrained regions of parameter space by iteratively removing the lowest-likelihood samples from an active set and replacing them with uniformly sampled points above the current likelihood threshold; it provides estimates of the evidence (partition function) and posterior samples that can be reweighted to compute thermodynamic observables at any temperature.
citation_title Nested sampling for general Bayesian computation
mention_or_use use
algorithm_name Nested sampling
algorithm_description Initialize an active set of K samples drawn from the prior. Repeatedly: (1) identify and remove the active point with smallest likelihood L*; (2) estimate the fraction X of prior mass remaining (X_i ≈ α^i with α≈exp(-1/K) or K/(K+1)); (3) generate a replacement sample uniformly from the prior conditional on L(θ) > L* (in practice via short constrained Markov-chain Monte Carlo starting from a random active point); (4) record removed points and associated X-weights to numerically integrate Z = ∫_0^1 L(X) dX. Posterior weights χ_n = (X_{n-1}-X_n) L_n / Z permit calculation of expectation values (e.g., thermodynamic averages) at any β by Boltzmann reweighting. The paper also describes a parallelization that removes the P lowest-likelihood points per iteration and replaces them on P processors, adjusting α accordingly.
algorithm_type Bayesian sampling / Monte Carlo sampling
is_deterministic False
underlying_principles Bayesian evidence estimation; mapping partition function/integral to 1D integral of likelihood vs prior-mass L(X); Boltzmann statistics for thermodynamics; free-energy estimation via evidence; reduction of high-dimensional sampling to nested likelihood-constrained sampling.
force_field_or_energy_function Algorithm-agnostic; in this paper it is applied to an extended Gō-type empirical potential (see 'Extended Gō-type model' entry) and the Boltzmann weight exp(-βE) is used as the likelihood.
computational_complexity Cost dominated by number of MC moves: total MC moves ≈ m × K (m = MC steps per nested iteration). Number of nested iterations ∝ K; memory O(K) for active set. Example from paper: protein G used K=20,000, m=15,000 → ~1.38×10^11 MC moves and ≈22 hours on 32 Xeon X5650 CPUs. Practical complexity scales linearly with K and with the MC cost per move.
protein_types_tested Small proteins / peptides in this work: 16-residue polyalanine β-hairpin, protein G (56 residues, PDB 1PGA), Src SH3 domain (PDB 1SRL, ~60 residues), chymotrypsin inhibitor 2 (PDB 2CI2, ~64 residues).
performance_metrics Reported metrics: expectation value of RMSD at β=1 for protein G E(RMSD|β=1)=3.21 Å; example room-temperature conformers with backbone RMSDs 1.93 Å, 2.96 Å, 3.97 Å, 5.22 Å and energies -189 to -191 (units of RT). Heat capacity curves for the 16-residue hairpin computed and shown to agree with parallel tempering (continuous C_v curve from single nested-sampling run vs discrete temperatures for parallel tempering). Convergence criterion: Z(β) convergence at β=1.
benchmark_datasets PDB test proteins used in paper: 1PGA (protein G), 1SRL (SH3), 2CI2 (chymotrypsin inhibitor 2), and a designed 16-residue polyalanine hairpin.
requires_templates False
is_interpretable True
key_assumptions The likelihood/prior formulation (Boltzmann factor) holds; replacement samples can be drawn uniformly from the prior conditional on L>L* (practically via constrained MC); the active set size K controls the minimum resolvable basin (modes smaller than ~1/K are unlikely to be explored); shrinkage of prior mass between iterations approximated by α ≈ exp(-1/K) (or K/(K+1)).
limitations Sampling of disconnected low-energy regions requires sufficiently large K (modes with relative prior volume < 1/K are unlikely to be discovered); constrained MC cannot jump between disconnected components at fixed L*, so exploration may miss narrow modes without large K or nonlocal moves; computationally expensive (cost ∝ mK); choice of metric for topological analysis (RMSD) can affect landscape reproducibility; sensitive to K and m settings.
comparison_with_other_methods Compared with parallel tempering and other methods in text: nested sampling gives continuous thermodynamic observables at all temperatures from one run (no need for multiple-temperature simulations), and is argued to be superior for systems with first-order-like transitions; for the 16-residue hairpin nested sampling and parallel tempering produced similar heat capacity curves (both used same total MC moves), but nested sampling avoids having to choose temperature ladders; nested sampling is better at locating small low-energy regions and crossing large energy barriers (e.g., double-funnel in protein G) where parallel tempering and simulated annealing can become trapped.

brief_description A topological visualization method that uses nested-sampling output samples and a nearest-higher-energy connectivity graph to identify basins and plot a landscape chart whose width at a given energy is proportional to accumulated prior or posterior weights, revealing funnel structure and basin probabilities.
citation_title Efficient sampling of atomic configurational spaces
mention_or_use use
algorithm_name Energy landscape charting via topological analysis
algorithm_description Compute a distance metric (here backbone+side-chain non-hydrogen RMSD) between all nested-sampling sample pairs. Build a directed graph connecting each sample to its k nearest neighbors with higher energy (k chosen e.g., 15). Iteratively add nodes from lowest to higher energy, tracking disconnected subgraphs; define basins at splitting events and compute relative basin widths from sums of prior weights ω_n (or posterior weights χ_n at given β). Apply horizontal exponential scaling of energy to improve visualization. Optionally prune basins below a probability threshold (e.g., <1/1000).
algorithm_type visualization / topological analysis
is_deterministic True
underlying_principles Topology of sample connectivity under an RMSD metric, phase-space volume weighting (prior ω_n) and posterior reweighting χ_n = ω_n e^{-βE_n}/Z to display thermally relevant basins; energy-ordered graph construction reveals basin splitting.
force_field_or_energy_function Analysis tool independent of force field; in this paper used to analyze outputs from nested sampling with the extended Gō-type potential.
computational_complexity Graph construction requires computing pairwise distances O(N^2) naive (or O(N log N) with approximate nearest neighbors); connectivity building O(N·k); visualization complexity proportional to number of samples. The paper used k=15 and pruned small basins.
protein_types_tested Applied to nested-sampling outputs for polyalanine hairpin, protein G, SH3 domain, CI2.
performance_metrics Used to identify double-funnel topology in protein G, splitting energies (e.g., funnels 1 vs 2 split at energies varying between 220 and 580 units across simulations; funnel 1 vs 1A split at ~-75 to -55 units), and to show which basins are thermally accessible at β=1.
