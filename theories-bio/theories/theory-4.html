Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-4

Name: Kinetostatic Energy Minimization Theory

Type: specific

Theory Query: Build a theory of how proteins can be folded algorithmically and deterministically without using black-box machine learning solutions, based on the following results.

Description: Protein folding can be treated as a quasi-static energy minimization problem where the backbone is modeled as rigid peptide linkages connected by torsional hinges (dihedral angles), and iterative updates of dihedral angles driven by torques from interatomic forces lead to progressive energy minimization toward local minima representing folded states. The theory posits that neglecting inertial effects (kinetostatic approximation) is justified when folding timescales >> vibrational periods, enabling deterministic optimization algorithms. Success depends on: (1) appropriate force field capturing electrostatics and van der Waals interactions, (2) efficient Jacobian mapping from Cartesian forces to joint torques, (3) stable step-size selection balancing convergence speed and numerical stability, and (4) global optimization strategies to escape local minima.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Kinetostatic Approximation Validity

Statement: For protein folding problems where barrier-crossing timescales (microseconds-milliseconds) are 10⁶-10⁹ times longer than bond vibrational periods (femtoseconds), inertial terms can be neglected and dynamics approximated as overdamped or quasi-static motion along the energy gradient, reducing 6N-dimensional phase space to 3N-dimensional configuration space optimization.

Domain/Scope: Applies to conformational search and energy minimization for proteins in implicit solvent where explicit dynamics are not required; not suitable for studying kinetics or dynamic properties.

Special Cases

  1. Breaks down for high-frequency vibrational modes if time-stepping is too coarse
  2. Not appropriate for studying dynamic properties like protein-ligand encounter rates
  3. May miss rare events requiring thermal activation if used as pure optimization

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. KCM treats protein as rigid peptide planes with torsional hinges, neglecting inertia (kinetostatic assumption), enabling energy-minimization-based folding [e9.2] [e6.5]
  2. Protofold KCM approach successfully finds low-energy conformations through successive kinetostatic compliance without explicit dynamics [e6.5] [e9.2]
  3. Sign gradient descent on KCM converges to local free-energy minima in ~600 iterations for small backbone chains [e9.4]
  4. LQR-based CG optimization uses overdamped limit (mass neglected) to synthesize harmonic approximations of local energy wells [e2.1] [e2.7]

Statement 1: Sign-Gradient Descent Convergence

Statement: Updating dihedral angles via θ_{k+1} = θ_k + κ_k·sgn(τ(θ_k)) where τ is the torque vector and κ_k is an adaptively decaying step-size (e.g., geometric decay with β₀∈(0,1)) provides asymptotic convergence to local energy minima under neighborhood sign-consistency conditions (θ*-θ_k)ᵀsgn(τ(θ_k))>0, with 2-3x faster convergence and improved numerical stability compared to normalized-torque heuristics.

Domain/Scope: Applies to deterministic energy minimization on smooth energy landscapes where gradient information is available; demonstrated for small backbone chains with 15-32 dihedral angles.

Special Cases

  1. Convergence guarantees are local and depend on neighborhood sign-consistency condition
  2. Requires step-size sequence satisfying specific decay conditions from sign-descent theory
  3. May not converge if started far from any local minimum or in highly frustrated regions

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Sign gradient descent KCM with adaptive step-size converged to lower free energy in 600 iterations vs 1500 for conventional normalized-torque method [e9.4] [e9.3]
  2. SGD-based update provides formal convergence conditions via sign-descent theory (Moulay et al.) unlike heuristic normalized-torque [e9.4]
  3. Conventional KCM iteration acts like steepest descent but normalization by infinity-norm limits step size, requiring smaller fixed steps [e9.3] [e6.8]
