Extraction Schema Details for extraction-schema-7

Extraction Schema (General Information)

Schema ID: extraction-schema-7

Extraction Query: Extract any mentions of population differences in the prevalence of activating EGFR mutations in lung cancer and proposed reasons for higher frequency in East Asians. Include quantitative evidence about mutation frequencies by ancestry, smoking status, sex, and age; germline variants and their allele frequencies/associations; environmental and hormonal exposures; mutational signatures and tumor mutation burden; co-mutation patterns; and differential response to EGFR-TKIs by ethnicity. Capture study design, methods, and any counter-evidence or limitations.

Field Name Type Description

egfr_mutation_frequency_by_group str Prevalence of activating EGFR mutations stratified by ancestry (e.g., East Asian vs European), sex, age, smoking status; include numbers and percentages if reported.

difference_persists_in_never_smokers bool Did the higher EGFR mutation frequency in East Asians persist among never smokers, according to this study? (true/false/null).
difference_persists_after_adjustment bool Did the difference persist after adjustment for sex, age, smoking, histology, and other factors? (true/false/null).
egfr_mutation_subtypes_distribution str Distribution of EGFR activating mutation subtypes (e.g., exon 19 deletions, L858R, G719X, L861Q, exon 20 insertions) overall and by ancestry if reported.
co_mutation_landscape str Co-mutations and mutual exclusivity patterns (e.g., TP53, KRAS, STK11, KEAP1) and whether these differ by ancestry or smoking status.
tumor_mutational_burden_and_spectrum str TMB values, base substitution spectra, and comparisons across ancestry/smoking groups if reported.
mutational_signatures str COSMIC mutational signatures identified in EGFR-mutant tumors and controls (e.g., smoking Signature 4 absence/presence, APOBEC 2/13, others) and any ancestry-specific differences.
environmental_exposures_assessed str Environmental exposures evaluated (e.g., cooking oil fumes, indoor coal burning, air pollution/PM2.5, radon, secondhand smoke, occupational exposures, prior tuberculosis scars) with associated risk estimates for EGFR-mutant lung cancer if available.
hormonal_factors str Hormone-related factors (e.g., estrogen exposure, ER/ERβ expression, hormone therapy) and associations with EGFR-mutant status or prevalence by sex/ancestry.
germline_variants_and_haplotypes str Specific germline variants/haplotypes associated with EGFR-mutant lung cancer or EGFR expression (e.g., EGFR promoter rs712829/-216G>T, rs712830/-191C>A, intron 1 CA-SSR1 repeat length, TERT/CLPTM1L/TP63 loci), with allele frequencies by ancestry and effect sizes.
inherited_egfr_pathogenic_variants str Reports of inherited EGFR variants predisposing to lung cancer (e.g., germline T790M), their prevalence and any ancestry enrichment.
gene_environment_interactions str Evidence of interactions between germline variants and environmental exposures or sex/smoking in determining EGFR-mutant risk.
testing_or_selection_bias str Potential biases affecting observed frequencies (e.g., differences in molecular testing rates, case mix, stage at diagnosis, referral bias) and how addressed.
egfr_tki_response_by_ethnicity str Clinical outcomes of EGFR-TKI therapy stratified by ethnicity (e.g., ORR, PFS, OS), and whether differences remain after controlling for mutation subtype and covariates.
methods_key_details str Key methodological details relevant to interpretation (e.g., sequencing platform, targeted vs WES/WGS, mutation calling criteria, ancestry inference method, statistical adjustments).
mechanistic_hypotheses_proposed str Authors' proposed biological or environmental mechanisms explaining higher EGFR mutation prevalence in East Asians (concise summary).
counter_evidence_or_null_results str Any results that contradict common hypotheses (e.g., no association with cooking fumes after adjustment, no ancestry effect among never-smokers).
limitations_and_conflicts str Study limitations and any disclosed conflicts of interest or funding sources that may influence interpretation.
notable_quotes str Short verbatim quotes (1–2 sentences) directly supporting claims about ancestry differences or their causes, with page/section reference if available.
