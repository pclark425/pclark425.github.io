Paper Title: Trajectory-based training enables protein simulations with accurate folding and Boltzmann ensembles in cpu-hours

Paper Venue: PLoS Comput. Biol.

Paper TL;DR: This work uses the contrastive divergence technique from machine learning to train from simulations of 450 proteins, and applies it to the recently developed Upside model, which provides a starting point both for investigation of folding dynamics and as an inexpensive Bayesian prior for protein physics.

Paper Abstract: An ongoing challenge in protein chemistry is to identify the underlying interaction energies that capture protein dynamics. The traditional trade-off in biomolecular simulation between accuracy and computational efficiency is predicated on the assumption that detailed force fields are typically well-parameterized, obtaining a significant fraction of possible accuracy. We re-examine this trade-off in the more realistic regime in which parameterization is a greater source of error than the level of detail in the force field. To address parameterization of coarse-grained force fields, we use the contrastive divergence technique from machine learning to train from simulations of 450 proteins. In our procedure, the computational efficiency of the model enables high accuracy through the precise tuning of the Boltzmann ensemble. This method is applied to our recently developed Upside model, where the free energy for side chains is rapidly calculated at every time-step, allowing for a smooth energy landscape without steric rattling of the side chains. After this contrastive divergence training, the model is able to de novo fold proteins up to 100 residues on a single core in days. This improved Upside model provides a starting point both for investigation of folding dynamics and as an inexpensive Bayesian prior for protein physics that can be integrated with additional experimental or bioinformatic data.

brief_description A trajectory-based parameter optimization procedure that updates energy-function parameters to reduce differences between ensembles sampled near the native (restrained) structure and unrestrained (free) simulations, approximating gradient descent on the free-energy difference.
citation_title On Contrastive Divergence Learning
mention_or_use use
algorithm_name Contrastive divergence optimization for force-field parameterization
algorithm_description Two ensembles are generated per minibatch: (1) native-restrained ensemble (structures restrained within ~1 Å Cα-RMSD of crystal) and (2) free ensemble (short unrestrained dynamics). For each parameter α_i, the update is α_{t+1} = α_t + (ε/M) Σ_a (⟨dV/dα_i⟩_restrained - ⟨dV/dα_i⟩_free), where ensemble averages are taken over the sampled trajectories. Small parameter updates are applied iteratively over minibatches (here 38 minibatches of 12 proteins each) until no parameter can further reduce the difference; replica exchange is used during sampling to improve barrier crossing. The free-ensemble sampling is short (often insufficient for global equilibration), so updates reflect local basin differences.
algorithm_type parameter optimization / statistical-learning-driven force-field training (sampling-based)
is_deterministic False
underlying_principles Approximate maximum-likelihood / minimum Kullback–Leibler divergence between model and ‘data’ ensembles; free-energy derivative estimation via sampled trajectories (Boltzmann ensembles).
force_field_or_energy_function Used to fit parameters of the Upside energy function (spline-based pairwise terms, environment terms, hydrogen-bond strength, Ramachandran terms).
computational_complexity Uses many short MD trajectories per parameter update; practical runtime: free-ensemble trajectories used are 5000 time units (~10 minutes wall-clock) with the first half discarded; training cycles performed over 38 minibatches of 12 proteins each with multiple passes and step-size annealing.
protein_types_tested Applied during parameterization using a training set of 456 proteins (divided into minibatches).
performance_metrics Converged training reduced minibatch-averaged RMSD from ~6 Å to ~3 Å over optimization; parameters stabilized to yield improved folding and ensemble properties.
benchmark_datasets 456-protein training set (from PDB) used in this study; validation on 23 benchmark proteins and CASP11 targets.
requires_templates False
is_interpretable True
key_assumptions Short (local) trajectories around the native basin provide sufficient signal to balance competing energy terms; ensemble differences estimated from finite-time sampling approximate free-energy derivatives well enough for parameter updates.
limitations Because the free-ensemble simulations are short, training mainly optimizes near-native basin behavior and may not correct deficiencies that manifest far from the native state; continuing optimization to convergence can overfit to near-native fluctuations and reduce performance; requires careful step-size control to avoid parameter oscillations.
