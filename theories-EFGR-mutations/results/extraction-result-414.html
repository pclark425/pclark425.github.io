Paper ID: paper-2294275

Paper Title: Mutations in the epidermal growth factor receptor gene are linked to smoking-independent, lung adenocarcinoma

Paper Abstract: Epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) mutations are a potential predictor of the effectiveness of EGFR inhibitors for the treatment of lung cancer. Although EGFR mutations were reported to occur with high frequency in nonsmoking Japanese adenocarcinoma patients, the exact nature has not been fully elucidated. We examined EGFR gene mutations within exons 18–21 and their correlations to clinico-pathological factors and other genetic alterations in tumour specimens from 154 patients who underwent resection for lung cancer at Kyoto University Hospital. Epidermal growth factor receptor mutations were observed in 60 tumours (39.0%), all of which were adenocarcinoma. Among the patients with adenocarcinoma (n=108), EGFR mutations were more frequently observed in nonsmokers than former smokers or current smokers (83.0, 50.0, 15.2%, respectively), in women than men (76.3 vs 34.0%), in tumours with bronchio-alveolar component than those without bronchio-alveolar component (78.9 vs 42.9%), and in well or moderately differentiated tumours than poorly differentiated tumours (72.0, 64.4, 34.2%). No tumours with EGFR mutations had any K-ras codon 12 mutations, which were well-known smoking-related gene mutations. In conclusion, adenocarcinomas with EGFR mutation had a distinctive clinico-pathological feature unrelated to smoking. Epidermal growth factor receptor mutations may play a key role in the development of smoking-independent adenocarcinoma.

egfr_mutation_subtypes_distribution Exon 19 deletions were the single largest category (34/154, 21.9%), multiple distinct 15-bp deletions around codons 746-750 (type 1 series with start point E746 most frequent); L858R (exon21) 22/154 (14.2%); exon18 point mutations (G719A, S720F) 2/154 (1.3%); exon20 insertions/duplications/point events 3/154 (1.9%). Authors note exon19 deletion start-site distribution differs from some US series (E746 vs L747) but functional sensitivity to TKIs appears similar per literature.
co_mutation_landscape K-ras codon12 mutations observed in 10/154 (6.5%), all in smokers; K-ras mutations were mutually exclusive with EGFR mutations (no tumour harboured both; negative correlation P=0.001). p53 mutations (exons5-8) in 51/154 (33.1%) with no statistical correlation to EGFR mutation status. Promoter hypermethylation frequencies: p16 64/154 (41.6%), RASSF1A 78/154 (50.6%), APC1A 82/154 (53.2%); none correlated with EGFR mutation status.
tumor_mutational_burden_and_spectrum Not reported (no TMB values or base-substitution spectra provided).
mutational_signatures Not analysed or reported; authors speculate a possible DNA-editing agent/mechanism that could produce both deletions and point mutations in the EGFR tyrosine-kinase domain but provide no signatures.
environmental_exposures_assessed Cigarette smoking was assessed (never/former/current and pack-years). Strong inverse association between smoking and EGFR mutations: among adenocarcinomas nonsmokers >> former >> current smokers (percentages given above). No other environmental exposures (cooking fumes, indoor coal, radon, air pollution, TB scarring, secondhand smoke) were assessed in this study.
hormonal_factors No direct hormonal exposure data collected. Female sex had higher crude EGFR mutation frequency (76.3% vs 34.0%) but gender was strongly correlated with smoking (most males were smokers); logistic regression found absence of smoking history, not female sex, independently associated with EGFR mutations (OR 11.4, 95% CI 2.7-47.3, P<0.001). Authors discuss social differences in smoking as a likely explanation for female predominance but did not test estrogen/hormone-related hypotheses.
germline_variants_and_haplotypes No germline-association analysis performed. Corresponding nonmalignant lung tissue was tested in only six cases with tumour EGFR mutations and no EGFR mutations were detected in nonmalignant tissue (supporting somatic origin in those cases). No allele frequencies or germline SNP/haplotype associations reported.
inherited_egfr_pathogenic_variants Not reported / not observed (no systematic germline screen; only six matched normal tissues checked and were negative).
gene_environment_interactions No germline-by-environment interaction analyses performed. Observational interaction: sex and smoking are correlated (most males smokers); logistic regression indicates smoking (absence) independently associated with EGFR mutation, implying smoking modifies the apparent sex association.
testing_or_selection_bias Authors note key selection biases: cohort restricted to surgically resected tumours (early-stage bias) so frequencies may not represent all NSCLC; only exons 18-21 tested (mutations outside these exons not assessed); PCR-SSCP may miss mutations that do not alter band patterns (potential underestimation); single-center Japanese sample limits generalisability. No patients had prior gefitinib exposure. Authors declare no funding for the project.
egfr_tki_response_by_ethnicity No treatment-response data from this surgical cohort (no patients had received gefitinib prior to resection). The paper cites other studies linking EGFR mutations to gefitinib/erlotinib responsiveness but provides no ethnicity-stratified TKI-outcome analysis from its own data.
methods_key_details Tumours frozen at −80°C; DNA extracted with FastDNA Kit. EGFR exons 18-21 PCR primers and cycles described; screening by PCR-SSCP (GenePhor System) at specified gel temperatures, altered bands sequenced for confirmation. K-ras codon12 by mutagenic PCR-RFLP. Promoter methylation by methylation-specific PCR. Statistics: chi-square/Fisher's exact, Mann-Whitney U, logistic regression (StatView).
mechanistic_hypotheses_proposed Authors propose EGFR activating mutations are drivers of smoking-independent adenocarcinoma and may arise by mechanisms distinct from smoking-induced mutagenesis; they speculate a 'DNA-editing' agent/process could produce both the characteristic exon19 deletions and exon21 point mutations in the EGFR tyrosine-kinase domain, and that social/behavioral differences in smoking prevalence explain sex differences in mutation frequency.
counter_evidence_or_null_results EGFR mutations did not correlate with p53 mutation status or promoter hypermethylation of p16, RASSF1A, APC1A. Despite female predominance in raw data, adjusted analysis showed smoking history (absence) — not female sex — was independently associated with EGFR mutations. The exon19 deletion start-site distributions differ between this Japanese series and some US series (E746 vs L747), indicating heterogeneity across populations but not a functional sensitivity difference to TKIs.
limitations_and_conflicts Limitations: single-center surgical cohort (selection bias to resectable/early-stage disease), PCR-SSCP may miss some mutations, only exons 18-21 examined, small number of matched normal tissues tested (n=6). Authors report no funding and no conflicts disclosed.
