Theory (General Information)

ID: theory-25

Name: Pollution-promoted EGFR selection model

Type: general

Theory Query: Build a theory of why activating EGFR mutations in lung cancer are more common in East Asian populations, and how this relates to responsiveness to EGFR kinase inhibitors, based on the following results.

Description: Pre-existing oncogenic EGFR mutations are detectable in normal lung epithelia. In regions with higher ambient and indoor particulate exposure (notably parts of East Asia), chronic PM2.5-driven inflammation (via macrophage recruitment and IL-1β) promotes clonal expansion and malignant progression of EGFR-mutant alveolar type II cells, especially among never-smokers. This increases the proportion of EGFR-mutant lung adenocarcinoma observed in East Asian clinical series. Once EGFR driver mutations are present, therapeutic responsiveness to EGFR TKIs is driven by mutation status rather than ethnicity.

Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: PM2.5-IL-1β promotion preferentially expands pre-existing EGFR-mutant clones

Statement: Higher PM2.5 exposure causally promotes expansion and malignant outgrowth of pre-existing EGFR-mutant alveolar cells via macrophage recruitment and IL-1β signaling, raising the incidence fraction of EGFR-mutant lung adenocarcinoma in exposed populations.

Domain/Scope: Population and cellular scales: never-smokers and light-smokers in regions with elevated PM2.5; applies to lung adenocarcinoma initiation/promotion; not intended to explain tobacco mutagenesis spectra.

Special Cases

  1. At very low PM2.5 exposure, promotion is weak and enrichment of EGFR-mutant tumors diminishes.
  2. Heavy tobacco exposure can dominate initiation with distinct mutation spectra (e.g., KRAS, TP53), attenuating relative EGFR contribution.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Ambient PM2.5 correlates with increased incidence/progression of EGFR-mutant lung adenocarcinoma across cohorts; mouse models show macrophage/IL-1β-driven promotion, and ultra-deep sequencing detects oncogenic EGFR mutations in normal lung. [e408.1] [e408.0]
  2. East Asian cohorts (Korea, Taiwan, China) are represented in the PM2.5-EGFR analyses; East Asia bears high PM burden in multiple settings. [e408.1] [e408.0]
  3. EGFR-mutant disease is highly prevalent among East Asian never-smokers with adenocarcinoma, consistent with a promoter-dominant, non-tobacco pathway. [e419.0] [e409.2] [e409.1] [e393.0] [e382.0] [e389.0] [e380.0]

Statement 1: Mutation status, not ancestry, governs TKI benefit

Statement: Given an activating EGFR mutation, the magnitude and direction of benefit from EGFR-TKIs are similar across ancestries; the East Asian advantage in TKI-responsive cases arises primarily from higher mutation prevalence rather than differential pharmacodynamic efficacy.

Domain/Scope: First-line and later-line systemic therapy for advanced EGFR-mutant NSCLC across global populations.

Special Cases

  1. Uncommon and compound EGFR variants display heterogeneous TKI sensitivity and may deviate from classical responders.
  2. Acquired resistance mechanisms (T790M after first/second-generation TKIs; C797X, MET/HER2 amplification after osimertinib) modulate durability across all ancestries.

Supporting Evidence for this Law

  1. Narrative reviews emphasize mutation status—not ethnicity—predicts response; pooled figures show ~68% response in EGFR-positive versus ~11% in EGFR-negative patients. [e409.0] [e393.0]
  2. Asian and Western randomized trials both show marked PFS benefit of TKIs in EGFR-mutant tumors (e.g., IPASS/OPTIMAL in Asia; EURTAC in Europe). [e393.0]
  3. Within East Asian cohorts, EGFR-mutant cases have high response rates to TKIs independent of ethnicity comparison (e.g., Korean cohort RR 90% mutant vs 9% wild-type). [e370.0]
