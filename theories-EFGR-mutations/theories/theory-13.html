Theory (Statement/Laws)

Theory Statements/Laws

Statement 0: Quantitative Ancestry-Driven Admixture Law for EGFR

Statement: In lung adenocarcinoma, the prevalence of activating EGFR mutations exhibits a monotonic relationship to the proportion of East Asian and/or Native American ancestry in an individual's genome. This scaling can be modeled as linear or log-linear within certain admixture ranges, and persists even after controlling for environmental and lifestyle factors such as smoking, sex, and socioeconomic status. Self-reported race or ethnicity does not reliably predict EGFR mutation prevalence compared to quantitative ancestry.

Domain/Scope: Applies to admixed or ancestrally heterogeneous populations (Latin America, Brazil, US Hispanics, South Asia, Caribbean, North Africa, American Indigenous, and any admixed urban population), specifically for non-small cell lung adenocarcinoma (LUAD), and is robust to typical urban/rural migration and agnostic to location of residence (i.e., persists after migration).

Special Cases

  1. Local founder effects or unique historical events (e.g., isolated Peruvian or Kinh Vietnamese founder populations) may cause deviations from the admixture-predicted frequency.
  2. Recent, or unrecognized, admixture (Asian/Native American/other) may bias local estimates.
  3. At very high (>50%) or very low (<5%) ancestry proportions, the relationship may become nonlinear or plateau.

Supporting Evidence for this Law
