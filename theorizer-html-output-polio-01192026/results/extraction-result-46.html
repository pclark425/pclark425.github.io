Paper Abstract: Among the three wild poliovirus (WPV) serotypes, only WPV type 1 (WPV1) has been reported in polio cases or detected from environmental surveillance globally since 2012. Pakistan remains one of only three countries worldwide (the others are Afghanistan and Nigeria) that has never had interrupted WPV1 transmission. This report documents Pakistan's activities and progress toward polio eradication during January 2017-September 2018 and updates previous reports (1,2). In 2017, Pakistan reported eight WPV1 cases, a 60% decrease from 20 cases in 2016. As of September 18, 2018, four cases had been reported, compared with five cases at that time in 2017. Nonetheless, in 2018, WPV1 continues to be isolated regularly from environmental surveillance sites, primarily in the core reservoir areas of Karachi, Quetta, and Peshawar, signifying persistent transmission. Strategies to increase childhood immunity have included an intense schedule of supplemental immunization activities (SIAs), expanding and refining deployment of community-based vaccination implemented by community health workers recruited from the local community in reservoir areas, and strategic placement of permanent transit points where vaccination is provided to mobile populations. Interruption of WPV1 transmission will require further programmatic improvements throughout the country with a focus on specific underperforming subdistricts in reservoir areas.

key_findings During Jan 2017–Sep 2018, 12 WPV1 cases were reported (8 in 2017, 4 in Jan–Sep 2018); ages of the 12 cases were 4–38 months. Environmental surveillance continued to detect WPV1 regularly (e.g., among 39 long-running sites: 60 positive samples in 2016, 89 in 2017, 53 Jan–Aug 2018), indicating persistent transmission in reservoir areas (Karachi, Quetta block, Peshawar). No WPV1 cases were reported among older children, adolescents, or adults in this period in the report. SIA exposure among 2018 cases: parental recall indicated all 2018 cases had received OPV during SIAs (3 to >7 doses); one case had received three OPV doses and one IPV dose via routine services. In 2017 cases: one zero-dose, one SIA-only, three routine+SIA.
vaccination_status_by_age Routine OPV3 coverage estimate for infants by age 1 year = 75% (WHO/UNICEF estimate for 2017). Vaccination-history surrogate analyses used children 6–23 months with nonpolio AFP to estimate zero-dose prevalence: provinces with highest zero-dose percentages included Balochistan (2% in 2016), Gilgit-Baltistan (15% in 2017), and in 2018 several provinces each 1% (Balochistan, Azad Jammu and Kashmir, Islamabad, Gilgit-Baltistan). Case-level: 2018 WPV1 cases were recalled to have 3 to >7 SIA doses; none were zero-dose.

Thus the report provides no direct evidence for older-age transmission but cannot exclude it due to scope limitations.
