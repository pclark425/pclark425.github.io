Paper Abstract: After reporting a single wild poliovirus (WPV) type 1 (WPV1) case in 2021, Pakistan reported 14 cases during April 1-July 31, 2022. Pakistan and Afghanistan are the only countries where endemic WPV transmission has never been interrupted (1). In its current 5-year strategic plan, the Global Polio Eradication Initiative (GPEI) has set a goal of interrupting all WPV1 transmission by the end of 2023 (1-3). The reemergence of WPV cases in Pakistan after 14 months with no case detection has uncovered transmission in southern Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province, the most historically challenging area. This report describes Pakistan's progress toward polio eradication during January 2021-July 2022 and updates previous reports (4,5). As of August 20, 2022, all but one of the 14 WPV1 cases in Pakistan during 2022 have been reported from North Waziristan district in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. In underimmunized populations, excretion of vaccine virus can, during a period of 12-18 months, lead to reversion to neurovirulence, resulting in circulating vaccine-derived polioviruses (cVDPVs), which can cause paralysis and outbreaks. An outbreak of cVDPV type 2 (cVDPV2), which began in Pakistan in 2019, has been successfully contained; the last case occurred in April 2021 (1,6). Despite program improvements, 400,000-500,000 children continue to be missed during nationwide polio supplementary immunization activities (SIAs),* and recent isolation of poliovirus from sewage samples collected in other provinces suggests wider WPV1 circulation during the ongoing high transmission season. Although vaccination efforts have been recently complicated by months of flooding during the summer of 2022, to successfully interrupt WPV1 transmission in the core reservoirs in southern Khyber Pakhtunkhwa and reach the GPEI goal, emphasis should be placed on further improving microplanning and supervision of SIAs and on systematic tracking and vaccination of persistently missed children in these reservoir areas of Pakistan.

role_of_older_age_groups The report contains no direct evidence implicating older children, adolescents, or adults as contributors to poliovirus transmission in Pakistan; all reported WPV1 paralytic cases during the period were in infants/toddlers (7–28 months). Surveillance definitions include persons aged <15 years for AFP sensitivity, but no cases or virologic findings are attributed specifically to older age groups; environmental surveillance detects virus circulation but is not age-specific.
key_findings During Jan 2021–Jul 2022 Pakistan reported 15 WPV1 cases (1 in 2021, 14 in 2022 as of Aug 20, 2022) clustered in southern Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (13 in North Waziristan, 1 in Lakki Marwat). Case ages ranged 7–28 months (median 15 months). 87% of WPV1 case-patients had received zero OPV doses via essential immunization; 13% had 1–3 OPV doses. Environmental surveillance found WPV1-positive sewage samples in multiple provinces (e.g., Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Punjab, Islamabad) genetically linked to Khyber Pakhtunkhwa viruses; earliest Bannu isolates were orphan viruses indicating surveillance gaps. No quantitative data (seroprevalence, shedding, secondary attack rates) are provided for older children, adolescents, or adults.
vaccination_status_by_age Reported routine immunization estimate for 2021 (children aged 12–23 months): national estimate 3-dose OPV + 1 IPV = 83% (WHO/UNICEF). Provincial 3-dose OPV in 12–23 months ranged from 45.1% (Balochistan) to 94.9% (Punjab); no districts in Balochistan, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, and Sindh achieved ≥80% coverage; 31 of 36 districts in Punjab achieved ≥80%. SIAs were targeted to children <5 years; during combined Nov 2021 campaign measles-rubella vaccine reached persons aged 9 months–15 years but bOPV was given to children aged <5 years only.
interventions_targeting_older_ages No polio vaccination interventions specifically targeted older children, adolescents, or adults; national SIAs and SNIDs targeted children aged <5 years. A measles-rubella campaign (Nov 2021) reached ages 9 months–15 years with measles-rubella vaccine, but bOPV doses administered during that campaign were limited to children <5 years. Environmental surveillance (sewage sampling) is age-independent and was expanded but does not target older age groups specifically.
counter_evidence_or_limitations The report does not present direct data assessing poliovirus infection or shedding in older children, adolescents, or adults. AFP surveillance is focused on persons aged <15 years (so adolescents are included in surveillance denominators), but AFP detects paralytic cases and will miss asymptomatic or nonparalytic infections, especially in older age groups. Environmental surveillance detects circulation irrespective of age but cannot attribute transmission to specific age groups. The authors note surveillance gaps (e.g., orphan viruses) which could mask undetected transmission in any age group. Thus absence of reported older-age paralytic cases is not definitive evidence that older age groups do not participate in transmission; the report lacks seroprevalence or shedding studies in older populations.
geographic_scope National Pakistan with detailed focus on provinces and districts: Khyber Pakhtunkhwa (notably North Waziristan and Bannu), Punjab, Islamabad, Balochistan, Sindh, and other provinces as reported; SIAs described nationally and in 25 very high-risk districts.
